श्री शुक उवाच

क्वचिद् वनाशाय मनो दधद् व्रजात् प्रातः समुत्थाय वयस्यवत्सपान् ।

प्रबोधयञ्छृङ्गरवेण चारुणा विनिर्गतो वत्सपुरःसरो हरिः ॥10.12.1॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुक उवाच Shri Shuka said, क्वचित् once/one day, वन-आशाय for grazing in the forest, मनः mind, दधत् setting/fixing, व्रजात् from Vraja, प्रातः in the morning, समुत्थाय having risen, वयस्य-वत्सपान् the cowherd boys who were His friends, प्रबोधयन् awakening, शृङ्ग-रवेण with the sound of His horn, चारुणा beautiful/sweet, विनिर्गतः went out, वत्स-पुरःसरः with the calves going ahead, हरिः Hari/Krishna

आश   2. आश m. (√ 2. अश्), food src, Kosha Ashtadhyayi

Anvaya: श्री शुक उवाच — क्वचित् वनाशाय मनः दधत् हरिः व्रजात् प्रातः समुत्थाय चारुणा शृङ्गरवेण वयस्यवत्सपान् प्रबोधयन् वत्सपुरःसरः विनिर्गतः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री शुक उवाच Shri Shuka said — क्वचित् one day, वनाशाय for grazing in the forest, मनः दधत् setting His mind, हरिः Hari Krishna, व्रजात् from Vraja, प्रातः in the morning, समुत्थाय having risen early, चारुणा शृङ्गरवेण with the sweet and beautiful sound of His horn, वयस्यवत्सपान् His cowherd boy companions, प्रबोधयन् awakening, वत्सपुरःसरः with the calves going ahead, विनिर्गतः went out.

Simple meaning: Shri Shuka said — One day, setting His mind on grazing in the forest, Hari Krishna rose early in the morning, awakened His cowherd friends with the sweet and beautiful sound of His horn, and went out from Vraja with the calves going ahead of Him.

तेनैव साकं पृथुकाः सहस्रशः स्निग्धाः सुशिग्वेत्रविषाणवेणवः ।

स्वान् स्वान् सहस्रोपरिसंख्ययान्वितान् वत्सान् पुरस्कृत्य विनिर्ययुर्मुदा ॥10.12.2॥

Word-by-word meaning: तेन एव साकम् along with Him, पृथुकाः the young boys, सहस्रशः by the thousands, स्निग्धाः affectionate/loving, सु-शिक्-वेत्र-विषाण-वेणवः equipped with lunch bags, staffs, horns and flutes, स्वान् स्वान् their own respective, सहस्र-उपरि-संख्यया-अन्वितान् numbering more than a thousand each, वत्सान् calves, पुरस्कृत्य placing in front, विनिर्ययुः went out, मुदा with joy

Anvaya: तेन एव साकं सहस्रशः स्निग्धाः सुशिग्वेत्रविषाणवेणवः पृथुकाः स्वान् स्वान् सहस्रोपरिसंख्ययान्वितान् वत्सान् पुरस्कृत्य मुदा विनिर्ययुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तेन एव साकं Along with Him, सहस्रशः by the thousands, स्निग्धाः affectionate and loving, सुशिग्वेत्रविषाणवेणवः equipped with lunch bags, staffs, horns and flutes, पृथुकाः the young boys, स्वान् स्वान् सहस्रोपरिसंख्ययान्वितान् वत्सान् their own respective calves numbering more than a thousand each, पुरस्कृत्य placing in front, मुदा with joy, विनिर्ययुः went out.

Simple meaning: Along with Him, thousands of affectionate young boys equipped with lunch bags, staffs, horns and flutes, joyfully went out placing their own respective herds of calves — each numbering more than a thousand — in front of them.

कृष्णवत्सैरसंख्यातैर्यूथीकृत्य स्ववत्सकान् ।

चारयन्तोऽर्भलीलाभिर्विजह्रुस्तत्र तत्र ह ॥10.12.3॥

Word-by-word meaning: कृष्ण-वत्सैः by Krishna and the calves, असंख्यातैः by countless (boys), यूथी-कृत्य having grouped into herds, स्व-वत्सकान् their own calves, चारयन्तः grazing/herding, अर्भ-लीलाभिः with childish pastimes, विजह्रुः they sported/roamed, तत्र तत्र here and there, ह indeed

Anvaya: असंख्यातैः कृष्णवत्सैः स्ववत्सकान् यूथीकृत्य, चारयन्तः, अर्भलीलाभिः तत्र तत्र ह विजह्रुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: असंख्यातैः With countless, कृष्णवत्सैः along with Krishna and the calves, स्ववत्सकान् their own calves, यूथीकृत्य having formed into groups/herds, चारयन्तः while grazing, अर्भलीलाभिः with childish pastimes, तत्र तत्र here and there, ह indeed, विजह्रुः they sported.

Simple meaning: Together with Krishna and the countless calves, the boys, after grouping their calves into herds and tending them, roamed here and there, delighting in their childish pastimes.

फलप्रवालस्तबकसुमनःपिच्छधातुभिः ।

काचगुञ्जामणिस्वर्णभूषिता अप्यभूषयन् ॥10.12.4॥

Word-by-word meaning: फल-प्रवाल-स्तबक-सुमनः-पिच्छ-धातुभिः with fruits, tender shoots, clusters/bunches of  flowers, feathers (especially peacock feathers), minerals/colors, काच-गुञ्जा-मणि-स्वर्ण-भूषिताः adorned with glass, berries, beads and gold, अपि although, अभूषयन् they decorated/adorned (others)

Anvaya: फलप्रवालस्तबकसुमनःपिच्छधातुभिः (च) काचगुञ्जामणिस्वर्णभूषिताः अपि (ते) अभूषयन्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: फलप्रवालस्तबकसुमनःपिच्छधातुभिः With fruits, tender shoots, clusters, flowers, feathers, and minerals, काचगुञ्जामणिस्वर्णभूषिताः being adorned with glass, gunja berries, jewels, and gold, अपि although, (ते) they, अभूषयन् decorated (others).

Simple meaning: Although they were already adorned with ornaments of glass, gunja berries, jewels, and gold, they further decorated one another with fruits, tender shoots, clusters, flowers, peacock feathers, and natural colors.

मुष्णन्तोऽन्योन्यशिक्यादीन् ज्ञातानाराच्च चिक्षिपुः ।

तत्रत्याश्च पुनर्दूराद्धसन्तश्च पुनर्ददुः ॥10.12.5॥

Word-by-word meaning: मुष्णन्तः stealing, अन्योन्य-शिक्य-आदीन् one another’s lunch bags and other items, ज्ञातान् to those who know/notice, आरात् from nearby, च and, चिक्षिपुः they threw, तत्रत्याः those standing there, च and, पुनः again, दूरात् from a distance, हसन्तः laughing, च and, पुनः again, ददुः they gave back

शिक्य - A burden or load carried in a sling. src. Kosha, Ashtadhyayi

Anvaya: अन्योन्यशिक्यादीन् मुष्णन्तः, ज्ञातान् आरात् चिक्षिपुः; तत्रत्याः च पुनः दूरात् हसन्तः पुनः ददुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अन्योन्यशिक्यादीन् One another’s lunch bags and other items, मुष्णन्तः stealing, ज्ञातान् to those who notice, आरात् from nearby, चिक्षिपुः they threw; तत्रत्याः those standing there, च and, पुनः again, दूरात् from a distance, हसन्तः laughing, पुनः again, ददुः they gave back.

Simple meaning: They would steal one another’s lunch bags and other belongings, and when the owners noticed, they would toss them nearby. Others would then pick them up, and from a distance, laughing, return them again.

यदि दूरं गतः कृष्णोवनशोभेक्षणाय तम् ।

अहं पूर्वमहं पूर्वमिति संस्पृश्य रेमिरे ॥10.12.6॥

Word-by-word meaning: यदि when, दूरम् far, गतः went, कृष्णः Krishna, वन-शोभा-ईक्षणाय to see the beauty of the forest, तम् Him, अहम् I, पूर्वम् first, अहम् I, पूर्वम् first, इति thus, संस्पृश्य touching, रेमिरे they enjoyed/sported

Anvaya: यदि कृष्णः वनशोभेक्षणाय दूरं गतः, (तदा ते) “अहं पूर्वम् अहं पूर्वम्” इति तं संस्पृश्य रेमिरे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यदि When, कृष्णः Krishna, वनशोभेक्षणाय to see the beauty of the forest, दूरं गतः went far, (तदा ते) then they, “अहं पूर्वम् अहं पूर्वम्” saying “I first, I first,” तं Him, संस्पृश्य touching, रेमिरे they enjoyed.

Simple meaning: Whenever Krishna went a little far away to admire the beauty of the forest, the boys would run to Him, saying “I will touch Him first! I will touch Him first!” and joyfully touch Him.

केचिद् वेणून् वादयन्तो ध्मान्तः शृङ्गाणि केचन् ।

केचिद् भृङ्गैः प्रगायन्तः कूजन्तः कोकिलैः परे ॥10.12.7॥

Word-by-word meaning: केचित् some, वेणून् flutes, वादयन्तः playing, ध्मान्तः blowing, शृङ्गाणि horns, केचन others, केचित् some, भृङ्गैः with bees, प्रगायन्तः singing along, कूजन्तः cooing, कोकिलैः with cuckoos, परे others

Anvaya: केचित् वेणून् वादयन्तः, केचन शृङ्गाणि ध्मान्तः, केचित् भृङ्गैः प्रगायन्तः, परे कोकिलैः कूजन्तः (सन्ति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: केचित् वेणून् वादयन्तः Some playing flutes; केचन शृङ्गाणि ध्मान्तः others blowing horns; केचित् भृङ्गैः प्रगायन्तः some singing along with bees, परे कोकिलैः कूजन्तः others cooing with cuckoos.

Simple meaning: Some boys played on flutes, others blew horns; some sang along with the buzzing bees, and others imitated the cooing of cuckoos.

विच्छायाभिः प्रधावन्तो गच्छन्तः साधु हंसकैः ।

बकैरुपविशन्तश्च नृत्यन्तश्च कलापिभिः॥10.12.8॥

Word-by-word meaning: विच्छायाभिः with the shadows of flock of birds, प्रधावन्तः running, गच्छन्तः walking, साधु well/properly, हंसकैः with swans, बकैः with cranes, उपविशन्तः sitting, च and, नृत्यन्तः dancing, च and, कलापिभिः with peacocks

विच्छाय -  The shadow of a flock of birds. Src. Kosha Ashtadhyayi

Anvaya: विच्छायाभिः प्रधावन्तः, हंसकैः साधु गच्छन्तः, बकैः उपविशन्तः च, कलापिभिः नृत्यन्तः च (सन्ति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: विच्छायाभिः प्रधावन्तः Running with the shadows of the birds; हंसकैः साधु गच्छन्तः walking gracefully with swans; बकैः उपविशन्तः sitting with cranes; च कलापिभिः नृत्यन्तः and dancing with peacocks.

Simple meaning: They ran along with the shadows of the birds, walked gracefully like swans, sat down like cranes, and danced along with the peacocks.

विकर्षन्तः कीशबालानारोहन्तश्च तैर्द्रुमान् ।

विकुर्वन्तश्च तैः साकं प्लवन्तश्च पलाशिषु ॥10.12.9॥

Word-by-word meaning: विकर्षन्तः pulling/tugging, कीश-बालान् baby monkeys by their tails, आरोहन्तः climbing, च and, तैः with them, द्रुमान् trees, विकुर्वन्तः imitating/mimicking their movements, च and, तैः साकम् together with them, प्लवन्तः leaping/jumping, च and, पलाशिषु on the branches/leaves

Anvaya: कीशबालान् विकर्षन्तः, तैः च द्रुमान् आरोहन्तः, तैः साकं विकुर्वन्तः च, पलाशिषु प्लवन्तः च (सन्ति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: कीशबालान् विकर्षन्तः Pulling baby monkeys’ tails; तैः द्रुमान् आरोहन्तः climbing trees with them; तैः साकं विकुर्वन्तः imitating together with them; च and, पलाशिषु प्लवन्तः leaping on the branches.

Simple meaning: They would pull the baby monkeys by their tails, climb trees along with them, imitate their actions, and leap about on the branches.

साकं भेकैर्विलङ्घन्तः सरित्प्रस्रवसम्प्लुताः ।

विहसन्तः प्रतिच्छायाः शपन्तश्च प्रतिस्वनान् ॥10.12.10॥

Word-by-word meaning: साकम् together with, भेकैः with frogs, विलङ्घन्तः leaping/jumping, सरित्-प्रस्रव-सम्प्लुताः jumping into rivers and streams/splashed in flowing waters, विहसन्तः laughing, प्रतिच्छायाः at their reflections, शपन्तः scolding/rebuking, च and, प्रतिस्वनान् the echoes

Anvaya: भेकैः साकं विलङ्घन्तः, सरित्प्रस्रवसम्प्लुताः, प्रतिच्छायाः विहसन्तः च, प्रतिस्वनान् शपन्तः च (सन्ति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: भेकैः साकं विलङ्घन्तः Jumping together with the frogs; सरित्प्रस्रवसम्प्लुताः splashing in rivers and streams; प्रतिच्छायाः विहसन्तः laughing at their reflections; च प्रतिस्वनान् शपन्तः and scolding the echoes.

Simple meaning: They jumped along with the frogs, splashed in rivers and streams, laughed at their own reflections, and even playfully scolded the echoes.

इत्थं सतां ब्रह्मसुखानुभूत्या दास्यं गतानां परदैवतेन ।

मायाश्रितानां नरदारकेण साकं विजह्रुः कृतपुण्यपुञ्जाः ॥10.12.11॥

Word-by-word meaning: इत्थम् thus/in this manner, सताम् of the realized souls, ब्रह्म-सुख-अनुभूत्या by whom the bliss of Brahman is experienced, दास्यम् the state of servitude, गतानाम् of those who have attained, पर-दैवतेन with the Supreme Lord, माया-आश्रितानाम् of those under the influence of maya, नर-दारकेण as a human child, साकम् together with, विजह्रुः they sported, कृत-पुण्य-पुञ्जाः those who have accumulated heaps of merit

Anvaya: इत्थं सतां ब्रह्मसुखानुभूत्या, दास्यं गतानां परदैवतेन, मायाश्रितानां नरदारकेण साकं कृतपुण्यपुञ्जाः (ते) विजह्रुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: इत्थं Thus, सतां ब्रह्मसुखानुभूत्या the realized souls by whom the bliss of Brahman is experienced, दास्यं गतानां परदैवतेन with the Supreme Lord of those who have attained servitude, मायाश्रितानां नरदारकेण in the form of a human child for those under maya, साकं together, कृतपुण्यपुञ्जाः (ते) those boys who had amassed heaps of merit, विजह्रुः sported.

Simple meaning: Thus, those boys—who had accumulated heaps of pious merit—played with Him, who is experienced by realized souls as the bliss of Brahman, who is worshiped as the Supreme Lord by devotees in servitude, and who appeared as an ordinary human child to those under the influence of maya.

यत्पादपांसुर्बहुजन्मकृच्छ्रतो धृतात्मभिर्योगिभिरप्यलभ्यः ।

स एव यद्दृग्विषयः स्वयं स्थितः किं वर्ण्यते दिष्टमतो व्रजौकसाम् ॥10.12.12॥

Word-by-word meaning: यत् whose, पाद-पांसुः the dust of the feet, बहु-जन्म-कृच्छ्रतः after great austerities over many births, धृत-आत्मभिः by self-controlled (yogis), योगिभिः by yogis, अपि even, अलभ्यः not obtainable, सः he, एव indeed, यत् whose, दृक्-विषयः the object of direct vision, स्वयम् personally, स्थितः present, किम् what, वर्ण्यते can be described, दिष्टम् fortune/blessing, अतः therefore, व्रज-ओकसाम् of the residents of Vraja

Anvaya: यत् पादपांसुः बहुजन्मकृच्छ्रतः धृतात्मभिः योगिभिः अपि अलभ्यः, सः एव यत् दृग्विषयः स्वयं स्थितः; अतः व्रजौकसां दिष्टं किं वर्ण्यते।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यत् पादपांसुः Whose dust of the feet, बहुजन्मकृच्छ्रतः after severe austerities over many births, धृतात्मभिः योगिभिः अपि अलभ्यः is not attainable even by self-controlled yogis, सः एव यत् दृग्विषयः स्वयं स्थितः that very Lord, as the object of direct sight, is personally present; अतः therefore, व्रजौकसां दिष्टं किं वर्ण्यते what can be described as the good fortune of the residents of Vraja?

Simple meaning: That very Lord, whose foot-dust cannot be attained even by self-controlled yogis after many births of severe austerities, is personally present before the eyes of the residents of Vraja. What words can describe their great fortune?

Simple meaning: "What is to be done here? Neither should this wicked one live nor should the death of these good companions happen? How can both be accomplished?" — having carefully considered this and having understood the solution, the all-seeing Hari entered the asura’s mouth.

O the supreme of devotees (Sage Shaunaka)!, तु Indeed, सः बादरायणिः इत्थं पृष्टः स्म Having asked thus, that Badarayani, तत्स्मारितानन्तहृताखिलेन्द्रियः (सन्) becoming reminded of that Eternal Lord, lost all his senses, कृच्छ्रात् पुनः then with great difficulty, बहिर्दृशिः (सन्) his awareness returned to this world, तं शनैः प्रत्याह and spoke to Parikshit slowly.

Simple meaning: Suta said: When asked thus, Badrayani, reminded of the Eternal Lord, withdrew all his senses and meditated upon Krishna. Then with difficulty, he brought his awareness back and spoke slowly to Parikshit.

श्रीमद्भागवते महापुराणे पारमहंस्यां संहितायां दशमस्कन्धे पूर्वार्धे

अघासुरमोक्षः नाम द्वादशः अध्यायः ॥12॥

Thus ends the twelfth discourse named ‘The Deliverance of Aghasura’ in the first half of Book Ten of the great and glorious Bhagavata-Purana.
