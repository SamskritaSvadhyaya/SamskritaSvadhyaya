A description of the Rains and Autumn

श्रीमद्भागवतमहापुराणम्

दशमः स्कन्धः

श्री शुक उवाच

तयोस्तदद्भुतं कर्म दावाग्नेर्मोक्षमात्मनः ।

गोपाः स्त्रीभ्यः समाचख्युः प्रलम्बवधमेव च ॥10.20.1॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, तयोः of those two (Krishna and Balarama), तत्-अद्भुतम् that wonderful, कर्म activity/deed, दावाग्नेः from the forest fire, मोक्षम् the deliverance, आत्मनः of themselves, गोपाः the cowherd boys, स्त्रीभ्यः to the women (the gopis and mothers), समाचख्युः they fully described, प्रलम्ब-वधम् the killing of Pralamba, एव च and also

Anvaya: श्रीशुकः उवाच — गोपाः आत्मनः दावाग्नेः मोक्षं प्रलम्बवधम् एव च तयोः तत् अद्भुतं कर्म स्त्रीभ्यः समाचख्युः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, गोपाः the cowherd boys, आत्मनः दावाग्नेः मोक्षं their own deliverance from the forest fire, प्रलम्बवधम् एव च and also the killing of Pralamba, तयोः तत् अद्भुतं कर्म (and) that wonderful deed of those two, स्त्रीभ्यः समाचख्युः described to the women.

Simple meaning: Shri Shuka said: Upon returning to the village, the cowherd boys gave a full account to the women of Vraja about how Balarama had killed the asura Pralamba and how Krishna had miraculously delivered them all from the fierce forest fire.

गोपवृद्धाश्च गोप्यश्च तदुपाकर्ण्य विस्मिताः ।

मेनिरे देवप्रवरौ कृष्णरामौ व्रजं गतौ ॥10.20.2॥

Word-by-word meaning: गोप-वृद्धाः the elderly cowherd men, च and, गोप्यः the cowherd women (gopis), च and, तत्-उपाकर्ण्य hearing that, विस्मिताः astonished, मेनिरे they considered, देव-प्रवरौ the two best of demigods, कृष्ण-रामौ Krishna and Balarama, व्रजम् to Vraja, गतौ having come

Anvaya: गोपवृद्धाः गोप्यः च तत् उपाकर्ण्य विस्मिताः (सन्तः), व्रजं गतौ कृष्णरामौ देवप्रवरौ मेनिरे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: गोपवृद्धाः गोप्यः च The elderly cowherd men and the cowherd women, तत् उपाकर्ण्य hearing that, विस्मिताः being astonished, व्रजं गतौ कृष्णरामौ Krishna and Balarama, who had come to Vraja, देवप्रवरौ मेनिरे considered them to be the greatest of demigods.

Simple meaning: Hearing the amazing accounts from the boys, both the elderly men and the women of Vraja were struck with wonder. They began to believe that Krishna and Balarama were actually the most exalted of demigods who had descended to their village.

ततः प्रावर्तत प्रावृट् सर्वसत्त्वसमुद्भवा ।

विद्योतमानपरिधिर्विस्फूर्जितनभस्तला ॥10.20.3॥

Word-by-word meaning: ततः then, प्रावर्तत began/set in, प्रावृट् the rainy season, सर्व-सत्त्व-समुद्भवा which causes the birth/flourishing of all living beings, विद्योतमान-परिधिः whose horizon was illuminated (by lightning), विस्फूर्जित-नभस्तला whose sky resounded (with thunder).

Anvaya: ततः सर्वसत्त्वसमुद्भवा विद्योतमानपरिधिः विस्फूर्जितनभस्तला प्रावृट् प्रावर्तत।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: ततः Then, सर्वसत्त्वसमुद्भवा (the season) which brings about the flourishing of all living beings, विद्योतमानपरिधिः whose quarters are illuminated by lightning, विस्फूर्जितनभस्तला whose sky is filled with the sound of thunder, प्रावृट् प्रावर्तत the rainy season began.

Simple meaning: Then the rainy season began—the time of year that brings life and growth to all living creatures. The horizon flashed with constant lightning, and the entire sky resounded with the deep vibration of thunder.

सान्द्रलीलाम्बुदैर्व्योम सविद्युत्स्तनयित्नुभिः ।

अस्पष्टज्योतिराच्छन्नं ब्रह्मेव सगुणं बभौ ॥10.20.4॥

Word-by-word meaning: सान्द्र-नील-अम्बुदैः by dense, dark-blue clouds, व्योम the sky, स-विद्युत्-स्तनयित्नुभिः accompanied by lightning and thunder, अस्पष्ट-ज्योतिः with its lights (the sun, moon, and stars) indistinct, आच्छन्नम् covered, ब्रह्म इव like the Brahman (spirit), स-गुणम् possessing qualities (manifest), बभौ it appeared

Anvaya: सविद्युत्स्तनयित्नुभिः सान्द्रनीलाम्बुदैः आच्छन्नम् अस्पष्टज्योतिः व्योम सगुणं ब्रह्म इव बभौ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सविद्युत्स्तनयित्नुभिः सान्द्रनीलाम्बुदैः By dense dark clouds accompanied by lightning and thunder, आच्छन्नम् covered, अस्पष्टज्योतिः its lights being obscured, व्योम the sky, सगुणं ब्रह्म इव बभौ appeared like the Brahman manifested with qualities.

Simple meaning: The sky was completely covered by thick, dark-blue clouds that rumbled with thunder and flashed with lightning. With the sun and stars obscured from view, the overcast sky appeared like the Brahman (spirit) with the three material qualities (Jivatma with its light of wisdom obscured by the Sattva, Rajas and Tamas corresponding in the case of the sky to the flashes of lightning, thunder and the clouds).

अष्टौ मासान् निपीतं यद् भूम्याश्चोदमयं वसु ।

स्वगोभिर्मोक्तुमारेभे पर्जन्यः काल आगते ॥10.20.5॥

Word-by-word meaning: अष्टौ eight, मासान् months, निपीतम् drunk up, यत् which, भूम्याः from the earth, उद-मयम् consisting of water, वसु wealth/substance, स्व-गोभिः by it’s waters, मोक्तुम् to release, आरेभे began, पर्जन्यः the rain-god/the cloud, काले in due time, आगते having arrived.

गो - बाणः, इन्द्रस्य वज्रायुधम्, जलम्, किरणः, नेत्रम्, पशुः, स्वर्गः, वचनम्, दिक्, भूमिः - जलम् in this context src. Shabdapath, Ashtadhyayi

Anvaya: यत् भूम्याः उदमयं वसु अष्टौ मासान् निपीतं, (तत्) काल आगते पर्जन्यः स्वगोभिः मोक्तुम् आरेभे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यत् भूम्याः उदमयं वसु That watery wealth of the earth, अष्टौ मासान् निपीतं which was drunk for eight months, (तत्) काल आगते that, when the time arrived, पर्जन्यः the rain-cloud, स्वगोभिः by his own waters, मोक्तुम् आरेभे began to release.

Simple meaning: For eight months, the clouds had drunk up the earth's watery wealth. Now that the proper season had arrived, the clouds began to release that accumulated moisture back to the earth.

तडित्वन्तो महामेघाश्चण्डश्वसनवेपिताः ।

प्रीणनं जीवनं ह्यस्य मुमुचुः करुणा इव ॥10.20.6॥

Word-by-word meaning: तडित्वन्तः possessing lightning, महा-मेघाः the great clouds, चण्ड-श्वसन-वेपिताः shaken by fierce winds, प्रीणनम् pleasing/refreshing, जीवनम् the life-giving (water), हि indeed, अस्य of this (the world/earth), मुमुचुः they released, करुणाः compassionate (persons), इव like

Anvaya: चण्डश्वसनवेपिताः तडित्वन्तः महामेघाः करुणाः इव अस्य (जगतः) प्रीणनं जीवनं हि मुमुचुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: चण्डश्वसनवेपिताः Shaken by fierce winds, तडित्वन्तः possessing lightning, महामेघाः the great clouds, करुणाः इव like compassionate persons, अस्य (जगतः) of this world, प्रीणनं जीवनं refreshing and life-giving (water), हि मुमुचुः indeed released.

Simple meaning: Tossed and shaken by fierce winds, the great lightning-charged clouds released their refreshing, life-giving water upon the earth just as compassionate souls who, being moved by the "winds" of others' distress, give away their very life-substance to bring relief to the world.

तपःकृशा देवमीढा आसीद् वर्षीयसी मही ।

यथैव काम्यतपसस्तनुः सम्प्राप्य तत्फलम् ॥10.20.7॥

Word-by-word meaning: तपः-कृशा emaciated by austerities (heat), देव-मीढा moistened by the rain-god, आसीत् became, वर्षीयसी very lush/full/flourishing, मही the earth, यथा just as, एव certainly, काम्य-तपसः of one performing austerities for a desired result, तनुः the body, सम्प्राप्य having attained, तत्-फलम् the fruit of that (austerity)

Anvaya: तपःकृशा मही देवमीढा (सती) – यथा एव काम्यतपसः तनुः तत्फलं सम्प्राप्य – वर्षीयासी आसीत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तपःकृशा मही The earth, emaciated by the heat of summer, देवमीढा (सती) having been moistened by the rain-god, यथा एव just as, काम्यतपसः तनुः the body of one who has performed austerities, तत्फलं सम्प्राप्य having attained the desired fruit, वर्षीयसी आसीत् became full and lush.

Simple meaning: The earth, which had been dried and emaciated by the intense summer heat, became lush and vibrant once it was moistened by the rain, just as the body of a person who, after undergoing austerities to achieve a goal, finally becomes healthy and full of life upon receiving the desired result.

Key Comparisons

निशामुखेषु खद्योतास्तमसा भान्ति न ग्रहाः ।

यथा पापेन पाखण्डा न हि वेदाः कलौ युगे ॥10.20.8॥

Word-by-word meaning: निशा-मुखेषु at the beginning of the nights, खद्योताः fireflies, तमसा by the darkness, भान्ति shine, न not, ग्रहाः the planets/stars, यथा just as, पापेन by sin/wickedness, पाखण्डाः heretics/false doctrines, न not, हि indeed, वेदाः the Vedas, कलौ युगे in the age of Kali

Anvaya: निशामुखेषु तमसा खद्योताः भान्ति, न (तु) ग्रहाः, यथा कलौ युगे पापेन पाखण्डाः (भान्ति), न हि वेदाः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: निशामुखेषु In the twilight of the nights, तमसा by the darkness, खद्योताः भान्ति fireflies shine, न (तु) ग्रहाः but not the stars, यथा just as, कलौ युगे in the Kali-yuga, पापेन by sin, पाखण्डाः (भान्ति) heretics shine, न हि वेदाः and indeed not the Vedas.

Simple meaning: In the darkness of the rainy season nights, fireflies are visible while the stars and planets are hidden, just as in the Age of Kali, the darkness of sin allows false, heretical doctrines to flourish and gain prominence, while the true light of the Vedas remains obscured.

श्रुत्वा पर्जन्यनिनदं मण्डूका व्यसृजन् गिरः।

तूष्णीं शयानाः प्राग् यद्वद् ब्राह्मणा नियमात्यये ॥10.20.9॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्रुत्वा hearing, पर्जन्य-निनदम् the sound of the rain-cloud (thunder), मण्डूकाः the frogs, व्यसृजन् emitted/uttered, गिरः sounds/voices, तूष्णीम् silently, शयानाः lying down/resting, प्राक् before, यत्-वत् just as, ब्राह्मणाः brahmanas, नियम-अत्यये at the end of their vow (or at the start of their routine).

Anvaya: प्राक् तूष्णीं शयानाः मण्डूकाः पर्जन्यनिनदं श्रुत्वा गिरः व्यसृजन्, यद्वत् नियमात्यये ब्राह्मणाः (गिरः व्यसृजन्)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: प्राक् तूष्णीं शयानाः मण्डूकाः The frogs who were previously lying silent, पर्जन्यनिनदं श्रुत्वा hearing the thunder of the rain-cloud, गिरः व्यसृजन् uttered their voices, यद्वत् just as, नियमात्यये at the end of a silent vow (or at the break of dawn), ब्राह्मणाः (गिरः व्यसृजन्) brahmanas recite their chants.

Simple meaning: Having remained silent throughout the summer, the frogs began to croak loudly upon hearing the rumbling of the clouds just as Vedic students or brahmanas who, after finishing a silent vow or at the proper hour of study, begin their loud and rhythmic recitation of the scriptures.

Analysis of the Metaphor: The poet compares the natural sound of the frogs in the rainy season to the ritualistic chanting of the Vedas. In many traditional commentaries, नियमात्यये is interpreted as the end of the night (dawn), when brahmanas break their silence to begin the morning prayers. The "trigger" for the frogs is the thunder, just as the "trigger" for the students is the command of the teacher or the specific time of the day.

आसन्नुत्पथवाहिन्यः क्षुद्रनद्योऽनुशुष्यतीः ।

पुंसो यथास्वतन्त्रस्य देहद्रविणसम्पदः ॥10.20.10॥

Word-by-word meaning: आसन् became, उत्पथ-वाहिन्यः flowing out of their paths (overflowing the banks), क्षुद्र-नद्यः the small rivers/streams, अनुशुष्यतीः which had been dried up, पुंसः of a person, यथा just as, अ-स्वतन्त्रस्य who is not self-controlled (slave to senses), देह-द्रविण-सम्पदः the riches of the body and wealth

Anvaya: अनुशुष्यतीः क्षुद्रनद्यः उत्पथवाहिन्यः आसन्, यथा अस्वतन्त्रस्य पुंसः देहद्रविणसम्पदः (भवन्ति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अनुशुष्यतीः क्षुद्रनद्यः The small streams that had dried up, उत्पथवाहिन्यः आसन् began to overflow their paths, यथा just as, अस्वतन्त्रस्य पुंसः of a person who is not self-controlled, देहद्रविणसम्पदः (भवन्ति) the body and his wealth (are used improperly).

Simple meaning: The tiny streams that had completely dried up during the summer suddenly overflowed their banks with the rains just as a man who lacks self-control, as soon as he acquires a little physical strength or a bit of wealth, he ignores proper boundaries and "overflows" into reckless or sinful behavior.

हरिता हरिभिः शष्पैरिन्द्रगोपैश्च लोहिताः ।

उच्छिलीन्ध्रकृतच्छाया नृणां श्रीरिव भूरभूत् ॥10.20.11॥

Word-by-word meaning: हरिता green, हरिभिः with green, शष्पैः by the young grass, इन्द्र-गोपैः with Indragopa insects (velvet mites/ladybugs), च and, लोहिताः red, उच्छिलीन्ध्र-कृत-छाया with the shade/beauty created by burgeoning mushrooms, नृणाम् of men, श्रीः prosperity/wealth, इव like, भूः the earth, अभूत became

Anvaya: हरिभिः शष्पैः हरिता, इन्द्रगोपैः लोहिता, उच्छिलीन्ध्रकृतच्छाया च भूः, नृणां श्रीः इव अभूत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: हरिभिः शष्पैः हरिता Green with the young blades of grass, इन्द्रगोपैः लोहिता red with Indragopa insects, उच्छिलीन्ध्रकृतच्छाया च and possessing the beauty created by emerging mushrooms, भूः the earth, नृणां श्रीः इव अभूत् became like the prosperity of men.

Simple meaning: The earth became vibrant green with new grass and was dotted with red Indragopa insects. With mushrooms springing up everywhere to add to its beauty, the earth looked like a wealthy person whose prosperity is displayed through various riches and adornments.

क्षेत्राणि सस्यसम्पद्भिः कर्षकाणां मुदं ददुः ।

धनिनामुपतापं च दैवाधीनमजानताम् ॥10.20.12॥

Word-by-word meaning: क्षेत्राणि the fields, सस्य-सम्पद्भिः with their wealth of crops, कर्षकाणाम् of the farmers, मुदम् joy, ददुः gave, धनिनाम् of the wealthy (landlords/moneylenders), उपतापम् distress/remorse, च and, दैव-अधीनम् (that prosperity is) dependent on destiny, अजानताम् who do not understand

Anvaya: सस्यसम्पद्भिः क्षेत्राणि कर्षकाणां मुदं ददुः, (परन्तु) दैवाधीनम् अजानतां धनिनाम् उपतापं च (ददुः)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सस्यसम्पद्भिः क्षेत्राणि The fields, with their abundance of crops, कर्षकाणां मुदं ददुः gave joy to the farmers, (परन्तु) दैवाधीनम् अजानतां but to those who do not understand that it is all dependent on destiny, धनिनाम् उपतापं च (ददुः) they gave distress to the wealthy people.

Simple meaning: The fields, now lush with an abundance of crops, brought great joy to the hardworking farmers. However, that same prosperity caused distress to the wealthy—those who, failing to realize that all success ultimately depends on the will of Providence, felt envious or regretted not being able to extract more from the abundance.

Philosophical Insight

The verse highlights two different reactions to prosperity:

जलस्थलौकसः सर्वे नववारिनिषेवया ।

अबिभ्रद् रुचिरं रूपं यथा हरिनिषेवया ॥10.20.13॥

Word-by-word meaning: जल-स्थल-ओकसः the inhabitants of the water and the land, सर्वे all, नव-वारि-निषेवया by utilizing/honoring the fresh water, अबिभ्रत् assumed/manifested, रुचिरम् beautiful, रूपम् form, यथा just as, हरि-निषेवया by service to Lord Hari.

Anvaya: सर्वे जलस्थलौकसः नववारिनिषेवया रुचिरं रूपम् अबिभ्रत्, यथा हरिनिषेवया (रुचिरं रूपं बिभ्रति)।

This verse is a masterpiece of Sanskrit wordplay, using the word Guna in two different senses:

Element

Material Level

Spiritual Level

The Sky

Gunini: Filled with clouds and colors.

Prakriti: The manifest world of the three modes.

The Rainbow

Nirgunam: A bow without a string.

The Purusha: The Lord who is Nirguna (beyond modes).

Guna-vyatikare: The mixture of light and water.

Just as the rainbow is seen in the sky but is not "strung" or tied to the physical laws of a wooden bow, the Divine appears within the material creation but is never bound by its limitations.

The poet lists four specific "impurities" of the rainy season that Autumn cleanses, creating a parallel to spiritual purification when the mind is devoted to Krishna:

गाधवारिचरास्तापमविन्दञ्छरदर्कजम् ।

Word-by-word meaning: गाध-वारि-चराः those living in shallow water, तापम् distress/heat, अविन्दन् experienced, शरद्-अर्क-जम् born from the autumn sun, यथा just as, दरिद्रः a poor man, कृपणः miserable/wretched, कुटुम्बी a householder, अविजित-इन्द्रियः whose senses are uncontrolled

Anvaya: गाधवारिचराः शरदर्केजं तापम् अविन्दन्, यथा अविजितेन्द्रियः दरिद्रः कृपणः कुटुम्बी (तापम् अविन्दति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: गाधवारिचराः Those living in shallow water, शरदर्केजं born from the autumn sun, तापम् अविन्दन् experienced distress, यथा just as, अविजितेन्द्रियः one whose senses are not conquered, दरिद्रः कृपणः कुटुम्बी poor wretched householder (experiences distress).

Simple meaning: The creatures living in shallow water suffered greatly from the scorching heat of the autumn sun. They were like a poor, wretched householder who, having failed to control his senses, is constantly overwhelmed by the burning anxieties and miseries.

Philosophical Comparison

शनै शनैर्जहुः पङ्कं स्थलान्यामं च वीरुधः ।

यथाहंममतां धीराः शरीरादिष्वनात्मसु ॥10.20.39॥

Word-by-word meaning: शनैः शनैः gradually, जहुः gave up/abandoned, पङ्कम् the mud, स्थलानि the dry lands, आमम् the rawness/unripeness, च and, वीरुधः the plants/creepers, यथा just as, अहम्-ममताम् "I-ness" and "my-ness" (ego and possessiveness), धीराः the wise/sober, शरीर-आदिषु in the body and so on, अन्-आत्मसु in things that are not the self.

Anvaya: स्थलानि शनैः शनैः पङ्कं जहुः, वीरुधः (च) आमं (जहुः), यथा धीराः अनात्मसु शरीरादिषु अहंममतां (त्यजन्ति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: स्थलानि The dry lands, शनैः शनैः gradually, पङ्कं जहुः gave up mud, वीरुधः (च) and the plants, आमं (जहुः) gave up rawness, यथा just as, धीराः the wise, अनात्मसु in the non-self, शरीरादिषु in the body and so on, अहंममतां ego and possessiveness (give up).

Simple meaning: Little by little, the land gave up its mud and became dry, and the plants gave up their rawness as they reached maturity. In the same way, wise and sober people gradually give up the sense of "I" and "mine" regarding the physical body and other material things which are not the true self.

The Process of Ripening

This verse beautifully links the physical drying of the earth to the spiritual maturation of a seeker:

निश्चलाम्बुरभूत्तूष्णीं समुद्रः शरदागमे ।

आत्मन्युपरते सम्यङ्मुनिर्व्युपरतागमः ॥10.20.40॥

Word-by-word meaning: निश्चल-अम्बुः whose waters are still, अभूत् became, तूष्णीम् silent, समुद्रः the ocean, शरद्-आगमे with the arrival of autumn, आत्मनि in the self, उपरते when ceased (from worldly activity), सम्यक् completely, मुनिः a sage, व्युपरत-आगमः whose study of scriptures (or ritualistic noise) has ceased

Anvaya: शरदागमे समुद्रः निश्चलाम्बुः तूष्णीं (च) अभूत्, यथा आत्मनि सम्यक् उपरते व्युपरतागमः मुनिः (तूष्णीं भवति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: शरदागमे With the arrival of autumn, समुद्रः the ocean, निश्चलाम्बुः  with still waters, तूष्णीम् अभूत् became silent, यथा just as, आत्मनि in the self, सम्यक् completely, उपरते when withdrawn, व्युपरतागमः one whose scriptural study/ritual has ceased, मुनिः a sage (becomes silent).

Simple meaning: With the arrival of autumn, the ocean’s waters became perfectly still and its roaring waves fell silent. It resembled a sage who has completely withdrawn his mind from worldly affairs and, having realized the Self, has ceased even the external study of scriptures or the performance of rituals, remaining in a state of profound, silent peace.

The Silence of Realization

The monsoon ocean is characterized by turbulent waves and loud roaring, but the autumn ocean represents the state of Samadhi:

केदारेभ्यस्त्वपोऽगृह्णन् कर्षका दृढसेतुभिः ।

यथा प्राणैः स्रवज्ज्ञानं तन्निरोधेन योगिनः ॥10.20.41॥

Word-by-word meaning: केदारेभ्यः from the agricultural fields, तु indeed, अपः the water, अगृह्णन् held back/restrained, कर्षकाः the farmers, दृढ-सेतुभिः with strong embankments/dikes, यथा just as, प्राणैः through the senses, स्रवत् flowing out, ज्ञानम् knowledge/consciousness, तत्-निरोधेन by their restraint, योगिनः the yogis

Anvaya: कर्षकाः दृढसेतुभिः केदारेभ्यः अपः अगृह्णन्, यथा योगिनः तन्निरोधेन प्राणैः स्रवज्ज्ञानं (गृह्णन्ति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: कर्षकाः The farmers, दृढसेतुभिः with strong dikes, केदारेभ्यः from the fields, अपः the water, अगृह्णन् held back, यथा just as, योगिनः yogis, तन्निरोधेन  by their (senses') restraint, प्राणैः through the senses, स्रवज्ज्ञानं flowing knowledge/energy (restrain).

Simple meaning: Just as farmers build strong dikes to prevent the water from flowing out of their fields, yogis practice the restraint of their senses and breath to prevent their spiritual energy and knowledge from leaking away into the material world.

The Conservation of Energy

This verse uses a practical agricultural metaphor to explain a subtle yogic principle:

शरदर्कांशुजांस्तापान् भूतानामुडुपोऽहरत् ।

देहाभिमानजं बोधो मुकुन्दो व्रजयोषिताम् ॥10.20.42॥

Word-by-word meaning: शरद्-अर्क-अंशु-जान् born from the rays of the autumn sun, तापान् the sufferings/heat, भूतानाम् of all living beings, उडुपः the moon (lit. "lord of the stars"), अहरत् removed, देह-अभिमान-जम् born from identification with the body, बोधः spiritual knowledge, मुकुन्दः Lord Mukunda (Krishna), व्रज-योषिताम् of the women of Vraja

Anvaya: उडुपः भूतानां शरदर्कांशुजान् तापान् अहरत्, यथा बोधः देहाभिमानजं (तापं हरति), (यथा च) मुकुन्दः व्रजयोषितां (तापम् अहरत्)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: उडुपः The moon, भूतानां of living beings, शरदर्कांशुजान्  तापान् अहरत् removed the heat born from the rays of the autumn sun, यथा just as, बोधः spiritual knowledge, देहाभिमानजं  born from bodily identification (removes heat), (यथा च) and just as, मुकुन्दः Lord Mukunda, व्रजयोषिताम् of the women of Vraja (removed the heat).

Simple meaning: The autumn moon removed the distress caused to all living beings by the burning rays of the autumn sun. This is similar to how spiritual knowledge removes the suffering born from bodily identification, and just as Lord Mukunda removed the burning ache of separation from the gopis of Vraja.

The Cooling Grace

This verse contains a "double" comparison, linking a natural phenomenon to both a philosophical truth and a specific narrative emotion:

खमशोभत निर्मेघं शरद्विमलतारकम् ।

सत्त्वयुक्तं यथा चित्तं शब्दब्रह्मार्थदर्शनम् ॥10.20.43॥

Word-by-word meaning: खम् the sky, अशोभत shone/became beautiful, निर्मेघम् free from clouds, शरद्-विमल-तारकम् with the clear stars of autumn, सत्त्व-युक्तम् endowed with the quality of goodness (sattva), यथा just as, चित्तम् the consciousness/mind, शब्द-ब्रह्म-अर्थ-दर्शनम् perceive the meaning of the Vedic scriptures (shabda-brahma)

Anvaya: शरद्विमलतारकं निर्मेघं खम् अशोभत, यथा शब्दब्रह्मार्थदर्शनं सत्त्वयुक्तं चित्तं (शोभते)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: शरद्विमलतारकं Possessing the clear stars of autumn, निर्मेघं free from clouds, खम् the sky, अशोभत shone, यथा just as, शब्दब्रह्मार्थदर्शनं perceiving the meaning of the Vedic scriptures, सत्त्वयुक्तं endowed with goodness, चित्तम् the mind (shines).

Simple meaning: The autumn sky, now free from clouds and filled with brilliantly clear stars, shone with great beauty. It was like a mind purified by perceiving the true, deep meaning of the Vedic scriptures.

Clarity of Vision

This verse draws a parallel between the macrocosm of the sky and the microcosm of human consciousness:

अखण्डमण्डलो व्योम्नि रराजोडुगणैः शशी ।

यथा यदुपतिः कृष्णो वृष्णिचक्रावृतो भुवि ॥10.20.44॥

Word-by-word meaning: अखण्ड-मण्डलः possessing a full/perfect orb, व्योम्नि in the sky, रराज shone, उडु-गणैः with the hosts of stars, शशी the moon, यथा just as, यदु-पतिः the Lord of the Yadus, कृष्णः Krishna, वृष्णि-चक्र-आवृतः surrounded by the circle of the Vrishnis, भुवि on the earth

Anvaya: उडुगणैः (सह) अखण्डमण्डलः शशी व्योम्नि रराज, यथा वृष्णिचक्रावृतः यदुपतिः कृष्णः भुवि (राजते)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: उडुगणैः With the hosts of stars, अखण्डमण्डलः शशी the moon having a full orb, व्योम्नि रराज shone in the sky, यथा just as, वृष्णिचक्रावृतः surrounded by the circle of the Vrishnis, यदुपतिः कृष्णः the Lord of the Yadus, Krishna, भुवि on the earth (shines).

Simple meaning: The full moon, with its perfect circular orb, shone brilliantly in the sky surrounded by countless stars. It appeared just like Lord Krishna, the master of the Yadus, when He appeared on earth surrounded by the circle of His Vrishni dynasty.

The King and the Moon

This verse provides a beautiful visual parallel that connects the celestial realm to the earthly pastimes of the Lord:

आश्लिष्य समशीतोष्णं प्रसूनवनमारुतम् ।

जनास्तापं जहुर्गोप्यो न कृष्णहृतचेतसः ॥10.20.45॥

Word-by-word meaning: आश्लिष्य having embraced, सम-शीत-उष्णम् neither too cold nor too hot (temperate), प्रसून-वन-मारुतम् the breeze from the flowery forests, जनाः the people, तापम् their distress/fatigue, जहुः gave up/forgot, गोप्यः the gopis, न not, कृष्ण-हृत-चेतसः those whose hearts were stolen by Krishna

Anvaya: समशीतोष्णं प्रसूनवनमारुतम् आश्लिष्य जनाः तापं जहुः, (परन्तु) कृष्णहृतचेतसः गोप्यः न (जहुः)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: समशीतोष्णं प्रसूनवनमारुतम् Temperate (Not too cold and hot) breeze from the flowering forests, आश्लिष्य having embraced, जनाः the people, तापं जहुः gave up distress/fatigue, (परन्तु) but, कृष्णहृतचेतसः those whose hearts were stolen by Krishna, गोप्यः the gopis, न did not (give up their distress).

Simple meaning: By embracing the temperate autumn breeze that carried the scent of blossoming forests, the people of Vraja found relief from all their physical fatigue. However, the gopis, whose hearts had been stolen by Krishna, could not find relief from the burning ache of their love for Him.

The Contrast of Relief

This verse highlights the difference between physical comfort and the "divine distress" of love:

गावो मृगा खगा नार्यः पुष्पिण्यः शरदाभवन् ।

अन्वीयमानाः स्ववृषैः फलैरीशक्रिया इव ॥10.20.46॥

Word-by-word meaning: गावः the cows, मृगाः the deer, खगाः the birds, नार्यः the women, पुष्पिण्यः fertile/blooming (in their menses), शरदा by the autumn season, अभवन् became, अन्वीयमानाः being followed, स्व-वृषैः by their respective males, फलैः by results, ईश-क्रियाः activities performed for the Lord, इव like

Anvaya: शरदा स्ववृषैः अन्वीयमानाः गावः मृगाः खगाः नार्यः (च) पुष्पिण्यः अभवन्, फलैः ईशक्रियाः इव।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: शरदा In autumn, स्ववृषैः अन्वीयमानाः being followed by their own males, गावः मृगाः खगाः नार्यः the cows, the deer, the birds and the women, पुष्पिण्यः became fertile/blooming, फलैः ईशक्रियाः इव just as results (follow) the activities performed for the Lord.

Simple meaning: Under the influence of autumn, followed by their respective mates the cows, deer, birds, and women all conceived. Just as selfless activities performed for the Supreme Lord are naturally and inevitably followed by their auspicious results.

The Law of Natural Consequence

This verse uses a striking metaphor to link biological fertility with spiritual merit:

उदहृष्यन् वारिजानि सूर्योत्थाने कुमुद् विना ।

राज्ञा तु निर्भया लोका यथा दस्यून् विना नृप ॥10.20.47॥

Word-by-word meaning: उदहृष्यन् rejoiced/bloomed, वारिजानि the water-born flowers (lotuses), सूर्य-उत्थाने upon the rising of the sun, कुमुद् the night-blooming lily, विना except, राज्ञा by a (good) king, तु indeed, निर्भयाः fearless, लोकाः the people, यथा just as, दस्यून् thieves/rogues, विना without, नृप O King (Parikshit)

Anvaya: नृप! सूर्योत्थाने कुमुद् विना वारिजानि उदहृष्यन्, यथा राज्ञा (सति) दस्यून् विना लोकाः निर्भयाः (भवन्ति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नृप O King, सूर्योत्थाने upon the rising of the sun, कुमुद् विना except the night-blooming lily, वारिजानि the water-born lotuses, उदहृष्यन् bloomed/rejoiced, यथा just as, राज्ञा by a king, दस्यून् विना without thieves, लोकाः निर्भयाः the people (become) fearless .

Simple meaning: O King Parikshit, when the sun rose, all the water-born lotuses bloomed with joy, except for the night-blooming lily. Similarly, under the rule of a capable king, all citizens become fearless and thrive, with the sole exception of thieves and rogues, who find no joy in a well-governed state.

The Sun and the Lotus

This verse uses a classic poetic device to illustrate the effects of righteous leadership:

पुरग्रामेष्वाग्रयणैरैन्द्रियैश्च महोत्सवैः ।

बभौ भूः पक्वसस्याढ्या कलाभ्यां नितरां हरेः ॥10.20.48॥

Word-by-word meaning: पुर-ग्रामेषु in the cities and villages, आग्रयणैः with the Agrayana sacrifices (offering of the first grains), ऐन्द्रियैः for the pleasure of the senses, च and, महा-उत्सवैः with great festivals, बभौ shone, भूः the earth, पक्व-सस्य-आढ्या rich with ripe crops, कलाभ्याम् by the two portions/expansions (Krishna and Balarama), नितराम् exceedingly, हरेः of Lord Hari

आग्रयण   आग्रयण/ (अम्), n. oblation consisting of first-fruits at the end of the rainy season, src. Kosha Ashtadhyayi

Anvaya: पक्वसस्याढ्या भूः पुरग्रामेषु आग्रयणैः ऐन्द्रियैः महोत्सवैः च हरेः कलाभ्यां (कृष्णरामाभ्यां) नितरां बभौ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: पक्वसस्याढ्या Rich with ripe crops, भूः the earth, पुरग्रामेषु in the cities and villages, आग्रयणैः with first-harvest sacrifices, ऐन्द्रियैः pleasing to the senses, महोत्सवैः with great festivals, च and, हरेः of Lord Hari, कलाभ्यां by the two expansions (Krishna and Balarama), नितरां exceedingly, बभौ shone.

Simple meaning: The earth was now heavy with ripe, golden crops. In every city and village, people celebrated with Agrayana sacrifices—offering the first grains to the gods—and held grand festivals that delighted the senses. Because of the presence of Lord Hari’s two expansions, Krishna and Balarama, the entire land shone with extraordinary beauty and prosperity.

The Climax of Abundance

This verse describes the physical and spiritual "harvest" of the autumn season:

वणिङ्मुनिनृपस्नाता निर्गम्यार्थान् प्रपेदिरे ।

वर्षरुद्धा यथा सिद्धाः स्वपिण्डान् काल आगते ॥10.20.49॥

Word-by-word meaning: वणिक्-मुनि-नृप-स्नाताः the merchants, the sages, kings, and students (who have completed their vows), निर्गम्य coming out, अर्थान् their respective goals/objects, प्रपेदिरे obtained/pursued, वर्ष-रुद्धाः those who had been restricted by the rainy season, यथा just as, सिद्धाः perfected souls, स्व-पिण्डान् their respective bodies/destinations, काले आगते when the proper time arrives

Anvaya: वर्षरुद्धाः वणिङ्मुनिनृपस्नाताः निर्गम्य अर्थान् प्रपेदिरे, यथा काले आगते सिद्धाः स्वपिण्डान् (प्रपद्यन्ते)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: वर्षरुद्धाः Restricted by the rains, वणिङ्मुनिनृपस्नाताः merchants, sages, kings, and students, निर्गम्य having come out, अर्थान् प्रपेदिरे pursued their goals, यथा just as, काले आगते when the time comes, सिद्धाः perfected souls, स्वपिण्डान् their own bodies/forms (obtain).

Simple meaning: The merchants, sages, kings, and students, who had all been restricted to their homes by the rains, now ventured out to pursue their respective goals. They were like perfected souls who, having waited for the right moment, leave their temporary shells to attain their destined spiritual forms.

Breaking the Stagnation

During the four months of the rainy season (Caturmasya), travel and major activities were traditionally halted. Autumn marks the "release" into action:

श्रीमद्भागवते महापुराणे पारमहंस्यां संहितायां दशमस्कन्धे पूर्वार्धे प्रावृट्शरद्वर्णनं नाम विंशः अध्यायः॥20॥

Thus ends the twentieth discourse named 'A description of the Rains and Autumn', in the first half of Book Ten of the great and glorious Bhagavata-Purana.
