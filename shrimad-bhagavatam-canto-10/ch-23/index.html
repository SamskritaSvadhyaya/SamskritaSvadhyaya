Chapter 23

Redemption of the wives of some Brahmanas engaged in a sacrifice

श्रीमद्भागवतमहापुराणम्

दशमः स्कन्धः

यज्ञपत्नी उद्धरणं नाम त्रयोविंशः अध्यायः

(52 Shlokas)

गोपा ऊचुः

राम राम महावीर्य कृष्ण दुष्टनिबर्हण ।

एषा वै बाधते क्षुन्नस्तच्छान्तिं कर्तुमर्हथः॥10.23.1॥

Word-by-word meaning: गोपाः ऊचुः the cowherd boys said, राम राम O Rama, O Rama, महा-वीर्य O one of great prowess, कृष्ण O Krishna, दुष्ट-निबर्हण O destroyer of the wicked, एषा this, वै indeed, बाधते afflicts, क्षुत् hunger, नः us, तत्-शान्तिम् its peaceful resolution (satisfaction), कर्तुम् अर्हथः You both should do

Anvaya: गोपाः ऊचुः – राम! राम! महावीर्य!, कृष्ण! दुष्टनिबर्हण! एषा क्षुत् नः वै बाधते, (युवाम्) तच्छान्तिं कर्तुम् अर्हथः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: गोपाः ऊचुः The cowherd boys said: राम राम महावीर्य O Rama, Rama, of great prowess, कृष्ण दुष्टनिबर्हण O Krishna, destroyer of the wicked, एषा क्षुत् नः वै बाधते this hunger is indeed afflicting us, (युवाम्) तच्छान्तिं कर्तुम् अर्हथः You both should provide the means to satisfy it.

Simple meaning: The cowherd boys said: "O Rama, O Rama, You are so powerful! O Krishna, You are the destroyer of all that is evil! We are greatly troubled by hunger. Please, both of You, find a way to satisfy it."

श्री शुक उवाच

इति विज्ञापितो गोपैर्भगवान् देवकीसुतः ।

भक्ताया विप्रभार्यायाः प्रसीदन्निदमब्रवीत् ॥10.23.2॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, इति thus, विज्ञापितः being informed (requested), गोपैः by the cowherd boys, भगवान् the Supreme Lord, देवकी-सुतः the son of Devaki, भक्तायाः for His devotees, विप्र-भार्यायाः the wives of the brahmanas, प्रसीदन् desiring to show mercy (being pleased), इदम् this, अब्रवीत् spoke

Anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच – गोपैः इति विज्ञापितः देवकीसुतः भगवान् भक्तायाः विप्रभार्यायाः प्रसीदन् इदम् अब्रवीत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said: गोपैः इति विज्ञापितः being thus requested by the cowherd boys, देवकीसुतः भगवान् the son of Devaki, the Supreme Lord, भक्तायाः विप्रभार्यायाः प्रसीदन् desiring to show favor to the wives of the brahmanas who were His devotees, इदम् अब्रवीत् spoke this.

Simple meaning: Shri Shuka said: After being informed of the situation by the cowherd boys, the Supreme Lord Krishna, the son of Devaki, spoke the following words. He did so because He wished to show His special mercy to the wives of the brahmanas, who were His sincere devotees.

प्रयात देवयजनं ब्राह्मणा ब्रह्मवादिनः ।

सत्रमाङ्गिरसं नाम ह्यासते स्वर्गकाम्यया ॥10.23.3॥

Word-by-word meaning: प्रयात go, देव-यजनम् to the place of sacrifice, ब्राह्मणाः the brahmanas, ब्रह्म-वादिनः followers of the Vedas (or performers of rituals), सत्रम् a sacrifice, आङ्गिरसम् named Angirasa, नाम by name, हि indeed, आसते they are performing, स्वर्ग-काम्यया with the desire to reach heaven

Anvaya: ब्रह्मवादिनः ब्राह्मणाः स्वर्गकाम्यया आङ्गिरसं नाम सत्रं हि आसते, (यूयं) देवयजनं प्रयात।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: ब्रह्मवादिनः ब्राह्मणाः The brahmanas who are experts in Vedic rituals, स्वर्गकाम्यया with the desire for the heavenly planets, आङ्गिरसं नाम सत्रं हि आसते are indeed performing the sacrifice known as Angirasa, (यूयं) देवयजनं प्रयात you all should go to that place of sacrifice.

Simple meaning: Krishna said: "The learned brahmanas are currently performing a sacrifice known as Angirasa with the hope of attaining the heavenly planets. You all should go to the place where they are conducting this ritual.

तत्र गत्वौदनं गोपा याचतास्मद्विसर्जिताः ।

कीर्तयन्तो भगवत आर्यस्य मम चाभिधाम् ॥10.23.4॥

Word-by-word meaning: तत्र there, गत्वा having gone, ओदनम् cooked rice (food), गोपाः O cowherd boys, याचत ask for, अस्मत्-विसर्जिताः sent by us, कीर्तयन्तः chanting (announcing), भगवतः of the Lord, आर्यस्य of the noble elder brother (Balarama), मम of Me, च and, अभिधाम् the names

Anvaya: गोपाः! तत्र गत्वा अस्मद्विसर्जिताः भगवतः आर्यस्य मम च अभिधां कीर्तयन्तः ओदनं याचत।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: गोपाः! O cowherd boys, तत्र गत्वा Having gone there, अस्मद्विसर्जिताः as messengers sent by us, भगवतः आर्यस्य मम च अभिधां कीर्तयन्तः announcing the names of the noble Lord Balarama and Myself, ओदनं याचत ask for cooked rice.

Simple meaning: "O cowherd boys, go there and tell them that you have been sent by us. Announce the names of My noble elder brother Balarama and Myself, and ask them to give you some food."

इत्यादिष्टा भगवता गत्वायाचन्त ते तथा ।

कृताञ्जलिपुटा विप्रान् दण्डवत् पतिता भुवि ॥10.23.5॥

Word-by-word meaning: इति thus, आदिष्टाः being instructed, भगवता by the Supreme Lord, गत्वा having gone, अयाचन्त they begged, ते they (the cowherd boys), तथा in that manner, कृत-अञ्जलि-पुटाः with folded hands, विप्रान् from the brahmanas, दण्ड-वत् like a stick, पतिताः falling, भुवि on the ground

Anvaya: भगवता इति आदिष्टाः ते गत्वा भुवि दण्डवत् पतिताः कृताञ्जलिपुटाः तथा विप्रान् अयाचन्त।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: भगवता इति आदिष्टाः Being thus instructed by the Supreme Lord, ते गत्वा they went there, भुवि दण्डवत् पतिताः falling on the ground like a stick (offering prostrated obeisances), कृताञ्जलिपुटाः with hands folded in prayer, तथा विप्रान् अयाचन्त begged the brahmanas in that way.

Simple meaning: Following the Lord's instructions, the cowherd boys went to the sacrificial arena. They prostrated on the ground to offer their respects and, with folded hands, made their request to the brahmanas as Krishna had directed.

हे भूमिदेवाः शृणुत कृष्णस्यादेशकारिणः ।

प्राप्ताञ्जानीत भद्रं वो गोपान् नो रामचोदितान् ॥10.23.6॥

Word-by-word meaning: हे भूमि-देवाः O gods of the earth (brahmanas), शृणुत please listen, कृष्णस्य of Krishna, आदेश-कारिणः carrying out the orders, प्राप्तान् arrived, जानीत know (us), भद्रम् good fortune, वः unto you, गोपान् cowherd boys, नः us, राम-चोदितान् sent by Rama

Anvaya: हे भूमिदेवाः! शृणुत, वः भद्रम्। कृष्णस्य आदेशकारिणः रामचोदितान् नः गोपान् प्राप्तान् जानीत।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: हे भूमिदेवाः! O gods of the earth!, शृणुत Please listen, वः भद्रम् may there be good fortune unto you. कृष्णस्य आदेशकारिणः executing the order of Krishna, रामचोदितान् sent by Rama, नः गोपान् प्राप्तान् जानीत know us as the cowherd boys who have arrived here.

Simple meaning: "O earthly gods, please listen! May all good fortune be yours. Please recognize us as cowherd boys who have come here at the command of Krishna and have been sent by Balarama."

गाश्चारयन्तावविदूर ओदनं रामाच्युतौ वो लषतो बुभुक्षितौ ।

तयोर्द्विजा ओदनमर्थिनोर्यदि श्रद्धा च वो यच्छत धर्मवित्तमाः ॥10.23.7॥

Word-by-word meaning: गाः cows, चारयन्तौ grazing, अविदूरे nearby, ओदनम् cooked rice, राम-अच्युतौ Rama and Krishna (Acyuta), वः from you, लषतः desire, बुभुक्षितौ hungry, तयोः for them, द्विजाः O brahmanas, ओदनम् food, अर्थिनोः who are asking, यदि if, श्रद्धा faith (will), च and, वः your, यच्छत please give, धर्म-वित्-तमाः O best of the knowers of religion

Anvaya: द्विजाः! धर्मवित्तमाः! अविदूरे गाः चारयन्तौ बुभुक्षितौ रामाच्युतौ वः ओदनं लषतः। यदि वः श्रद्धा च (अस्ति), (तर्हि) अर्थिनोः तयोः ओदनं यच्छत।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: द्विजाः! O twice-born brahmanas, धर्मवित्तमाः! O best of the knowers of religion, अविदूरे गाः चारयन्तौ While grazing the cows nearby, बुभुक्षितौ hungry, रामाच्युतौ Rama and Krishna, वः ओदनं लषतः desire cooked rice from you. यदि वः श्रद्धा च (अस्ति) If you have faith, (तर्हि) अर्थिनोः तयोः ओदनं यच्छत then please give food to those two who are asking for it.

Simple meaning: "O learned brahmanas, you are the best among those who know the principles of religion. Rama and Krishna are grazing their cows nearby and are very hungry. They desire some food from you. If you have faith in Them, please give some rice to those two who are now requesting it from you."

दीक्षायाः पशुसंस्थायाः सौत्रामण्याश्च सत्तमाः ।

अन्यत्र दीक्षितस्यापि नान्नमश्नन् हि दुष्यति ॥10.23.8॥

Word-by-word meaning: दीक्षायाः of the initiation, पशु-संस्थायाः of the animal sacrifice, सौत्रामण्याः of the Sautramani sacrifice, च and, सत्तमाः O righteous souls, अन्यत्र otherwise, दीक्षितस्य of the initiated, अपि even, न not, अन्नम् food, अश्नन् eating, हि indeed, दुष्यति becomes polluted (commits a fault)

Anvaya: सत्तमाः! दीक्षायाः पशुसंस्थायाः सौत्रामण्याः च अन्यत्र दीक्षितस्य अपि अन्नम् अश्नन् न हि दुष्यति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सत्तमाः! O priests!, दीक्षायाः पशुसंस्थायाः सौत्रामण्याः च अन्यत्र Except during the period of initiation or the specific animal sacrifices and the Sautramani sacrifice, दीक्षितस्य अपि अन्नम् अश्नन् eating the food of even an initiated person, न हि दुष्यति one indeed does not become polluted.

Simple meaning: "O priests, it is well known that one does not commit a religious fault by eating the food of an initiated person, except during the specific periods of the Pashu-samstha and Sautramani sacrifices. Therefore, since this is not one of those restricted times, you can give us food without any hesitation.

इति ते भगवद्याच्ञां शृण्वन्तोऽपि न शुश्रुवुः ।

क्षुद्राशा भूरिकर्माणो बालिशा वृद्धमानिनः ॥10.23.9॥

Word-by-word meaning: इति thus, ते they (the brahmanas), भगवत्-याच्ञाम् the request of the Supreme Lord, शृण्वन्तः hearing, अपि although, न not, शुश्रुवुः heeded (listened), क्षुद्र-आशाः possessing petty desires, भूरि-कर्माणः engaged in elaborate rituals, बालिशाः childish (ignorant), वृद्ध-मानिनः considering themselves wise (elders)

Anvaya: क्षुद्राशाः भूरिकर्माणः बालिशाः वृद्धमानिनः ते भगवद्याच्ञां शृण्वन्तः अपि न शुश्रुवुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: क्षुद्राशाः Possessing petty desires, भूरिकर्माणः engaged in vast ritualistic activities, बालिशाः though ignorant, वृद्धमानिनः considering themselves to be very wise, ते they, भगवद्याच्ञां शृण्वन्तः अपि although hearing the request of the Supreme Lord, न शुश्रुवुः did not heed it.

Simple meaning: Although they heard the request of the Supreme Lord, the brahmanas chose to ignore it. Entangled in elaborate rituals for the sake of petty desires, these men were spiritually ignorant, yet they pridefully considered themselves to be wise and experienced authorities.

देशः कालः पृथग् द्रव्यं मन्त्रतन्त्रर्त्विजोऽग्नयः ।

देवता यजमानश्च क्रतुर्धर्मश्च यन्मयः ॥10.23.10॥

Word-by-word meaning: देशः the place, कालः the time, पृथक् distinct, द्रव्यम् the ingredients (of sacrifice), मन्त्र-तन्त्र-ऋत्विजः the hymns, the rituals, and the officiating priests, अग्नयः the sacrificial fires, देवताः the demigods, यजमानः the patron (performer of the sacrifice), च and, क्रतुः the sacrifice itself, धर्मः the religious principles, च and, यत्-मयः consisting of Him (the Supreme Lord)

Anvaya: देशः कालः पृथग् द्रव्यं मन्त्रतन्त्रर्त्विजः अग्नयः देवताः यजमानः च क्रतुः धर्मः च यन्मयः (सः एषः भगवान्)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: देशः कालः पृथग् द्रव्यं The place, time, and various physical ingredients, मन्त्रतन्त्रर्त्विजः अग्नयः the mantras, the methods of worship, the priests, and the fires, देवताः यजमानः च the presiding deities and the performer, क्रतुः धर्मः च the sacrificial ceremony and the resulting merit, यन्मयः all consist of Him (the Supreme Lord).

Simple meaning: The place, the time, the various ingredients, the mantras, the ritual procedures, the priests, the sacrificial fires, the demigods, the performer of the sacrifice, the sacrifice itself, and the religious merit—all of these are simply manifestations of the Supreme Lord Himself.

तं ब्रह्म परमं साक्षाद् भगवन्तमधोक्षजम् ।

मनुष्यदृष्ट्या दुष्प्रज्ञा मर्त्यात्मानो न मेनिरे ॥10.23.11॥

Word-by-word meaning: तम् Him, ब्रह्म परमम् the Supreme Brahman, साक्षात् directly, भगवन्तम् the Supreme Lord, अधोक्षजम् the one beyond the reach of material senses, मनुष्य-दृष्ट्या with a human perspective, दुष्प्रज्ञाः those of poor intelligence, मर्त्य-आत्मानः identifying themselves with the mortal body, न not, मेनिरे they considered

Anvaya: मर्त्यात्मानः दुष्प्रज्ञाः साक्षात् परं ब्रह्म अधोक्षजं तं भगवन्तं मनुष्यदृष्ट्या न मेनिरे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: मर्त्यात्मानः Those who identify the self with the mortal body, दुष्प्रज्ञाः being of poor intelligence, साक्षात् परं ब्रह्म directly the Supreme Brahman, अधोक्षजं who is beyond material perception, तं भगवन्तं that Supreme Lord, मनुष्यदृष्ट्या through a human perspective, न मेनिरे did not recognize (Him).

Simple meaning: Because they identified themselves with their mortal bodies and possessed poor intelligence, those brahmanas viewed the Supreme Lord through a limited human perspective. They failed to recognize that He is the Supreme Brahman and the Transcendental Lord who exists far beyond the reach of material senses.

न ते यदोमिति प्रोचुर्न नेति च परंतप ।

गोपा निराशाः प्रत्येत्य तथोचुः कृष्णरामयोः ॥10.23.12॥

Word-by-word meaning: न not, ते they, यत् because, ओम् इति "yes" (so be it), प्रोचुः spoke, न not, न इति "no", च and, परंतप O chastiser of enemies (Parikshita), गोपाः the cowherd boys, निराशाः disappointed (without hope), प्रत्येत्य having returned, तथा so, ऊचुः told, कृष्ण-रामयोः to Krishna and Rama

Anvaya: परंतप! यत् ते 'ओम्' इति न प्रोचुः, 'न' इति च न (प्रोचुः); निराशाः गोपाः प्रत्येत्य कृष्णरामयोः तथा ऊचुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: परंतप! O chastiser of enemies, यत् Becasue, ते 'ओम्' इति न प्रोचुः they did not say "yes", 'न' इति च न (प्रोचुः) and they did not even say "no"; निराशाः गोपाः the disappointed cowherd boys, प्रत्येत्य having returned, कृष्णरामयोः तथा ऊचुः told Krishna and Rama exactly what had happened.

Simple meaning: O Parantapa (King Parikshit), the brahmanas did not bother to say "yes," nor did they even say "no" to the request. Feeling completely disappointed, the cowherd boys returned to Krishna and Rama and reported exactly what had taken place.

तदुपाकर्ण्य भगवान् प्रहस्य जगदीश्वरः ।

व्याजहार पुनर्गोपान् दर्शयँल्लौकिकीं गतिम् ॥10.23.13॥

Word-by-word meaning: तत्-उपाकर्ण्य hearing that, भगवान् the Supreme Lord, प्रहस्य laughing, जगत्-ईश्वरः the Lord of the universe, व्याजहार addressed, पुनः again, गोपान् the cowherd boys, दर्शयन् showing (mimicking), लौकिकीम् human (worldly), गतिम् behavior (way)

Anvaya: जगदीश्वरः भगवान् तदुपाकर्ण्य प्रहस्य लौकिकीं गतिं दर्शयन् पुनः गोपान् व्याजहार।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: जगदीश्वरः भगवान् The Supreme Lord of the universe, तदुपाकर्ण्य hearing that report, प्रहस्य laughing, लौकिकीं गतिं दर्शयन् exhibiting the ways of ordinary human behavior, पुनः गोपान् व्याजहार spoke again to the cowherd boys.

Simple meaning: Upon hearing the report, the Supreme Lord of the universe laughed. Acting like an ordinary human being to show the nature of worldly life, He spoke again to the cowherd boys.

मां ज्ञापयत पत्नीभ्यः ससंकर्षणमागतम् ।

दास्यन्ति काममन्नं वः स्निग्धा मय्युषिता धिया ॥10.23.14॥

Word-by-word meaning: माम् Me, ज्ञापयत inform, पत्नीभ्यः to the wives (of the brahmanas), स-संकर्षणम् along with Balarama, आगतम् arrived, दास्यन्ति they will give, कामम् as much as desired, अन्नम् food, वः to you, स्निग्धाः affectionate, मयि in Me, उषिताः residing, धिया with their minds

Anvaya: (यूयं) पत्नीभ्यः ससंकर्षणं माम् आगतं ज्ञापयत; मयि धिया उषिताः स्निग्धाः (ताः) वः कामम् अन्नं दास्यन्ति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (यूयं) पत्नीभ्यः You all, to the wives (of the brahmanas), ससंकर्षणं माम् आगतं that I have arrived along with Balarama, ज्ञापयत inform; मयि धिया उषिताः those whose minds reside in Me, स्निग्धाः and who are full of affection, (ताः) वः कामम् अन्नं दास्यन्ति they will give you as much food as you desire.

Simple meaning: "Go and inform the wives of the brahmanas that I have arrived here with Balarama. Those women are very affectionate toward Me and always keep Me in their hearts; they will surely give you all the food you want."

गत्वाथ पत्नीशालायां दृष्ट्वाऽऽसीनाः स्वलङ्कृताः ।

नत्वा द्विजसतीर्गोपाः प्रश्रिता इदमब्रुवन् ॥10.23.15॥

Word-by-word meaning: गत्वा having gone, अथ then, पत्नी-शालायाम् in the house of the wives (of the brahmanas), दृष्ट्वा seeing, आसीनाः seated, सु-अलङ्कृताः well-decorated, नत्वा bowing down, द्विज-सतीः to the chaste wives of the brahmanas, गोपाः the cowherd boys, प्रश्रिताः humbly, इदम् this, अब्रुवन् said

Anvaya: अथ पत्नीशालायां गत्वा आसीनाः स्वलङ्कृताः द्विजसतीः दृष्ट्वा गोपाः नत्वा प्रश्रिताः इदम् अब्रुवन्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अथ Then, पत्नीशालायां गत्वा having gone to the place of the wives, आसीनाः स्वलङ्कृताः द्विजसतीः दृष्ट्वा seeing the chaste wives of the brahmanas seated and well-decorated, गोपाः नत्वा the cowherd boys having bowed down, प्रश्रिताः humbly, इदम् अब्रुवन् spoke this.

Simple meaning: The cowherd boys then went to the house where the wives of the brahmanas were staying. Finding those chaste ladies seated there and beautifully adorned, the boys bowed down before them and humbly spoke as follows.

नमो वो विप्रपत्नीभ्यो निबोधत वचांसि नः ।

इतोऽविदूरे चरता कृष्णेनेहेषिता वयम् ॥10.23.16॥

Word-by-word meaning: नमः obeisances, वः unto you, विप्र-पत्नीभ्यः to the wives of the brahmanas, निबोधत please hear, वचांसि the words, नः our, इतः from here, अविदूरे nearby, चरता who is wandering, कृष्णेन by Krishna, इह here, इषिताः sent, वयम् we

Anvaya: विप्रपत्नीभ्यः वः नमः। नः वचांसि निबोधत। इतः अविदूरे चरता कृष्णेन वयम् इह इषिताः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: विप्रपत्नीभ्यः वः नमः Unto you, the wives of the brahmanas, our obeisances. नः वचांसि निबोधत Please listen to our words. इतः अविदूरे चरता कृष्णेन by Krishna, who is wandering not far from here, वयम् इह इषिताः we have been sent here.

Simple meaning: "We offer our respectful obeisances unto you, the wives of the learned brahmanas. Please listen to what we have to say. Krishna is wandering with the cows not far from here, and He has sent us to you.

गाश्चारयन् स गोपालैः सरामो दूरमागतः ।

बुभुक्षितस्य तस्यान्नं सानुगस्य प्रदीयताम् ॥10.23.17॥

Word-by-word meaning: गाः the cows, चारयन् grazing, सः He (Krishna), गोपालैः with the cowherd boys, स-रामः along with Balarama, दूरम् a long distance, आगतः has come, बुभुक्षितस्य for Him who is hungry, तस्य for Him, अन्नम् food, स-अनुगस्य along with His companions, प्रदीयताम् please give

Anvaya: गोपालैः सरामः सः गाः चारयन् दूरम् आगतः। बुभुक्षितस्य सानुगस्य तस्य अन्नं प्रदीयताम्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: गोपालैः सरामः सः Along with the cowherd boys and Balarama, He, गाः चारयन् while grazing the cows, दूरम् आगतः has come a long distance. बुभुक्षितस्य सानुगस्य तस्य For Him and His companions who are hungry, अन्नं प्रदीयताम् please give food.

Simple meaning: "While grazing the cows with Balarama and the other cowherd boys, Krishna has traveled a long distance and has now arrived nearby. He and His companions are very hungry; therefore, please provide some food for them."

श्रुत्वाच्युतमुपायातं नित्यं तद्दर्शनोत्सुकाः ।

तत्कथाक्षिप्तमनसो बभूवुर्जातसम्भ्रमाः ॥10.23.18॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्रुत्वा hearing, अच्युतम् of Krishna (Acyuta), उपायातम् having arrived, नित्यम् always, तत्-दर्शन-उत्सुकाः eager for His sight (darshana), तत्-कथा-आक्षिप्त-मनसः their minds enchanted by stories of Him, बभूवुः they became, जात-सम्भ्रमाः greatly excited (agitated with joy)

Anvaya: नित्यं तद्दर्शनोत्सुकाः तत्कथाक्षिप्तमनसः (ताः) अच्युतम् उपायातं श्रुत्वा जातसम्भ्रमाः बभूवुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नित्यं तद्दर्शनोत्सुकाः Always eager to see Him, तत्कथाक्षिप्तमनसः and their minds being enchanted by hearing narrations about Him, (ताः) अच्युतम् उपायातं श्रुत्वा hearing that Acyuta (Krishna) had arrived nearby, जातसम्भ्रमाः बभूवुः became highly excited with joy.

Simple meaning: Those ladies had long been eager to see Krishna, and their minds were always enchanted by hearing stories about Him. As soon as they heard that He had arrived nearby, they became intensely excited and filled with joyful anticipation.

चतुर्विधं बहुगुणमन्नमादाय भाजनैः ।

अभिसस्त्रुः प्रियं सर्वाः समुद्रमिव निम्नगाः ॥10.23.19॥

Word-by-word meaning: चतुः-विधम् of four varieties, बहु-गुणम् possessing many rich qualities (delicious), अन्नम् food, आदाय taking, भाजनैः in large vessels, अभिसस्त्रुः they rushed, प्रियम् towards their beloved, सर्वाः all of them, समुद्रम् the ocean, इव like, निम्नगाः rivers

Anvaya: सर्वाः चतुर्विधं बहुगुणं अन्नं भाजनैः आदाय – निम्नगाः समुद्रम् इव – प्रियम् अभिसस्त्रुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सर्वाः All of them, चतुर्विधं बहुगुणं अन्नं food of four varieties and many excellent qualities, भाजनैः आदाय taking in large vessels, निम्नगाः समुद्रम् इव like rivers flowing toward the ocean, प्रियम् अभिसस्त्रुः rushed toward their beloved.

Simple meaning: All the wives immediately took large vessels filled with the four types of delicious food and rushed toward their beloved Krishna, just as rivers flow eagerly toward the ocean.

Note: The four kinds of food mentioned in our scriptures are:—

(1) भक्ष्य that which can be easily gulped, such as porridge

(2) भोज्य that which has to be masticated before it can be swallowed

(3) लेह्य that which can be licked, e.g., chutney

(4) चोष्य​  that which is sucked, e.g., an orange, sugarcane

निषिध्यमानाः पतिभिर्भ्रातृभिर्बन्धुभिः सुतैः ।

भगवत्युत्तमश्लोके दीर्घश्रुतधृताशयाः ॥10.23.20॥

Word-by-word meaning: निषिध्यमानाः being forbidden, पतिभिः by husbands, भ्रातृभिः by brothers, बन्धुभिः by relatives, सुतैः by sons, भगवति for the Supreme Lord, उत्तम-श्लोके for the one praised by sublime verses, दीर्घ-श्रुत-धृत-आशयाः their hearts held (fixed) by long-standing hearing (of His glories)

Anvaya: पतिभिः भ्रातृभिः बन्धुभिः सुतैः निषिध्यमानाः (अपि) उत्तमश्लोके भगवति दीर्घश्रुतधृताशयाः (ताः अभिसस्त्रुः)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: पतिभिः भ्रातृभिः बन्धुभिः सुतैः निषिध्यमानाः (अपि) Even though being forbidden by their husbands, brothers, relatives, and sons, उत्तमश्लोके भगवति in the Supreme Lord who is praised by sublime poetry, दीर्घश्रुतधृताशयाः (ताः) they whose hearts were firmly fixed by long-standing hearing of His glories, (अभिसस्त्रुः) rushed toward Him.

Simple meaning: Although their husbands, brothers, sons, and other relatives tried to stop them, the women could not be held back. Their hearts had long been captivated by hearing the glories of the Supreme Lord, Krishna, and they were determined to reach Him.

यमुनोपवनेऽशोकनवपल्लवमण्डिते ।

विचरन्तं वृतं गोपैः साग्रजं ददृशुः स्त्रियः ॥10.23.21॥

Word-by-word meaning: यमुना-उपवने in the garden near the Yamuna, अशोक-नव-पल्लव-मण्डिते decorated with the fresh leaves of Ashoka trees, विचरन्तम् wandering, वृतम् surrounded, गोपैः by the cowherd boys, स-अग्रजम् with His elder brother (Balarama), ददृशुः they saw, स्त्रियः the women

Anvaya: स्त्रियः अशोकनवपल्लवमण्डिते यमुनोपवने गोपैः वृतं साग्रजं विचरन्तं (कृष्णं) ददृशुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: स्त्रियः The women, अशोकनवपल्लवमण्डिते decorated with the new sprouts of Ashoka trees, यमुनोपवने in the grove by the Yamuna river, गोपैः वृतं surrounded by cowherd boys, साग्रजं along with His elder brother, विचरन्तं wandering, (कृष्णं) ददृशुः they saw (Krishna).

Simple meaning: In a garden near the Yamuna river that was beautifully adorned with the fresh, new leaves of Ashoka trees, the women finally saw Him. There was Krishna, wandering with His elder brother Balarama and surrounded by His cowherd friends.

श्यामं हिरण्यपरिधिं वनमाल्यबर्हधातुप्रवालनटवेषमनुव्रतांसे ।

विन्यस्तहस्तमितरेण धुनानमब्जं कर्णोत्पलालककपोलमुखाब्जहासम् ॥10.23.22॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्यामम् dark blue (complexion), हिरण्य-परिधिम् wearing golden-colored garments, वन-माल्य-बर्ह-धातु-प्रवाल-नट-वेषम् dressed like a dancer with forest garlands, peacock feathers, colored minerals, and tender shoots, अनुव्रतांसे on the shoulder of a devoted companion, विन्यस्त-हस्तम् having placed His hand, इतरेण with the other (hand), धुनानम् twirling, अब्जम् a lotus flower, कर्ण-उत्पल-अलक-कपोल-मुख-अब्ज-हासम् with lilies over His ears, curly hair on His cheeks, and a smile on His lotus-like face

Anvaya: श्यामं हिरण्यपरिधिं वनमाल्यबर्हधातुप्रवालनटवेषम् अनुव्रतांसे विन्यस्तहस्तम् इतरेण अब्जं धुनानं कर्णोत्पलालककपोलमुखाब्जहासं (तं कृष्णं ददृशुः) ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्यामम् Dark complexioned, हिरण्यपरिधिं wearing yellow silk, वनमाल्यबर्हधातुप्रवालनटवेषम् dressed like a dancer with forest garlands, peacock feathers, colored minerals, and buds, अनुव्रतांसे upon the shoulder of a follower, विन्यस्तहस्तम् having placed His hand, इतरेण with the other hand, अब्जम् a lotus, धुनानं twirling, कर्णोत्पलालककपोलमुखाब्जहासम् with lilies on His ears, curly hair on His cheeks, and a smile on His lotus-like face (they saw Him).

Simple meaning: The women beheld Krishna, whose complexion was the color of a dark rain cloud and who was draped in shimmering golden cloth. He was dressed like a dancer, adorned with peacock feathers, wreaths of forest flowers, touches of colorful mountain minerals, and fresh flower buds. He rested one arm on the shoulder of a friend, while with His other hand, He playfully twirled a lotus flower. Lilies tucked behind His ears brushed against His cheeks and curly locks, all framing the enchanting smile on His lotus-like face.

प्रायः श्रुतप्रियतमोदयकर्णपूरैर्यस्मिन् निमग्नमनसस्तमथाक्षिरन्ध्रैः ।

अन्तः प्रवेश्य सुचिरं परिरभ्य तापं प्राज्ञं यथाभिमतयो विजहुर्नरेन्द्र ॥10.23.23॥

Word-by-word meaning: प्रायः for a long time, श्रुत-प्रियतम-उदय-कर्ण-पूरैः by the streams of descriptions of their most beloved (Krishna) that acted as ornaments for their ears, यस्मिन् in whom, निमग्न-मनसः their minds being fully submerged, तम् Him, अथ then, अक्षि-रन्ध्रैः through the openings of their eyes, अन्तः within (their hearts), प्रवेश्य making Him enter, सुचिरम् for a very long time, परिरभ्य embracing, तापम् the distress (of separation), प्राज्ञम् a wise person, यथा just as, अभिमतयः people with various mental states or who identify with the material body, विजहुः they gave up, नरेन्द्र O King (Parikshit)

Anvaya: नरेन्द्र! प्रायः श्रुतप्रियतमोदयकर्णपूरैः यस्मिन् निमग्नमनसः (ताः स्त्रियः) अथ तम् अक्षिरन्ध्रैः अन्तः प्रवेश्य सुचिरं परिरभ्य तापं विजहुः यथा अभिमतयः प्राज्ञम्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नरेन्द्र O King!, प्रायः For a long time, श्रुतप्रियतमोदयकर्णपूरैः by the ear-ornaments consisting of the glories of their beloved which they had heard, यस्मिन् in whom, निमग्नमनसः having their minds absorbed, अथ then, तम् Him, अक्षिरन्ध्रैः through the apertures of the eyes, अन्तः inside, प्रवेश्य bringing in, सुचिरं for a long time, परिरभ्य embracing, तापं their suffering, विजहुः they abandoned, यथा just as, अभिमतयः people who identify with the body, प्राज्ञम् a realized soul..

Simple meaning: O King, for a long time these women had heard of their beloved Krishna's glories, which acted like beautiful ornaments for their ears and kept their minds constantly submerged in Him. Now, seeing Him in person, they took Him into their hearts through the path of their eyes. Embracing Him there for a long time, they finally let go of all the burning distress of separation—just as people of  false identifications with the physical body embrace a spiritually enlightened person and shake off their ignorance.

तास्तथा त्यक्तसर्वाशाः प्राप्ता आत्मदिदृक्षया ।

विज्ञायाखिलदृग्द्रष्टा प्राह प्रहसिताननः ॥10.23.24॥

Word-by-word meaning: ताः they (the women), तथा in that way, त्यक्त-सर्व-आशाः having abandoned all other desires, प्राप्ताः arrived, आत्म-दिदृक्षया with the desire to see Him (the Self), विज्ञाय understanding, अखिल-दृग्-द्रष्टा the Seer of all and everything, प्राह He spoke, प्रहसित-आननः His face brightened by a smile

Anvaya: अखिलदृग्द्रष्टा आत्मदिदृक्षया तथा त्यक्तसर्वाशाः प्राप्ताः ताः विज्ञाय प्रहसिताननः प्राह।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अखिलदृग्द्रष्टा The witness of all who sees everything, आत्मदिदृक्षया with the desire to see the Supreme Soul, तथा in that manner, त्यक्तसर्वाशाः having given up all worldly hopes, प्राप्ताः arrived, ताः them, विज्ञाय knowing, प्रहसिताननः with a smiling face, प्राह He spoke.

Simple meaning: Krishna, the witness of all hearts who sees everything, understood that these women had abandoned all worldly attachments and household duties just to see Him. Recognizing their singular devotion, He looked at them with a face brightened by a gentle smile and began to speak.

स्वागतं वो महाभागा आस्यतां करवाम किम् ।

यन्नो दिदृक्षया प्राप्ता उपपन्नमिदं हि वः ॥10.23.25॥

Word-by-word meaning: स्वागतम् welcome, वः to you, महाभागाः O most fortunate ladies, आस्यताम् please take your seats, करवाम shall I do, किम् what, यत् which, नः Me, दिदृक्षया with the desire to see, प्राप्ताः you have come, उपपन्नम् befitting, इदम् this, हि certainly, वः for you

Anvaya: महाभागाः! वः स्वागतम्। आस्यताम्। (वः कृते) किं करवाम यत् नः दिदृक्षया प्राप्ताः। इदं हि वः उपपन्नम्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: महाभागाः O most fortunate ones!, वः To you, स्वागतम् welcome. आस्यताम् Please sit down. किं करवाम What can I do (for you), यत् because, नः दिदृक्षया with the desire to see to Me, प्राप्ताः you have come. इदं हि this is certainly, वः उपपन्नम् befitting for you.

Simple meaning: Krishna said: "Welcome, O most fortunate ladies! Please make yourselves comfortable. What can I do for you? It is only natural and befitting for you to have come here with the simple desire to see Me, for your hearts are full of love."

नन्वद्धा मयि कुर्वन्ति कुशलाः स्वार्थदर्शनाः।

अहैतुक्यव्यवहितां भक्तिमात्मप्रिये यथा ॥10.23.26॥

Word-by-word meaning: ननु certainly, अद्धा directly/truly, मयि unto Me, कुर्वन्ति they perform, कुशलाः those who are expert or wise, स्वार्थ-दर्शनाः who perceive their own real self-interest, अहैतुकी-अव्यवहिताम् unmotivated (causeless) and uninterrupted, भक्तिम् devotional service, आत्म-प्रिये for the sake of the Soul/Beloved, यथा as

Anvaya: स्वार्थदर्शनाः कुशलाः मयि आत्मप्रिये यथा अहैतुक्यव्यवहितां भक्तिम् अद्धा कुर्वन्ति ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: स्वार्थदर्शनाः Those who see their own true interest, कुशलाः the expert and wise, मयि आत्मप्रिये unto Me, who am the most dear Soul, यथा in such a way, अहैतुक्यव्यवहितां भक्तिम् causeless and constant devotion, अद्धा directly, कुर्वन्ति they perform.

Simple meaning: Those who are truly wise and understand their own best interest perform direct devotional service unto Me. They offer this devotion—which is causeless and uninterrupted—because I am the Supreme Soul and the most dear object of love for everyone.

प्राणबुद्धिमनःस्वात्मदारापत्यधनादयः ।

यत्सम्पर्कात् प्रिया आसंस्ततः को न्वपरः प्रियः ॥10.23.27॥

Word-by-word meaning: प्राण-बुद्धि-मनः-स्व-आत्म-दार-अपत्य-धन-आदयः vital breath, intelligence, mind, relative, body, wife, children, wealth, and so on, यत्-सम्पर्कात् by whose contact, प्रियाः dear, आसन् became, ततः than one’s own self, कः who, नु indeed, अपरः other, प्रियः more dear

Anvaya: प्राणबुद्धिमनःस्वात्मदारापत्यधनादयः यत्सम्पर्कात् प्रियाः आसन् ततः अपरः कः नु प्रियः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: प्राणबुद्धिमनःस्वात्मदारापत्यधनादयः One's vital breath, intelligence, mind, relatives, body, wife, children, and wealth, यत्सम्पर्कात् because of whose connection, प्रियाः dear, आसन् became, ततः than one’s own self, अपरः other, कः who, नु indeed, प्रियः (could be more) dear.

Simple meaning: One's vital breath, intelligence, mind, body, relatives, spouse, children, and wealth are all dear only because of their connection to one’s own self. Since everything else derives its lovability from oneself, who could possibly be more dear than oneself?

तद् यात देवयजनं पतयो वो द्विजातयः ।

स्वसत्रं पारयिष्यन्ति युष्माभिर्गृहमेधिनः ॥10.23.28॥

Word-by-word meaning: तत् therefore, यात go, देव-यजनम् to the place of sacrifice, पतयः husbands, वः your, द्वि-जातयः the brahmanas, स्व-सत्रम् their own sacrifice, पारयिष्यन्ति will be able to complete, युष्माभिः with you, गृह-मेधिनः householders

Anvaya: तत् देवयजनं यात। वः द्विजातयः पतयः गृहमेधिनः युष्माभिः स्वसत्रं पारयिष्यन्ति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तत् Therefore, देवयजनं यात go to the sacrificial arena. वः Your, द्विजातयः पतयः brahmana husbands, गृहमेधिनः who are householders, युष्माभिः along with you, स्वसत्रं their own sacrifice, पारयिष्यन्ति will complete.

Simple meaning: Krishna continued: "Therefore, you should now return to the sacrificial arena. Your husbands, the brahmana householders, need your presence to successfully complete their sacrifice."

पत्न्य ऊचुः

मैवं विभोऽर्हति भवान् गदितुं नृशंसं सत्यं कुरुष्व निगमं तव पादमूलम् ।

प्राप्ता वयं तुलसिदाम पदावसृष्टं केशैर्निवोढुमतिलङ्घ्य समस्तबन्धून् ॥10.23.29॥

Word-by-word meaning: पत्न्यः ऊचुः the wives (of the brahmanas) said, मा not, एवम् like this, विभो O Almighty Lord, अर्हति should, भवान् You, गदितुम् to speak, नृशंसम् cruel words, सत्यम् true, कुरुष्व please make, निगमम् the promise of the revealed scriptures, तव Your, पादमूलम् the base of Your feet, प्राप्ताः have reached, वयम् we, तुलसि-दाम the Tulasi garland, पद-अवसृष्टम् that fall from Your feet, केशैः with our hair, निवोढुम् to carry, अतिलङ्घ्य having completely abandoned/transgressed, समस्त-बन्धून् all our relatives

Anvaya: पत्न्यः ऊचुः - विभो! भवान् एवं नृशंसं गदितुं न अर्हति। समस्तबन्धून् अतिलङ्घ्य तव पादमूलं प्राप्ताः वयं पदावसृष्टं तुलसिदाम केशैः निवोढुम् (इच्छामः)। तव निगमं सत्यं कुरुष्व ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: पत्न्यः ऊचुः The wives said, विभो O all-powerful Lord, भवान् You, एवं in this way, नृशंसं cruel (words), गदितुं न अर्हति should not speak. समस्तबन्धून् All relatives, अतिलङ्घ्य having left behind, तव पादमूलं Your the soles of Your feet, प्राप्ताः have reached, वयं we, पदावसृष्टं which has been offered at Your feet, तुलसिदाम the Tulasi garland, केशैः with our hair, निवोढुम् (इच्छामः) (wish) to carry. तव Your, निगमं scriptural promise, सत्यं कुरुष्व please make true.

Simple meaning: The wives of the brahmanas replied: "O Almighty Lord, please do not speak such cruel words to us. We have abandoned all our family ties and worldly obligations just to reach the shelter of Your lotus feet. Our only desire now is to wear upon our heads the Tulasi garland that has touched Your feet. Please, make Your own scriptural promise true—that those who surrender to You do not ever return. (न स पुनरावर्तते)"

गृह्णन्ति नो न पतयः पितरौ सुता वा न भ्रातृबन्धुसुहृदः कुत एव चान्ये ।

तस्माद् भवत्प्रपदयोः पतितात्मनां नो नान्या भवेद् गतिररिन्दम तद् विधेहि ॥10.23.30॥

Word-by-word meaning: गृह्णन्ति will accept, नः our, न not, पतयः husbands, पितरौ parents, सुताः sons, वा or, न not, भ्रातृ-बन्धु-सुहृदः brothers, relatives, and friends, कुतः how, एव certainly, च and, अन्ये others, तस्मात् therefore, भवत्-प्रपदयोः at the base of Your feet, पतित-आत्मनाम् who have surrendered our very selves, नः for us, न not, अन्या another, भवेत् may there be, गतिः destination/refuge, अरिन्दम O chastiser of enemies, तत् that, विधेहि please grant

Anvaya: नः पतयः पितरौ सुताः भ्रातृबन्धुसुहृदः वा न (अस्मान्) गृह्णन्ति, कुतः एव च अन्ये । तस्मात् अरिन्दम भवत्प्रपदयोः पतितात्मनां नः अन्या गतिः न भवेत् तत् विधेहि।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नः पतयः Our husbands, पितरौ parents, सुताः sons, भ्रातृबन्धुसुहृदः brothers, relatives, and friends, वा or, न (will) not, गृह्णन्ति accept (us), कुतः एव च अन्ये what to speak of others. तस्मात् Therefore, अरिन्दम O vanquisher of enemies!, भवत्प्रपदयोः At the base of Your feet, पतितात्मनां of us who have surrendered our souls, नः for us, अन्या गतिः न भवेत् another refuge, may not be, तत् विधेहि that (mercy) please ordain.

Simple meaning: Our husbands, parents, sons, brothers, and friends will no longer accept us back into our homes, so what can we expect from others? Therefore, O Chastiser of enemies, since we have completely surrendered ourselves at Your feet, please do not let us have any other destination but You. Please grant us Your shelter.

श्री भगवान् उवाच

पतयो नाभ्यसूयेरन् पितृभ्रातृसुतादयः ।

लोकाश्च वो मयोपेता देवा अप्यनुमन्वते ॥10.23.31॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री भगवान् उवाच the Lord said, पतयः husbands, न not, अभ्यसूयेरन् will be envious or find fault, पितृ-भ्रातृ-सुता-आदयः parents, brothers, sons, and so on, लोकाः the general public, च and, वः toward you, मया by Me, उपेताः sanctioned/guided, देवाः the demigods, अपि also, अनुमन्वते will approve

Anvaya: श्री भगवान् उवाच - मया उपेताः (यूयं) पतयः पितृभ्रातृसुतादयः च लोकाः वः न अभ्यसूयेरन् देवाः अपि अनुमन्वते।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री भगवान् उवाच Shri Bhagavan said, मया by Me, उपेताः protected/sanctioned, पतयः husbands, पितृ-भ्रातृ-सुता-आदयः parents, brothers, sons and others, च and, लोकाः the people in general, वः you, न अभ्यसूयेरन् will not find fault with, देवाः the demigods, अपि also, अनुमन्वते will support/approve.

Simple meaning: Lord Krishna said: "Rest assured, your husbands, fathers, brothers, and sons will not be displeased with you, nor will the general public criticize you. Because I have sanctioned your actions, even the demigods will look upon you with approval."

न प्रीतयेऽनुरागाय ह्यङ्गसङ्गो नृणामिह ।

तन्मनो मयि युञ्जाना अचिरान्मामवाप्स्यथ ॥10.23.32॥

Word-by-word meaning: न not, प्रीतये for the sake of love, अनुरागाय for the sake of affection, हि certainly, अङ्ग-सङ्गः physical contact, नृणाम् for human beings, इह in this world, तत् therefore, मनः the mind, मयि in Me, युञ्जानाः fixing, अचिरात् very soon, माम् Me, अवाप्स्यथ you will attain

Anvaya: इह नृणां प्रीतये अनुरागाय अङ्गसङ्गः न हि (भवति) तत् मयि मनः युञ्जानाः (यूयम्) अचिरात् माम् अवाप्स्यथ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: इह In this world, नृणां for humans, प्रीतये for divine love, अनुरागाय for deep affection, अङ्गसङ्गः physical association, न हि is certainly not (required), तत् therefore, मयि in Me, मनः युञ्जानाः fixing the mind, अचिरात् very soon, माम् अवाप्स्यथ you will attain Me.

Simple meaning: Krishna told the women: "In this world, physical proximity or bodily contact is not necessary to develop deep love and affection for Me. Therefore, return to your homes and keep your minds constantly fixed on Me. By doing so, you will very soon attain Me completely."

इत्युक्ता द्विजपत्न्यस्ता यज्ञवाटं पुनर्गताः ।

ते चानसूयवः स्वाभिः स्त्रीभिः सत्रमपारयन् ॥10.23.33॥

Word-by-word meaning: इति thus, उक्ताः addressed, द्विज-पत्न्यः the wives of the brahmanas, ताः they, यज्ञ-वाटम् to the place of sacrifice, पुनः again, गताः went, ते they (the brahmanas), च and, अनसूयवः without envy (not finding fault), स्वाभिः with their own, स्त्रीभिः wives, सत्रम् the sacrifice, अपारयन् completed

Anvaya: इति उक्ताः ताः द्विजपत्न्यः पुनः यज्ञवाटं गताः। ते च अनसूयवः स्वाभिः स्त्रीभिः सत्रम् अपारयन्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: इति उक्ताः Thus addressed (by Krishna), ताः द्विजपत्न्यः those wives of the brahmanas, पुनः again, यज्ञवाटं गताः went to the sacrificial arena. ते They (the husbands), च and, अनसूयवः being free from malice, स्वाभिः स्त्रीभिः with their own wives, सत्रम् अपारयन् finished the sacrifice.

Simple meaning: Having been addressed by Krishna in this way, the wives of the brahmanas returned to the sacrificial arena. Their husbands, who did not harbor any ill-will or envy toward them, then completed the sacrifice together with their wives.

तत्रैका विधृता भर्त्रा भगवन्तं यथाश्रुतम् ।

हृदोपगुह्य विजहौ देहं कर्मानुबन्धनम् ॥10.23.34॥

Word-by-word meaning: तत्र there (among the wives), एका one (wife), विधृता forcibly restrained, भर्त्रा by her husband, भगवन्तम् the Supreme Lord, यथा-श्रुतम् as she had heard (described), हृदा within her heart, उपगुह्य embracing, विजहौ she gave up, देहम् the body, कर्म-अनुबन्धनम् which is the result of past material activities

Anvaya: तत्र भर्त्रा विधृता एका यथाश्रुतं भगवन्तं हृदा उपगुह्य कर्मानुबन्धनं देहं विजहौ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तत्र There, भर्त्रा विधृता held back by her husband, एका one (woman), यथाश्रुतं as heard through descriptions, भगवन्तं the Supreme Lord, हृदा उपगुह्य embracing in her heart, कर्मानुबन्धनं देहं the bondage of karma, the body, विजहौ gave up.

Simple meaning: One of the wives, who had been forcibly restrained at home by her husband, was unable to go see Krishna in person. Instead, she sat down and concentrated on the Supreme Lord, embracing Him within her heart exactly as she had heard Him described. In that state of intense devotion, she gave up her material body, which was the product of her past karma, and attained liberation.

भगवानपि गोविन्दस्तेनैवान्नेन गोपकान् ।

चतुर्विधेनाशयित्वा स्वयं च बुभुजे प्रभुः ॥10.23.35॥

Word-by-word meaning: भगवान् the Supreme Lord, अपि also, गोविन्दः Govinda (Krishna), तेन with that, एव indeed, अन्नेन food, गोपकान् the cowherd boys, चतुर्विधेन of four varieties, आशयित्वा having fed, स्वयम् Himself, च and, बुभुजे ate, प्रभुः the Almighty Lord

Anvaya: प्रभुः भगवान् गोविन्दः अपि तेन एव चतुर्विधेन अन्नेन गोपकान् आशयित्वा स्वयं च बुभुजे।

Simple meaning: Then the Almighty Supreme Lord Govinda fed the cowherd boys with that very same food, which consisted of four different varieties. After they were satisfied, He Himself sat down and ate.

एवं लीलानरवपुर्नृलोकमनुशीलयन् ।

रेमे गोगोपगोपीनां रमयन् रूपवाक्कृतैः ॥10.23.36॥

Word-by-word meaning: एवम् in this way, लीला-नर-वपुः having a human-like body for His pastimes, नृ-लोकम् the world of human beings, अनुशीलयन् imitating/acting according to, रेमे He took pleasure, गो-गोप-गोपीनाम् the cows, cowherd boys, and cowherd girls, रमयन् giving pleasure (to them), रूप-वाक्-कृतैः by His beauty, His words, and His deeds

Anvaya: एवं लीलानरवपुः नृलोकम् अनुशीलयन् रूपवाक्कृतैः गोगोपगोपीनां रमयन् रेमे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: एवं Thus, लीलानरवपुः having assumed a human form for His pastimes, नृलोकम् अनुशीलयन् imitating the human world, रूपवाक्कृतैः by His beauty, words, and actions, गोगोपगोपीनां रमयन् giving delight to the cows, cowherds, and gopis, रेमे He enjoyed.

Simple meaning: In this way, the Lord, having assumed a human-like form to perform His pastimes, acted just like a person in the human world. By the enchantment of His physical beauty, His sweet words, and His extraordinary deeds, He delighted the cows, the cowherd boys, and the gopis, while He Himself enjoyed their company.

अथानुस्मृत्य विप्रास्ते अन्वतप्यन् कृतागसः ।

यद् विश्वेश्वरयोर्याच्ञामहन्म नृविडम्बयोः ॥10.23.37॥

Anvaya: अथ ते विप्राः अनुस्मृत्य कृतागसः (आत्मानं मन्यमानाः) अन्वतप्यन् यत् नृविडम्बयोः विश्वेश्वरयोः याच्ञाम् अहन्म।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अथ Then, ते विप्राः those brahmanas, अनुस्मृत्य remembering (what happened), कृतागसः having committed a sin, अन्वतप्यन् regretted, यत् that, नृविडम्बयोः of the two who were acting as humans, विश्वेश्वरयोः of the two Lords of the universe, याच्ञाम् the request, अहन्म we struck down (rejected).

Simple meaning: Afterwards, when the brahmanas reflected on what had occurred, they were filled with deep remorse. Realizing they had committed a great offense, they lamented: "We were so foolish that we rejected the simple request of the two Lords of the universe, who were playing the roles of ordinary human beings."

दृष्ट्वा स्त्रीणां भगवति कृष्णे भक्तिमलौकिकीम् ।

आत्मानं च तया हीनमनुतप्ता व्यगर्हयन् ॥10.23.38॥

Word-by-word meaning: दृष्ट्वा seeing, स्त्रीणाम् of the women, भगवति for the Supreme Lord, कृष्णे Krishna, भक्तिम् the devotion, अलौकिकीम् extraordinary (uncommon), आत्मानम् themselves, च and, तया of that (devotion), हीनम् devoid, अनुतप्ताः full of remorse, व्यगर्हयन् they began to condemn

Anvaya: स्त्रीणां भगवति कृष्णे अलौकिकीं भक्तिं दृष्ट्वा तया आत्मानं हीनं च (दृष्ट्वा) अनुतप्ताः व्यगर्हयन्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: स्त्रीणां Of the women, भगवति कृष्णे for the Supreme Lord Krishna, अलौकिकीं भक्तिं extraordinary devotion, दृष्ट्वा seeing, तया आत्मानं हीनं च and, devoid of that (bhakti) themselves, अनुतप्ताः being repentant, व्यगर्हयन् they condemned.

Simple meaning: Seeing the extraordinary and transcendental devotion their wives had for the Supreme Lord Krishna, and recognizing that they themselves were completely devoid of such devotion, the brahmanas felt great remorse and they condemned their own character and conduct.

धिग् जन्म नस्त्रिवृद्विद्यां धिग् व्रतं धिग् बहुज्ञताम् ।

धिक् कुलं धिक् क्रियादाक्ष्यं विमुखा ये त्वधोक्षजे ॥10.23.39॥

Word-by-word meaning: धिक् condemnation, जन्म birth (as a brahmana), नः our, त्रिवृत्-विद्याम् the three-fold Vedic knowledge, धिक् condemnation, व्रतम् our vows, धिक् condemnation, बहुज्ञताम् extensive learning, धिक् condemnation, कुलम् family lineage, धिक् condemnation, क्रिया-दाक्ष्यम् expertise in rituals, विमुखाः averse, ये who, तु indeed, अधोक्षजे toward the Transcendental Lord (Adhokshaja)

Anvaya: नः जन्म धिक्, त्रिवृद्विद्यां धिक्, व्रतं धिक्, बहुज्ञतां धिक्, कुलं धिक्, क्रियादाक्ष्यं धिक्, ये तु अधोक्षजे विमुखाः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नः जन्म धिक् Our birth is to be condemned, त्रिवृद्विद्यां धिक् the three Vedas is to be condemned, व्रतं धिक् vows are to be condemned, बहुज्ञतां धिक् vast knowledge is to be condemned, कुलं धिक् lineage is to be condemned, क्रियादाक्ष्यं धिक् skill in performance is to be condemned, ये तु those who, indeed, अधोक्षजे विमुखाः toward the Lord beyond the senses are indifferent.

Simple meaning: The brahmanas said: "To hell with our three-fold brahminical birth! To hell with our Vedic learning, our strict vows, and our vast knowledge! To hell with our noble lineage and our expertise in ritual performance! All of these are completely useless because we have remained indifferent toward the Transcendental Lord, who is beyond the reach of material senses."

नूनं भगवतो माया योगिनामपि मोहिनी ।

यद् वयं गुरवो नृणां स्वार्थे मुह्यामहे द्विजाः ॥10.23.40॥

Word-by-word meaning: नूनम् surely, भगवतः of the Supreme Lord, माया the illusory energy, योगिनाम् for great yogis, अपि even, मोहिनी bewildering, यत् because, वयम् we, गुरवः the spiritual masters, नृणाम् of men, स्वार्थे regarding our own self-interest, मुह्यामहे are bewildered, द्विजाः brahmanas

Anvaya: नूनं भगवतः माया योगिनाम् अपि मोहिनी यत् नृणां गुरवः वयं द्विजाः स्वार्थे मुह्यामहे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नूनं Certainly, भगवतः माया the illusory energy of the Supreme Lord, योगिनाम् अपि even for yogis, मोहिनी is bewildering, यत् because, नृणां  गुरवः the teachers of people, वयं द्विजाः we brahmanas, स्वार्थे in our own interest, मुह्यामहे are confused.

Simple meaning: The brahmanas said: "Surely the illusory energy of the Supreme Lord is so powerful that it bewilders even great yogis. Just see how we, who are supposed to be the spiritual teachers of all humanity, have become utterly confused about our own true self-interest."

अहो पश्यत नारीणामपि कृष्णे जगद्गुरौ ।

दुरन्तभावं योऽविध्यन्मृत्युपाशान् गृहाभिधान् ॥10.23.41॥

Word-by-word meaning: अहो Oh, पश्यत just see, नारीणाम् of these women, अपि even, कृष्णे in Krishna, जगत्-गुरौ the spiritual master of the universe, दुरन्त-भावम् the limitlessly deep devotion, यः which, अविध्यत् has severed, मृत्यु-पाशान् the shackles of death, गृह-अभिधान् known as "home" (domestic life)

Anvaya: अहो! जगद्गुरौ कृष्णे नारीणाम् अपि दुरन्तभावं पश्यत यः गृहाभिधान् मृत्युपाशान् अविध्यत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अहो Oh, जगद्गुरौ कृष्णे toward Krishna, the teacher of the universe, नारीणाम् of the women, अपि even, दुरन्तभावं the endless/unfathomable devotion, पश्यत look, यः which (devotion), गृहाभिधान् मृत्युपाशान् the ropes of death called by the name of 'household', अविध्यत् has cut asunder.

Simple meaning: The brahmanas continued: "Oh, just look at the unfathomable devotion these women have for Krishna, the teacher of the entire universe! It is this very devotion that has completely cut through the shackles of death known as 'domestic life'—fetters that usually bind a soul to the material world."

नासां द्विजातिसंस्कारो न निवासो गुरावपि ।

न तपो नात्ममीमांसा न शौचं न क्रियाः शुभाः ॥10.23.42॥

Word-by-word meaning: न not, आसाम् for these women, द्विजाति-संस्कारः the purificatory rites of the twice-born (upanayana), न not, निवासः residence, गुरौ in the home of a spiritual master, अपि even, न not, तपः austerities, न not, आत्म-मीमांसा philosophical inquiry into the self, न not, शौचम् (ritual) cleanliness, न not, क्रियाः rituals, शुभाः auspicious

Anvaya: आसां न द्विजातिसंस्कारः न गुरौ निवासः अपि न तपः न आत्ममीमांसा न शौचं न शुभाः क्रियाः (सन्ति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: आसां For these (women), न द्विजातिसंस्कारः there was no brahminical initiation, न गुरौ निवासः no residence with a guru, न अपि तपः not even penance, न आत्ममीमांसा no analysis of the soul, न शौचं no cleanliness, न शुभाः क्रियाः no auspicious rituals.

Simple meaning: The brahmanas marveled: "These women have not undergone the purificatory rites of the twice-born, they never lived as students in the house of a guru, they have practiced no great austerities, nor have they engaged in philosophical inquiry into the nature of the self. They are not even experts in ritual cleanliness or auspicious Vedic ceremonies."

Note: This verse highlights the contrast between the external "qualifications" of the brahmanas (which failed them) and the pure, spontaneous love of the women (which saved them).

अथापि ह्युत्तमश्लोके कृष्णे योगेश्वरे ।

भक्तिर्दृढा न चास्माकं संस्कारादिमतामपि ॥10.23.43॥

Word-by-word meaning: अथ अपि nevertheless, हि certainly, उत्तमश्लोके unto the Lord who is praised by choice poetry, कृष्णे Lord Krishna, योग-ईश्वरे the master of all mystic power, भक्तिः devotional service, दृढा firm, न not, च and, अस्माकम् our, संस्कार-आदि-मताम् who possess all purificatory rites and qualifications, अपि although

Anvaya: अथ अपि संस्कारादिमताम् अपि अस्माकं योगेश्वरे उत्तमश्लोके कृष्णे दृढा भक्तिः न (अस्ति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अथ अपि And yet, संस्कारादिमताम् who possess purificatory rites and so on, अपि although, अस्माकं for us, योगेश्वरे उत्तमश्लोके the master of yoga who is praised by sublime verses, कृष्णे toward Krishna, दृढा भक्तिः firm devotion, न is not (present).

Simple meaning: The brahmanas continued their self-reflection: "In spite of all these things—even though we have received all the purificatory initiations, studied the Vedas, and performed rituals—we do not possess firm devotion for Krishna, the Master of all mystic power and the subject of the most sublime prayers. Yet these women, without any of our 'qualifications,' are completely absorbed in Him."

Contextual Note: This concludes their realization that spiritual success is measured by devotion (bhakti), not by social status, ritual expertise, or intellectual study.

ननु स्वार्थविमूढानां प्रमत्तानां गृहेहया ।

अहो नः स्मारयामास गोपवाक्यैः सतां गतिः ॥10.23.44॥

Word-by-word meaning: ननु certainly, स्वार्थ-विमूढानाम् of those who are bewildered about their own self-interest, प्रमत्तानाम् who are obsessed/mad, गृह-ईहया with household endeavors, अहो oh, नः us, स्मारयामास He has reminded, गोप-वाक्यैः by the words of the cowherd boys, सताम् of the devotees (or the virtuous), गतिः the ultimate goal/refuge

Anvaya: अहो! गृहेहया प्रमत्तानां स्वार्थविमूढानां नः सतां गतिः (सः) गोपवाक्यैः ननु स्मारयामास।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अहो Oh!, गृहेहया By household chores/desires, प्रमत्तानां of those intoxicated/distracted, स्वार्थविमूढानां of those confused about their true goal, नः us, सतां गतिः the refuge of the saintly, गोपवाक्यैः through the words of the cowherd boys, ननु स्मारयामास has certainly reminded (of Himself).

Simple meaning: The brahmanas said: "Oh, just see! We were so intoxicated by our domestic duties and so bewildered about our own true self-interest, yet the Supreme Lord, who is the ultimate goal of the saintly, has kindly reminded us of His existence through the simple words of the cowherd boys."

Key Insight: This verse marks the brahmanas' realization that Krishna's request for food was not an act of a hungry child, but a merciful intervention intended to wake them up from their spiritual slumber. They acknowledge that despite their "superior" status, they needed the cowherd boys to act as messengers of the Divine.

अन्यथा पूर्णकामस्य कैवल्याद्याशिषां पतेः ।

ईशितव्यैः किमस्माभिरीशस्यैतद् विडम्बनम् ॥10.23.45॥

Word-by-word meaning: अन्यथा otherwise, पूर्ण-कामस्य of Him whose every desire is already fulfilled, कैवल्य-आदि-आशिषाम् of all blessings, beginning with liberation, पतेः of the Master, ईशितव्यैः by those who are meant to be ruled (subordinates), किम् what (need), अस्माभिः with us, ईशस्य of the Supreme Controller, एतत् this, विडम्बनम् an imitation (a pretense)

Anvaya: अन्यथा पूर्णकामस्य कैवल्याद्याशिषां पतेः ईशस्य ईशितव्यैः अस्माभिः किम्? एतत् (तु तस्य) विडम्बनम् (एव)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अन्यथा Otherwise, पूर्णकामस्य of the one whose desires are perfectly satisfied, कैवल्याद्याशिषां पतेः of the Master of all blessings including liberation, ईशस्य of the Supreme Lord, ईशितव्यैः with those to be ruled, अस्माभिः with us, किम् what (business/need could there be)? एतत् This (begging for food), विडम्बनम् is a pretense/play.

Simple meaning: The brahmanas continued: "Otherwise, why would the Supreme Lord—whose every desire is eternally fulfilled and who is the master of all benedictions, including liberation—have any need for anything from us, His lowly subjects? His acting as if He were hungry and asking us for food was simply a pretense, a part of His divine play."

Key Insight: The brahmanas now recognize the "begging" of Krishna as Lila (divine play). They realize that the Absolute Truth, being purna-kama (self-satisfied), doesn't "need" their ritual offerings, but gives them an opportunity to serve out of His own causeless mercy.

हित्वान्यान् भजते यं श्रीः पादस्पर्शाशया सकृत् ।

आत्मदोषापवर्गेण तद्याच्ञा जनमोहिनी ॥10.23.46॥

Word-by-word meaning: हित्वा abandoning, अन्यान् others (demigods like Brahma), भजते serves/worships, यम् whom, श्रीः the Goddess of Fortune (Lakshmi), पाद-स्पर्श-आशया with the desire to touch His feet, सकृत् even once, आत्म-दोष-अपवर्गेण by setting aside her own "fault" (her legendary fickleness/instability), तत्-याच्ञा His begging, जन-मोहिनी bewildering to the people

Anvaya: श्रीः आत्मदोषापवर्गेण अन्यान् हित्वा यं सकृत् पादस्पर्शाशया भजते, तद्याच्ञा जनमोहिनी (एव)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्रीः Goddess Lakshmi, आत्मदोषापवर्गेण by casting aside her habit of being restless, अन्यान् हित्वा leaving others, यं Whom, सकृत् पादस्पर्शाशया with the hope of touching His feet even once, भजते worships, तद्याच्ञा His (Krishna's) begging, जनमोहिनी is only to bewilder the common people.

Simple meaning: The brahmanas realized: "Even Goddess Lakshmi, for whose favor everyone else strives, abandons all others and casts aside her own fickle nature just to serve Him, desiring only to touch His lotus feet. When that same Supreme Lord comes to us to beg for food, it is clearly a divine play intended to bewilder the world and test our devotion."

Key Insight: This verse emphasizes the irony of the situation. The brahmanas are struck by the fact that the person who owns everything (being the husband of the Goddess of Fortune) appeared before them as a hungry boy. They recognize that His "need" was actually an invitation for them to participate in His pastimes.

देशः कालः पृथग्द्रव्यं मन्त्रतन्त्रर्त्विजोऽग्नयः ।

देवता यजमानश्च क्रतुर्धर्मश्च यन्मयः ॥10.23.47॥

Word-by-word meaning: देशः the place, कालः the time, पृथग्-द्रव्यम् the various physical ingredients (of sacrifice), मन्त्र-तन्त्र-ऋत्विजः the Vedic hymns, the ritual procedures, the officiating priests, अग्नयः the sacrificial fires, देवता the presiding deities, यजमानः the performer of the sacrifice (the patron), च and, क्रतुः the sacrifice itself, धर्मः the principles of religion, च and, यत्-मयः who is the embodiment of all these

Anvaya: देशः कालः पृथग्द्रव्यं मन्त्रतन्त्रर्त्विजः अग्नयः देवता यजमानः च क्रतुः धर्मः च यन्मयः (सः एव अयं श्रीकृष्णः)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: देशः The place, कालः time, पृथग्द्रव्यं various ingredients, मन्त्रतन्त्र-ऋत्विजः अग्नयः the mantras, rituals, priests, and fires, देवता the demigod, यजमानः च and the patron, क्रतुः the sacrifice, धर्मः च and the religious duty, यन्मयः consist of Him (are His manifestations).

Simple meaning: The brahmanas finally understood: "The place, the time, the ritual items, the mantras, the procedures, the priests, the fires, the deities, the patron, the sacrifice itself, and the very principles of religion—all of these are simply manifestations of Him (Krishna). He is the embodiment of all that we were trying to achieve through our rituals."

Key Insight: This is a pinnacle of realization in the chapter. The brahmanas recognize that their ritualistic life was focused on the "parts," but they had ignored the "Whole." They acknowledge that Krishna is not just a person asking for food, but the very substance and goal of the Vedic yajna they were performing.

स एष भगवान् साक्षाद् विष्णुर्योगेश्वरेश्वरः ।

जातो यदुष्वित्यशृण्म ह्यपि मूढा न विद्महे ॥10.23.48॥

Word-by-word meaning: सः he, एषः this, भगवान् the Supreme God, साक्षात् directly, विष्णुः Lord Vishnu, योग-ईश्वर-ईश्वरः the Lord of all masters of yoga, जातः born, यदुषु among the Yadus, इति thus, अशृण्म we heard, हि certainly, अपि although, मूढाः fools, न not, विद्महे we understood

Anvaya: योगेश्वरेश्वरः सः एषः साक्षात् भगवान् विष्णुः यदुषु जातः इति (वयं) अशृण्म हि, अपि मूढाः (वयं तं) न विद्महे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: योगेश्वरेश्वरः The Lord of all lords of yoga, सः एषः this very person, साक्षात् भगवान् विष्णुः is the Supreme Lord Vishnu Himself, यदुषु जातः born in the Yadu dynasty, इति thus, अशृण्म हि we had certainly heard, अपि yet, मूढाः न विद्महे being foolish, we did not recognize/understand Him.

Simple meaning: The brahmanas confessed: "This very Krishna is directly the Supreme God, Lord Vishnu, the master of all masters of yoga. We had indeed heard that He had taken birth in the Yadu dynasty, yet we were so foolish and blinded by our rituals that we failed to recognize Him when He stood before us."

Key Insight: This verse expresses the regret of the "learned" who have theoretical knowledge (shrutam) but lack realized vision (vijnana). They admit that while they knew the prophecies and the scriptures, their pride and material absorption prevented them from applying that knowledge to the living reality of the Lord's presence.

अहो वयं धन्यतमा येषां नस्तादृशीः स्त्रियः ।

भक्त्या यासां मतिर्जाता अस्माकं निश्चला हरौ ॥10.23.49॥

Word-by-word meaning: अहो oh, वयम् we, धन्य-तमाः most fortunate, येषाम् of whom, नः our, तादृशीः such (extraordinary), स्त्रियः wives, भक्त्या by the devotion, यासाम् of whom, मतिः the intelligence/consciousness, जाता has become, अस्माकम् our, निश्चला fixed/unshakable, हरौ in Lord Hari

Anvaya: अहो! वयं धन्यतमाः येषां नः तादृशीः स्त्रियः (सन्ति), यासां भक्त्या अस्माकं हरौ निश्चला मतिः जाता।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अहो Oh!, वयं धन्यतमाः we are most fortunate, येषां whose, नः our, तादृशीः स्त्रियः such wives (exist), यासां भक्त्या by whose devotion, अस्माकं our, हरौ toward Lord Hari, निश्चला मतिः जाता steady mind/intelligence has been born (become).

Simple meaning: The brahmanas exclaimed: "Oh, we are truly the most fortunate of men! We have such wonderful wives, for it is only because of their incredible devotion that our own minds have finally become fixed and steady in the service of Lord Hari."

Key Insight: This verse represents a beautiful moment of humility and transformation. The brahmanas, who initially looked down upon their wives as unlearned or "just women," now honor them as their spiritual guides. They recognize that while their own rituals failed to grant them God-realization, the pure love of their wives acted as a catalyst for their own spiritual awakening.

नमस्तुभ्यं भगवते कृष्णायाकुण्ठमेधसे ।

यन्मायामोहितधियो भ्रमामः कर्मवर्त्मसु ॥10.23.50॥

Word-by-word meaning: नमः obeisances, तुभ्यम् unto You, भगवते the Supreme Personality of Godhead, कृष्णाय unto Krishna, अकुण्ठ-मेधसे whose intelligence/knowledge is never limited or frustrated, यत्-माया-मोहित-धियः whose illusory energy has bewildered our intelligence, भ्रमामः we are wandering, कर्म-वर्त्मसु on the paths of fruitive ritualistic activities

Anvaya: अकुण्ठमेधसे भगवते कृष्णाय तुभ्यं नमः, यन्मायामोहितधियः (वयं) कर्मवर्त्मसु भ्रमामः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अकुण्ठमेधसे To the one of unfailing intelligence, भगवते कृष्णाय to the Supreme Lord Krishna, तुभ्यं to You, नमः (our) obeisances, यन्मायामोहितधियः we whose minds are bewildered by Your Maya, कर्मवर्त्मसु in the paths of (ritualistic) actions, भ्रमामः are wandering.

Simple meaning: The brahmanas offered their final prayer: "We offer our respectful obeisances unto You, O Lord Krishna, the Supreme God, whose divine intelligence is never obstructed. We have been wandering aimlessly along the paths of ritualistic duties, our vision clouded by Your illusory energy."

Key Insight: This verse serves as the formal surrender of the brahmanas. They contrast their own "bewildered intelligence" (mohita-dhiyah) with Krishna’s "unfailing intelligence" (akuntha-medhase). By admitting they were lost in the "paths of karma," they acknowledge that rituals without devotion are merely a maze. This prayer marks their transition from dry ritualism to the path of pure Bhakti.

स वै न आद्यः पुरुषः स्वमायामोहितात्मनाम् ।

अविज्ञातानुभावानां क्षन्तुमर्हत्यतिक्रमम् ॥10.23.51॥

Word-by-word meaning: सः He, वै certainly, नः our, आद्यः the original, पुरुषः Supreme Person, स्व-माया-मोहित-आत्मनाम् whose minds are bewildered by His own illusory energy, अविज्ञात-अनुभावानाम् who are ignorant of His (divine) influence/glory, क्षन्तुम् अर्हति ought to forgive, अतिक्रमम् the offense/transgression

Anvaya: स्वमायामोहितात्मनाम् अविज्ञातानुभावानां नः सः आद्यः पुरुषः वै अतिक्रमं क्षन्तुम् अर्हति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: स्वमायामोहितात्मनाम् Of those whose souls are bewildered by His Maya, अविज्ञातानुभावानाम् of those unaware of His greatness, नः our, सः He, आद्यः पुरुषः the Original Person, वै indeed, अतिक्रमम् the transgression, क्षन्तुम् अर्हति should forgive.

Simple meaning: The brahmanas concluded their prayer by saying: "Since we are bewildered by His illusory energy and were completely ignorant of His divine glories, we pray that the Original Supreme Person may kindly forgive us for the offense we have committed against Him."

This verse concludes the realization of the sacrificial brahmanas. It highlights a few key spiritual themes:

Interestingly, despite their realization, the brahmanas did not go to see Krishna in person at that moment because they were afraid of Kamsa's wrath—showing that while their hearts had changed, they still possessed a lingering fear of the material world. Krishna, however, accepted their mental surrender.

इति स्वाघमनुस्मृत्य कृष्णे ते कृतहेलनाः ।

दिदृक्षवोऽप्यच्युतयोः कंसाद् भीता न चाचलन् ॥10/23.52॥

Word-by-word meaning: इति thus, स्व-अघम् their own sin/offense, अनुस्मृत्य reflecting upon, कृष्णे toward Krishna, ते they (the brahmanas), कृत-हेलनाः who had committed neglect/disrespect, दिदृक्षवः desiring to see, अपि although, अच्युतयोः the two infallible ones (Krishna and Balarama), कंसात् from Kamsa, भीताः afraid, न not, च and, अचलन् they moved (toward Him)

Anvaya: इति कृष्णे कृतहेलनाः ते स्वाघम् अनुस्मृत्य अच्युतयोः दिदृक्षवः अपि कंसात् भीताः न च अचलन्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: इति In this way, कृष्णे कृतहेलनाः having committed disrespect toward Krishna, ते they, स्वाघम् अनुस्मृत्य remembering their own offense, अच्युतयोः the two infallible Lords, दिदृक्षवः अपि although desiring to see, कंसात् भीताः being afraid of Kamsa, न च अचलन् did not move (did not go to Vrindavan).

Simple meaning: Having reflected on the great offense they committed by disrespecting Lord Krishna, the brahmanas were filled with a deep desire to go and see the two infallible brothers, Krishna and Balarama. However, because they were terrified of King Kamsa and the potential consequences, they did not actually leave to meet Him.

Deep Insight and Context

This verse concludes the narrative with a poignant look at the struggle between awakening and fear.
