श्रीमद्भागवतमहापुराणम्

दशमः स्कन्धः

गर्भगतविष्णोः ब्रह्मादिकृतस्तुतिः नाम द्वितीयः अध्यायः

श्री शुक उवाच

प्रलम्बबकचाणूरतृणावर्तमहाशनैः।

मुष्टिकारिष्टद्विविदपूतनाकेशिधेनुकैः ॥10.2.1॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्रीशुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, प्रलम्ब-बक-चाणूर-तृणावर्त-महाशनैः with Pralamba, Baka, Chanoora, Trinavarta, Mahashana, मुष्टिक-अरिष्ट-द्विविद-पूतना-केशि-धेनुकैः with Mushtika, Arishta, Dvivida, Pootana, Keshi, Dhenuka

Anvaya: श्रीशुकः उवाच — प्रलम्बबकचाणूरतृणावर्तमहाशनैः मुष्टिकारिष्टद्विविदपूतनाकेशिधेनुकैः…

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्रीशुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said — प्रलम्बबकचाणूरतृणावर्तमहाशनैः Along with Pralamba, Baka, Chanoora, Trinavarta, Mahashana (Aghasura), मुष्टिकारिष्टद्विविदपूतनाकेशिधेनुकैः with Mushtika, Arishta, Dvivida, Pootana, Keshi, Dhenuka…

Simple meaning: Sri Shuka said, along with  Pralamba, Baka, Chanoora, Trinavarta, Mahashana (Aghasura), Mushtika, Arishta, Dvivida, Pootana, Keshi, and Dhenuka…

अन्यैश्चासुरभूपालैर्बाणभौमादिभिर्युतः ।

यदूनां कदनं चक्रे बली मागधसंश्रयः ॥10.2.2॥

Word-by-word meaning: अन्यैः च​ and by other, असुर-भूपालैः asura kings, बाण-भौम-आदिभिः by Bana, Bhauma (Narakasura) and others, युतः accompanied, यदूनाम् of the Yadus, कदनम् destruction/oppression, चक्रे he made, बली strong one (Kamsa), मागध-संश्रयः taking refuge with Jarasandha (king of Magadha)

Anvaya: बली मागधसंश्रयः (सन्) अन्यैः च​ असुरभूपालैः बाणभौमादिभिः युतः यदूनां कदनं चक्रे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: बली The mighty (Kamsa), मागधसंश्रयः (सन्) being helped by the king of Magadha, अन्यैः च​ असुरभूपालैः and with the other asura kings like, बाणभौमादिभिः युतः accompanied by Bana, Bhauma and others, यदूनां कदनं चक्रे carried out the oppression of the Yadus.

Simple meaning: The mighty Kamsa taking the help of the King of Magadha - Jarasandha, supported by other asura kings such as Bana and Bhauma, repeatedly attacked and harassed the Yadus.

ते पीडिता निविविशुः कुरुपाञ्चालकेकयान् ।

शाल्यवान् विदर्भान् निषधान् विदेहान् कोसलानपि ॥10.2.3॥

एके तमनुरुन्धाना ज्ञातयः पर्युपासते ।

Word-by-word meaning: ते they, पीडिताः oppressed, निविविशुः entered, कुरु-पाञ्चाल-केकयान् the lands of Kurus, Panchalas, Kekayas, शाल्यवान् Shalvas, विदर्भान् Vidarbhas, निषधान् Nishadhas, विदेहान् Videhas, कोसलान् Kosalas, अपि even, एके some, तम् him (Kamsa), अनुरुन्धानाः supporting/following, ज्ञातयः relatives, पर्युपासते attend/serve

Anvaya: ते पीडिताः कुरुपाञ्चालकेकयान् शाल्यवान् विदर्भान् निषधान् विदेहान् कोसलान् निविविशुः। एके ज्ञातयः तम् अनुरुन्धानाः पर्युपासते अपि।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: ते पीडिताः Oppressed, they, कुरुपाञ्चालकेकयान् (the territories of) Kurus, Panchalas, Kekayas, शाल्यवान् विदर्भान् निषधान् विदेहान् कोसलान् and also Shalyas, Vidharbas, Nishadhas, Videhas and Kosalas, निविविशुः entered. एके ज्ञातयः Some of his relatives, तम् अनुरुन्धानाः favoring him (Kamsa), पर्युपासते अपि even served.

Simple meaning: Harassed by Kamsa, Jarasandha and the asura kings, the Yadus fled and took refuge in various kingdoms—Kuru, Panchala, Kekaya, Shalva, Vidarbha, Nishadha, Videha, and Kosala. Some of Kamsa’s own relatives even supported and served him.

हतेषु षट्सु बालेषु देवक्या औग्रसेनिना ॥10.2.4॥

सप्तमो वैष्णवं धाम यमनन्तं प्रचक्षते ।

गर्भो बभूव देवक्या हर्षशोकविवर्धनः ॥10.2.5॥

Word-by-word meaning: हतेषु having been killed, षट्सु six, बालेषु infants, देवक्याः of Devaki, औग्रसेनिना by Ugrasena’s son (Kamsa), सप्तमः seventh, वैष्णवम् belonging to Vishnu, धाम part, यम् whom, अनन्तम् Ananta (Sesha), प्रचक्षते they speak of, गर्भः embryo/child, बभूव became, देवक्याः of Devaki, हर्ष-शोक-विवर्धनः increasing both joy and sorrow

Anvaya: देवक्याः षट्सु बालेषु औग्रसेनिना हतेषु, वैष्णवं धाम यम् अनन्तं प्रचक्षते, देवक्याः सप्तमः गर्भः हर्षशोकविवर्धनः बभूव।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: देवक्याः षट्सु बालेषु After the six infants born to Devaki, औग्रसेनिना हतेषु were killed by Augraseni (Ugrasena’s Son - Kamsa), वैष्णवं धाम the divine part of Vishnu, यम् अनन्तं प्रचक्षते whom they say is Ananta (Adishesha), देवक्याः Devaki’s, सप्तमः गर्भः seventh foetus, हर्षशोकविवर्धनः बभूव became the object of both joy and sorrow.

Simple meaning: After he cruelly killed the six newborn sons of Devaki, she conceived her seventh child, who was none other than Ananta Sesha, the divine expansion of Vishnu. His arrival filled her heart with a mix of joy and fear—joy at carrying a divine being, and sorrow because Kamsa would kill her this son also.

भगवानपि विश्वात्मा विदित्वा कंसजं भयम् ।

यदूनां निजनाथानां योगमायां समादिशत् ॥10.2.6॥

Word-by-word meaning: भगवान् the Supreme Lord, अपि also, विश्व-आत्मा the soul of the universe, विदित्वा knowing, कंसजम् arising from Kamsa, भयम् fear, यदूनाम् of the Yadus, निज-नाथानाम् of those who had Himself as the protector, योग-मायाम् Yogamaya, समादिशत् instructed

निजनाथानाम् - निजः नाथः येषां ते - बहुव्रीहिः

Anvaya: विश्वात्मा भगवान् अपि निजनाथानां यदूनां कंसजं भयं विदित्वा योगमायां समादिशत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: विश्वात्मा भगवान् अपि The Supreme Lord, who pervades the entire universe, निजनाथानां यदूनां of the Yadus who had Himself as their protector, कंसजं भयं विदित्वा understanding the fear born from Kamsa, योगमायां समादिशत् instructed Yogmaya.

Simple meaning: Understanding that Kamsa posed a grave threat to the Yadus who had Himself as their protector, the Supreme Lord, who pervades the entire universe, directed His Yogamaya to act.

गच्छ देवि व्रजं भद्रे गोपगोभिरलङ्कृतम् ।

रोहिणी वसुदेवस्य भार्याऽऽस्ते नन्दगोकुले ।

अन्याश्च कंससंविग्ना विवरेषु वसन्ति हि ॥10.2.7॥

Word-by-word meaning: गच्छ go, देवि O goddess, व्रजम् to Vraja, भद्रे O auspicious one, गोप-गोभिः by cowherds and cows, अलङ्कृतम् adorned, रोहिणी Rohini, वसुदेवस्य of Vasudeva, भार्या wife, आस्ते resides, नन्द-गोकुले in the cowherd settlement of Nanda, अन्याः others, च also, कंस-संविग्नाः frightened of Kamsa, विवरेषु in secret places, वसन्ति live, हि indeed

Anvaya: देवि! भद्रे! गोपगोभिः अलङ्कृतं व्रजं गच्छ। वसुदेवस्य भार्या रोहिणी नन्दगोकुले आस्ते। अन्याः च कंससंविग्नाः विवरेषु वसन्ति हि।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: देवि! भद्रे! O divine and auspicious lady! गोपगोभिः अलङ्कृतं व्रजं Vraja which is adorned by cowherds and cows, गच्छ go. वसुदेवस्य भार्या Vasudeva's wife, रोहिणी Rohini, नन्दगोकुले आस्ते lives in Nanda’s settlement. अन्याः च Others also, कंससंविग्नाः frightened by Kamsa, विवरेषु in secret places, वसन्ति हि live indeed.

Simple meaning: The Lord said: “O divine and auspicious lady! Go to Vraja, which is adorned by cowherds and cows. There lives Rohini, the wife of Vasudeva, in Nanda’s cowherd settlement. Others also, frightened by Kamsa, live there in secret places.

देवक्या जठरे गर्भं शेषाख्यं धाम मामकम् ।

तत् संनिकृष्य रोहिण्या उदरे संनिवेशय ॥10.2.8॥

Word-by-word meaning: देवक्याः from Devaki, जठरे in the womb, गर्भम् the embryo, शेष-आख्यम् named Shesha, धाम part, मामकम् belonging to Me, तत् that, संनिकृष्य drawing away, रोहिण्याः of Rohini, उदरे into the womb, संनिवेशय place

Anvaya: देवक्याः जठरे शेषाख्यं मामकं धाम तत् गर्भं संनिकृष्य रोहिण्याः उदरे संनिवेशय।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: देवक्याः जठरे From the womb of Devaki, शेषाख्यं मामकं धाम my part manifestation named Shesha, तत् गर्भं that foetus, संनिकृष्य drawing away, रोहिण्याः उदरे संनिवेशय in Rohini’s womb, place.

Simple meaning: “Transfer my part manifestation named Shesha from Devaki’s womb into the womb of Rohini.

अथाहमंशभागेन देवक्याः पुत्रतां शुभे ।

प्राप्स्यामि त्वं यशोदायां नन्दपत्न्यां भविष्यसि ॥10.2.9॥

Word-by-word meaning: अथ Later, अहम् I, अंश-भागेन by a portion of Myself, देवक्याः of Devaki, पुत्रताम् (literally) son-ship/status of son, शुभे O auspicious one, प्राप्स्यामि will obtain, त्वम् you, यशोदायाम् in (the womb of) Yashoda, नन्द-पत्न्याम् the wife of Nanda, भविष्यसि will become

Anvaya: अथ अहम् अंशभागेन देवक्याः पुत्रतां प्राप्स्यामि। शुभे! त्वं नन्दपत्न्यां यशोदायां भविष्यसि।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अथ Later, अहम् अंशभागेन I, with a part of myself, देवक्याः पुत्रतां प्राप्स्यामि will attain the status of Devaki’s son. शुभे! O auspicious one! त्वं You, नन्दपत्न्यां यशोदायां भविष्यसि will be born in (the womb of) the wife of Nanda, Yashoda.

Simple meaning: “Later, I will be born as Devaki’s son through a manifestation of my divine power. O auspicious one! You will be born as the daughter of Yashoda, Nanda’s wife.

अर्चिष्यन्ति मनुष्यास्त्वां सर्वकामवरेश्वरीम् ।

धूपोपहारबलिभिः सर्वकामवरप्रदाम् ॥10.2.10॥

Word-by-word meaning: अर्चिष्यन्ति will worship, मनुष्याः human beings, त्वाम् you, सर्व-काम-वर-ईश्वरीम् the supreme goddess who grants all boons, धूप-उपहार-बलिभिः with incense, offerings and sacrifices, सर्व-काम-वर-प्रदाम् the giver of all desired boons

Anvaya: मनुष्याः सर्वकामवरेश्वरीं सर्वकामवरप्रदां त्वां धूपोपहारबलिभिः अर्चिष्यन्ति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: मनुष्याः Human beings, सर्वकामवरेश्वरीं the supreme goddess who grants all boons, सर्वकामवरप्रदां the giver of all desired boons, त्वां you, धूपोपहारबलिभिः अर्चिष्यन्ति will worship with incense, offerings and sacrifices.

Simple meaning: “Human beings will worship you, the supreme goddess who grants all boons, the giver of every desired blessing with incense, offerings, and sacrificial oblations.

नामधेयानि कुर्वन्ति स्थानानि च नरा भुवि ।

दुर्गेति भद्रकालीति विजया वैष्णवीति च ॥10.2.11॥

कुमुदा चण्डिका कृष्णा माधवी कन्यकेति च ।

माया नारायणीशानी शारदेत्यम्बिकेति च ॥10.2.12॥

Word-by-word meaning: नाम-धेयानि names, कुर्वन्ति will make, स्थानानि sacred places, च and, नराः people, भुवि on earth, दुर्गा इति as Durga, भद्रकाली इति as Bhadrakali, विजया as Vijaya, वैष्णवी इति as Vaishnavi, च and, कुमुदा Kumuda, च and, चण्डिका Chandika, कृष्णा Krishna, माधवी Madhavi, कन्यका Kanyaka, इति thus, च and, माया Maya, नारायणी Narayani, ईशानी Ishani, शारदा Sharada, इति thus, अम्बिका Ambika, इति thus, च and

Anvaya: नराः भुवि दुर्गा इति, भद्रकाली इति, विजया वैष्णवी इति च, कुमुदा चण्डिका कृष्णा माधवी कन्यका माया नारायणी ईशानी शारदा इति, अम्बिका इति नामधेयानि स्थानानि च कुर्वन्ति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नराः Human beings, भुवि on the earth, दुर्गा इति as Durga, भद्रकाली इति as Bhadrakali, विजया Vijaya, वैष्णवी इति च and as Vaishnavi, कुमुदा Kumuda, चण्डिका Chandika, कृष्णा Krishna, माधवी Madhavi, कन्यका Kanyaka, माया Maya, नारायणी Narayani, ईशानी Ishani, शारदा इति as Sharada,अम्बिका इति as Ambika, नामधेयानि स्थानानि च कुर्वन्ति (will give you these) names and create holy shrines.

Simple meaning: “People in the world will create holy shrines and worship you under different names like Durga, Bhadrakali, Vijaya, and Vaishnavi, Kumuda, Chandika, Krishna, Madhavi, Kanyaka, Maya, Narayani, Ishani, Sharada, and Ambika.

गर्भसंकर्षणात् तं वै प्राहु: संकर्षणं भुवि ।

रामेति लोकरमणाद् बलं बलवदुच्छ्रयात् ॥10.2.13॥

Word-by-word meaning: गर्भ-संकर्षणात् on account of being transferred from the womb, तम् him, वै indeed, प्राहुः (they will) call, संकर्षणम् Sankarshana, भुवि on earth, रामः इति as Rama, लोक-रमणात् on account of delighting the world, बलम् Bala, बलवत्-उच्छ्रयात् because of being eminent amongst those having strength

उच्छ्रय - Height, elevation (physical and moral); — Growth, increase, intensity; src. Kosha Ashtadhyayi

Anvaya: वै भुवि गर्भसंकर्षणात् तं संकर्षणं, लोकरमणात् रामः इति, बलवदुच्छ्रयात् बलं प्राहुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: वै भुवि Indeed, on the earth, गर्भसंकर्षणात् on account of being transferred from the womb, तं to him, संकर्षणं as Sankarshana, लोकरमणात् on account of delighting the world, रामः इति as Rama, बलवदुच्छ्रयात् because of being eminent amongst those having strength, बलं as Bala, प्राहुः (will be) called.

Simple meaning: “On the earth, he will be called as Sankarshana because he was drawn from one womb to another, as Rama on account of delighting the world, and as Bala because of being eminent amongst those having strength.”

सन्दिष्टैवं भगवता तथेत्योमिति तद्वचः ।

प्रतिगृह्य परिक्रम्य गां गता तत् तथाकरोत् ॥10.2.14॥

Word-by-word meaning: सन्दिष्टा instructed, एवम् thus, भगवता by the Lord, तथा so be it, इति thus, ओम् sacred assent, इति thus, तत्-वचः his words, प्रतिगृह्य having accepted, परिक्रम्य circumambulating, गाम् to the earth, गता went, तत् that, तथा thus, अकरोत् carried out

ओम् - As a particle it implies (a) solemn affirmation and respectful assent (so be it, amen !). (b) assent or acceptance (yes, all right); Src. Kosha Ashtadhyayi

Anvaya: एवं भगवता सन्दिष्टा तद्वचः तथा इति ओम् इति प्रतिगृह्य परिक्रम्य गां गता तत् तथा अकरोत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: एवं Thus, भगवता सन्दिष्टा instructed by the Lord, तद्वचः His words, तथा इति “So be it”, ओम् इति thus affirming, प्रतिगृह्य having accepted, परिक्रम्य circumambulating (the Lord), गां गता went to the earth, तत् तथा अकरोत् carried out His command.

Simple meaning: Having received the Lord’s instructions, Yogamaya respectfully accepted them, circumambulated Him, descended to the earth, and fulfilled His command precisely.

Simple meaning: When Kamsa saw Devaki who bore the Invincible Lord within herself shining brilliantly and lighting up the palace with her pure smile, he became inwardly shaken and thought, “Surely Lord Hari, who will end my life, has entered her womb—this is why she now appears so extraordinary.

Note: Shlokas 10.2.19 and 10.2.20 seem contradictory to each other. These two verses do appear to contradict each other at first glance:

Verse 10.2.19 says Devaki "did not shine well" (न रेजे) while carrying the Lord—like a confined flame or knowledge hidden in an ignorant person.

Verse 10.2.20 says Kamsa saw her "illuminating the house with her radiance" (प्रभया भवनं विरोचयन्तीम्) and looking extraordinarily different.

The resolution lies in understanding two different perspectives:

Verse 19 describes Devaki's external, worldly condition—she was imprisoned, in chains, under constant threat. Despite carrying the Supreme Lord, she couldn't express her natural maternal joy and radiance openly due to her captivity. The similes emphasize this suppression: a flame behind closed doors doesn't illuminate outwardly; Saraswati in a fool doesn't manifest as wisdom.

Verse 20 describes what Kamsa perceived with his inner eye of fear and premonition. Despite her imprisonment, he noticed something had changed—a subtle spiritual radiance, a serene smile, an inexplicable glow. His guilty conscience and fear made him hypersensitive to any change in her, allowing him to perceive the divine presence within her that worldly eyes might miss.

So there's no real contradiction: outwardly she appeared subdued by circumstances, yet to Kamsa's anxious, paranoid perception, even subtle changes betrayed the extraordinary presence within her womb. The verses beautifully capture both the tragedy of her imprisonment and the impossibility of completely concealing the Divine.

न नामरूपे गुणजन्मकर्मभिर्निरूपितव्ये तव तस्य साक्षिणः।

मनोवचोभ्यामनुमेयवर्त्मनो देव क्रियायां प्रतियन्त्यथापि हि ॥10.2.36॥

Word-by-word meaning: न not, नाम-रूपे by name, by form, गुण-जन्म-कर्मभिः by qualities, birth, by actions, निरूपितव्ये to be defined or described, तव of you, तस्य of that, साक्षिणः the witness, मनः-वचोभ्याम् by the mind and by words, अनुमेय is inferred, वर्त्मनः path, देव O Lord, क्रियायाम् in activities, प्रतियन्ति they recognize or perceive, अथ अपि even so, हि indeed

Anvaya: देव! (गुणः यस्य च येन वा प्रकाशते) तस्य साक्षिणः तव नामरूपगुणजन्मकर्मभिः न निरूपितव्ये। अथ वर्त्मनः मनोवचोभ्याम् अनुमेय। अपि हि क्रियायाम् प्रतियन्ति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: देव! (गुणः यस्य च येन वा प्रकाशते) The qualities which shine because of whom and by whom, तस्य साक्षिणः it’s witness, तव नामरूपगुणजन्मकर्मभिः (that person) by your name, your form, your qualities, your birth, or your actions, न निरूपितव्ये cannot define. अथ Yet indeed, वर्त्मनः of those who follow Your path, मनोवचोभ्याम् अनुमेय  is only inferable by mind and speech (by constantly thinking of You in mind and chanting your names). क्रियायाम् अपि हि प्रतियन्ति And also by being engaged in Your activities (devotion).

Simple meaning: O Lord! The people who have witnessed You, cannot truly describe You in terms of your name, form, qualities, birth, or actions. Still, those who follow Your path, can infer You through mind and speech (by constantly thinking of You in mind and chanting your names) and also by being engaged in Your devotion.

शृण्वन् गृणन् संस्मरयंश्च चिन्तयन् नामानि रूपाणि च मङ्गलानि ते ।

क्रियासु यस्त्वच्चरणारविन्दयोराविष्टचेता न भवाय कल्पते ॥10.2.37॥

Word-by-word meaning: शृण्वन् hearing, गृणन् chanting, संस्मरयन् remembering, च and, चिन्तयन् contemplating, नामानि names, रूपाणि forms, च and, मङ्गलानि auspicious, ते your, क्रियासु in activities, यः who, त्वत्-चरण-अरविन्दयोः of Your lotus feet, आविष्ट-चेताः whose mind is deeply absorbed, न not, भवाय for material existence, कल्पते deserve

Anvaya: यः ते मङ्गलानि नामानि रूपाणि च शृण्वन् गृणन् संस्मरयन् चिन्तयन्, क्रियासु त्वच्चरणारविन्दयोः आविष्टचेताः, सः भवाय न कल्पते।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यः One who, ते मङ्गलानि नामानि रूपाणि च Your auspicious names and forms, शृण्वन् गृणन् संस्मरयन् चिन्तयन् hears, chants, remembers and contemplates, क्रियासु in all the activities, त्वच्चरणारविन्दयोः आविष्टचेताः whose mind is deeply absorbed in Your lotus feet, सः he, भवाय to the material world, न कल्पते does not come back.

Simple meaning: A person who constantly hears, chants, remembers, and meditates on Your auspicious names and forms, and whose mind remains absorbed in Your lotus feet during all activities, is no longer bound to worldly existence.

दिष्ट्या हरेऽस्या भवतः पदो भुवो भारोऽपनीतस्तव जन्मनेशितुः ।

दिष्ट्याङ्कितां त्वत्पदकैः सुशोभनैर्द्रक्ष्याम गां द्यां च तवानुकम्पिताम् ॥10.2.38॥

Word-by-word meaning: दिष्ट्या fortunately, हरे O Hari, अस्याः of this, भवतः of your, पदः feet, भुवः of the earth, भारः burden, अपनीतः removed, तव by your, जन्मना by birth, ईशितुः of the Supreme controller, दिष्ट्या again fortunately, अङ्किताम् marked, त्वत्-पदकैः by your footprints, सुशोभनैः beautiful, द्रक्ष्याम we shall see, गाम् the earth, द्याम् the heavens, च and, तव your, अनुकम्पिताम् favored with mercy

Anvaya: हरे! दिष्ट्या ईशितुः तव जन्मना भवतः पदः अस्याः भुवः भारः अपनीतः। दिष्ट्या सुशोभनैः त्वत्पदकैः अङ्कितां तव अनुकम्पितां गां द्यां च द्रक्ष्याम।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: हरे! O Hari! दिष्ट्या By good fortune, ईशितुः Of the Supreme controller, तव जन्मना by Your birth, भवतः पदः Your feet, अस्याः भुवः of this Earth’s, भारः अपनीतः burden will be removed.  दिष्ट्या By good fortune, सुशोभनैः त्वत्पदकैः अङ्कितां By the beautiful imprints of Your feet, तव अनुकम्पितां and graced by Your mercy, गां द्यां च द्रक्ष्याम we will see the Earth and the Heavens.

Simple meaning: O Hari! By good fortune, by Your birth as the Supreme Lord, the burden of this earth has been removed by Your feet. Again by good fortune, we shall see the earth and the heavens, graced by Your mercy and beautifully marked by Your footprints.

न तेऽभवस्येश भवस्य कारणं विना विनोदं बत तर्कयामहे ।

भवो निरोधः स्थितिरप्यविद्यया कृता यतस्त्वय्यभयाश्रयात्मनि ॥10.2.39॥

Word-by-word meaning: न not, ते for you, अ-भवस्य of non existence or destruction, ईश O Lord, भवस्य of creation or becoming, कारणम् cause, विना without, विनोदम् play or pastime, बत indeed or alas, तर्कयामहे we can reason or conceive, भवः creation, निरोधः dissolution, स्थितिः maintenance, अपि also, अविद्यया by ignorance, कृताः are effected, यतः because, त्वयि in you, अभय-आश्रय-आत्मनि who are the abode of fearlessness

Anvaya: ईश! ते अभवस्य भवस्य च कारणं विनोदं विना न तर्कयामहे । यतः भवः निरोधः स्थितिः अपि अभयाश्रयात्मनि त्वयि अविद्यया कृताः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: ईश! O Lord!, ते अभवस्य भवस्य च कारणं The reason for Your creation or destruction, विनोदं विना without Your Divine play, न तर्कयामहे cannot attribute (to anything else). यतः Because, भवः निरोधः स्थितिः अपि creation, preservation, and dissolution, अभयाश्रयात्मनि त्वयि अविद्यया due to that ignorance of You who are beyond fear and the eternal refuge of all, कृताः is perceived.

Simple meaning: O Lord! We cannot attribute creation and destruction in You to any necessity or compulsion—they arise only from Your divine play. For the processes of creation, preservation, and dissolution are perceived due to the ignorance of You who are beyond fear and the eternal refuge of all.

मत्स्याश्वकच्छपनृसिंहवराहहंसराजन्यविप्रविबुधेषु कृतावतारः ।

त्वं पासि नस्त्रिभुवनं च यथाधुनेश भारं भुवो हर यदूत्तम वन्दनं ते ॥10.2.40॥

Word-by-word meaning: मत्स्य-अश्व-कच्छप-नृसिंह-वराह-हंस-राजन्य-विप्र-विबुधेषु  fish (Matsya), horse (Hayagriva), tortoise (Kurma), man-lion (NaraSimha), boar (Varah), swan, among kings (as Rama and Parashurama), among brahmanas (as Vamana), among the devas, कृत-अवतारः one who has assumed incarnations, त्वम् you, पासि protect, नः us, त्रिभुवनम् the three worlds, च and, यथा as, अधुना now, ईश O Lord, भारम् burden, भुवः of the earth, हर remove, यदूत्तम O best of the Yadus, वन्दनम् obeisance, ते to you

The avatar of Vishnu that takes the form of a swan is known as the Hamsa Avatar. In this form, Vishnu appeared as a magnificent white or golden swan to impart supreme spiritual knowledge (Veda shastras) to Brahma and the Sanatkumara sages. The incarnation represents purity, wisdom, and the ability to distinguish truth from untruth.

Anvaya: ईश! मत्स्याश्वकच्छपनृसिंहवराहहंसराजन्यविप्रविबुधेषु यथा कृतावतारः त्वम् अधुना त्रिभुवनं नः पासि। भुवः भारं हर। यदूत्तम! ते वन्दनम्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: ईश! O Lord!, मत्स्याश्वकच्छपनृसिंहवराहहंसराजन्यविप्रविबुधेषु यथा कृतावतारः As you had assumed incarnations of fish, horse, tortoise, man-lion, boar, swan, among kings (as Rama and Parashurama), among brahmanas (as Vamana), among the devas, त्वम् You, अधुना त्रिभुवनं नः पासि now, protect the three worlds and us. भुवः भारं हर Reduce the burden of the Earth.  यदूत्तम! O Best among the Yadus, ते वन्दनम् obeisance to You.

Simple meaning: O Lord! Just as You, who have taken incarnations as fish, horse, tortoise, man-lion, boar, swan, among kings, among brahmanas, and among the devas, You now protect us and the three worlds, so remove the burden of the earth. O best of the Yadus! Obeisance to You.

दिष्ट्याम्ब ते कुक्षिगतः परः पुमानंशेन साक्षाद् भगवान् भवाय नः ।

मा भूद् भयं भोजपतेर्मुमूर्षोर्गोप्ता यदूनां भविता तवात्मजः ॥10.2.41॥

Word-by-word meaning: दिष्ट्या fortunately, अम्ब O mother (addressing Devaki), ते your, कुक्षि-गतः entered the womb, परः supreme, पुमान् person, अंशेन by a plenary portion, साक्षात् directly, भगवान् the Supreme Lord, भवाय for the welfare, नः of us, मा not, भूत् be, भयम् fear, भोज-पतेः of the lord of the Bhojas (Kamsa), मुमूर्षोः who desires to kill, गोप्ता protector, यदूनाम् of the Yadus, भविता will become, तव your, आत्मजः son

Anvaya: अम्ब! दिष्ट्या परः पुमान् साक्षात् भगवान् अंशेन नः भवाय ते कुक्षिगतः। मुमूर्षोः भोजपतेः भयं मा भूत्; तव आत्मजः यदूनां गोप्ता भविता।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अम्ब! O mother (Devaki)! दिष्ट्या Fortunately, परः पुमान् The Supreme Person, साक्षात् भगवान् The Supreme Lord Himself, अंशेन by a plenary portion, नः भवाय for our welfare, ते कुक्षिगतः has come to your womb.  मुमूर्षोः भोजपतेः Of the king of Bhojas, who is inclined to kill, भयं मा भूत् do not be afraid. तव आत्मजः Your son, यदूनां गोप्ता भविता will be the protector of the Yadus.

Simple meaning: O mother! By great fortune the Supreme Person, the Lord Himself, has entered your womb by His plenary portion for our welfare. Let there be no fear of the lord of the Bhojas who wishes to kill, for your son will become the protector of the Yadus.

श्री शुक उवाच

इत्यभिष्टूय पुरुषं यद्रूपमनिदं यथा ।

ब्रह्मेशानौ पुरोधाय देवाः प्रतिययुर्दिवम् ॥10.2.42॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुकः Shri Shuka, उवाच said, इति thus, अभिष्टूय having fully praised, पुरुषम् the Supreme Person, यत् which, रूपम् form, अनिदम् transcendental not material, यथा as it is, ब्रह्म-ईशानौ Brahma and Ishan (Shiva), पुरोधाय placing in front led by, देवाः the devas, प्रतिययुः returned, दिवम् to heaven

Anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच—इति यथा अनिदं यत् रूपं पुरुषम् अभिष्टूय, ब्रह्मेशानौ पुरोधाय देवाः दिवं प्रतिययुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said—इति Thus, यथा अनिदं यत् रूपं पुरुषम् अभिष्टूय After glorifying the Supreme Lord in His transcendental form, ब्रह्मेशानौ पुरोधाय placing Brahma and Shiva in the front, देवाः दिवं प्रतिययुः the gods returned to the heavens.

Simple meaning: Shri Shuka said: After glorifying the Supreme Lord in His transcendental form, the devas, led by Brahma and Shiva, departed and returned to their celestial abode.
