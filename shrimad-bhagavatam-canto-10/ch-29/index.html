A description of the (celebrated) Rasa Play (of the Lord)

श्रीमद्भागवतमहापुराणम्

दशमः स्कन्धः

भगवतो रासक्रीडावर्णनं नाम एकोनत्रिंशः अध्यायः

(48 Shlokas)

श्री शुक उवाच

भगवानपि ता रात्रीः शरदोत्फुल्लमल्लिकाः ।

वीक्ष्य रन्तुं मनश्चक्रे योगमायामुपाश्रितः ॥10.29.1॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, भगवान् the Supreme Lord, अपि also, ताः those, रात्रीः nights, शरत्-उत्फुल्ल-मल्लिकाः when the jasmine flowers were blooming due to the autumn season, वीक्ष्य seeing, रन्तुम् to enjoy/to sport, मनः चक्रे set His mind, योग-मायाम् His internal spiritual potency, उपाश्रितः taking resort of

रात्रीः रात्रि,  इकारान्तः, स्त्रीलिङ्गः, पाक्षिकनदीसंज्ञकः, द्वितीयाबहुवचनम् src. Shabdapath, Ashtadhyayi

ताः तद्,  दकारान्तः, स्त्रीलिङ्गः, सम्बोधनाभावः, सर्वनामशब्दः, प्रथमाबहुवचनम् द्वितीयाबहुवचनम् src. Shabdapath, Ashtadhyayi

द्वितीयाबहुवचनम् in this context because it is qualifying रात्रीः

Anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच— भगवान् अपि शरदोत्फुल्लमल्लिकाः ताः रात्रीः वीक्ष्य योगमायाम् उपाश्रितः (सन्) रन्तुं मनश्चक्रे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said— भगवान् अपि The Supreme Lord also, शरदोत्फुल्लमल्लिकाः ताः रात्रीः वीक्ष्य seeing those nights of autumn in which the jasmine flowers were in full bloom, योगमायाम् उपाश्रितः (सन्) taking resort of His internal spiritual potency, रन्तुं मनश्चक्रे made up His mind to enjoy.

Simple Meaning: Shri Shuka said: Seeing those beautiful autumn nights filled with the fragrance of blooming jasmine flowers, the Supreme Lord Krishna, decided to engage in His divine pastimes. To do so, He took         resort of His internal spiritual potency, Yogamaya.

तदोडुराजः ककुभः करैर्मुखं प्राच्या विलिम्पन्नरुणेन शन्तमैः ।

स चर्षणीनामुदगाच्छुचो मृजन् प्रियः प्रियाया इव दीर्घदर्शनः ॥10.29.2॥

Word-by-word meaning: तदा then, उडु-राजः the king of the stars (the moon), ककुभः of the directions, करैः with his rays (hands), मुखम् the face, प्राच्याः of the eastern direction, विलिम्पन् smearing/painting, अरुणेन with reddish color, शन्-तमैः which are most soothing/comforting, सः he (the moon), चर्षणीनाम् of the people, उदगात् rose, शुचः the distress/heat, मृजन् wiping away, प्रियः a beloved, प्रियायाः of his beloved, इव like, दीर्घ-दर्शनः appearing after a long time

ककुभ् भकारान्तः, स्त्रीलिङ्गः (दिशा, दिक्) प्रथमाबहुवचनम् (ककुभः)  द्वितीयाबहुवचनम् (ककुभः)  पञ्चम्येकवचनम् (ककुभः)  षष्ठ्येकवचनम् (ककुभः)  सम्बोधनबहुवचनम् (ककुभः)

In this case - ककुभः ककुभ्,  भकारान्तः, स्त्रीलिङ्गः, षष्ठ्येकवचनम् src. Shabdapatha, Ashtadhyayi

Anvaya: तदा शन्तमैः अरुणेन करैः प्राच्याः ककुभः मुखं विलिम्पन्, – दीर्घदर्शनः प्रियः प्रियायाः इव – चर्षणीनां शुचः मृजन‍् उडुराजः उदगात्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तदा Then, शन्तमैः अरुणेन करैः with most soothing reddish rays, प्राच्याः ककुभः मुखं विलिम्पन् painting the face of the eastern direction, दीर्घदर्शनः प्रियः प्रियायाः इव like a beloved appearing before his lover after a long absence, चर्षणीनां शुचः मृजन‍् wiping away the distress of the people, उडुराजः उदगात् the moon (king of stars) rose.

Simple Meaning: Then the moon, the king of the stars, rose in the sky. With his most soothing reddish rays, he began to paint the face of the eastern horizon. Just as a beloved husband returns after a long journey to wipe away the grief of his wife, the moon rose to dispel the suffering of the people, appearing like a long-awaited sight.

दृष्ट्वा कुमुद्वन्तमखण्डमण्डलं रमाननाभं नवकुङ्कुमारुणम् ।

वनं च तत्कोमलगोभिरञ्जितं जगौ कलं वामदृशां मनोहरम् ॥10.29.3॥

Word-by-word meaning: दृष्ट्वा having seen, कुमुद्वन्तम् which causes the night-blooming lilies to blossom, अखण्ड-मण्डलम् the full disc (of the moon), रमा-आनन-आभम् resembling the face of the Goddess of Fortune, नव-कुङ्कुम-अरुणम् as red as fresh kunkuma, वनम् the forest, च and, तत्-कोमल-गोभिः by those gentle rays, अञ्जितम् decorated/bathed, जगौ He sang, कलम् sweetly/melodiously, वाम-दृशाम् for the women with beautiful eyes (the gopis), मनः-हरम् captivating the mind

Anvaya: नवकुङ्कुमारुणं रमाननाभम् अखण्डमण्डलं कुमुद्वन्तं (चन्द्रं) तत्कोमलगोभिः अञ्जितं वनं च दृष्ट्वा, (भगवान्) वामदृशां मनोहरं कलं जगौ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नवकुङ्कुमारुणं रमाननाभम् अखण्डमण्डलं (चन्द्रं) The full disc of the moon, which was as red as fresh kunkuma and glowed like the face of the Goddess of Fortune, कुमुद्वन्तं and which causes the lilies to bloom, तत्कोमलगोभिः अञ्जितं वनं च दृष्ट्वा seeing the forest bathed in those gentle rays, (भगवान्) (the Lord), वामदृशां मनोहरं कलं जगौ sang sweetly and melodiously in a way that captivates the minds of the beautiful-eyed gopis.

Simple Meaning: Lord Krishna saw the full moon rising, glowing with a reddish hue like fresh kunkuma and looking as beautiful as the face of Rama (the Goddess of Fortune). Seeing the entire Vraja forest bathed in the soft, cooling moonlight which caused the lilies to bloom, He began to play His flute and sing melodiously, capturing the hearts of the beautiful-eyed gopis.

निशम्य गीतं तदनङ्गवर्धनं व्रजस्त्रियः कृष्णगृहीतमानसाः ।

आजग्मुरन्योन्यमलक्षितोद्यमाः स यत्र कान्तो जवलोलकुण्डलाः ॥10.29.4॥

Word-by-word meaning: निशम्य having heard, गीतम् the song, तत् that, अनङ्ग-वर्धनम् which increases Manmatha’s influence (desire/love) (अन्-अङ्ग - one without a body - Manmatha - god of love), व्रज-स्त्रियः the women of Vraja (the gopis), कृष्ण-गृहीत-मानसाः whose minds were captured by Krishna, आजग्मुः they came, अन्योन्यम् one another, अ-लक्षित-उद्यमाः without their efforts being noticed, सः He, यत्र where, कान्तः their lover, जव-लोल-कुण्डलाः their earrings swinging due to their speed

Anvaya: अनङ्गवर्धनं तत् गीतं निशम्य कृष्णगृहीतमानसाः जवलोलकुण्डलाः व्रजस्त्रियः अलक्षितोद्यमाः (सत्यः) यत्र सः कान्तः (आसीत्), तत्र अन्योन्यम् आजग्मुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अनङ्गवर्धनं तत् गीतं निशम्य Hearing that song which increases the flow of love, कृष्णगृहीतमानसाः whose minds were seized by Krishna, जवलोलकुण्डलाः व्रजस्त्रियः the women of Vraja with their earrings swinging due to their speed, अलक्षितोद्यमाः without noticing one another's efforts, यत्र सः कान्तः (आसीत्) where their beloved was, तत्र अन्योन्यम् आजग्मुः came there separately.

Simple Meaning: Upon hearing the sound of the flute, which arouses the deepest sentiments of divine love, the gopis’ hearts were completely captivated by Krishna. Without stopping to tell one another, they all rushed toward their beloved Lord. They ran so fast that their earrings swung violently back and forth as they hurried to reach the spot where He stood.

दुहन्त्योऽभिययुः काश्चिद् दोहं हित्वा समुत्सुकाः ।

पयोऽधिश्रित्य संयावमनुद्वास्यापरा ययुः ॥10.29.5॥

Word-by-word meaning: दुहन्त्यः while milking, अभिययुः they went, काश्चित् some (gopis), दोहम् the milking, हित्वा leaving, समुत्सुकाः being very eager, पयः milk, अधिश्रित्य having placed on the stove, संयावम् wheat-cake (or porridge), अनुद्वास्य without removing, अपराः others, ययुः went

Anvaya: काश्चित् समुत्सुकाः दुहन्त्यः (स्त्रियः) दोहं हित्वा अभिययुः, अपराः पयः अधिश्रित्य संयावम् अनुद्वास्य (च) ययुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: काश्चित् समुत्सुकाः दुहन्त्यः (स्त्रियः) Some very eager women while in the middle of milking, दोहं हित्वा left the milking unfinished, अपराः पयः अधिश्रित्य others, having placed milk on the fire, संयावम् अनुद्वास्य (च) ययुः and without taking the wheat-cakes off the stove, they left.

Simple Meaning: The gopis were so overcome with eagerness to meet Krishna that they abandoned their household chores mid-task. Some who were milking cows simply left the pails and ran toward Him. Others, who were in the middle of cooking, left milk boiling on the stove and wheat-cakes in the oven without even waiting to take them off the fire.

परिषयन्त्यस्तद्धित्वा पाययन्त्यः शिशून् पयः ।

शुश्रूषयन्त्यः पतीन् काश्चिदश्नन्त्योऽपास्य भोजनम् ॥10.29.6॥

Word-by-word meaning: परिवेषयन्त्यः while serving food, तत् that, हित्वा abandoning, पाययन्त्यः while feeding, शिशून् to their infants, पयः milk, शुश्रूषयन्त्यः while attending to/serving, पतीन् their husbands, काश्चित् some, अश्नन्त्यः while eating, अपास्य setting aside/leaving, भोजनम् the meal

Anvaya: काश्चित् (भोजनं) परिवेषयन्त्यः तत् हित्वा, (काश्चित्) शिशून् पयः पाययन्त्यः, (काश्चित्) पतीन् शुश्रूषयन्त्यः, (काश्चित् च) अश्नन्त्यः भोजनम् अपास्य (ययुः)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: काश्चित् (भोजनं) परिवेषयन्त्यः Some who were serving food, तत् हित्वा left that aside, (काश्चित्) शिशून् पयः पाययन्त्यः some who were breastfeeding their infants (left them), (काश्चित्) पतीन् शुश्रूषयन्त्यः some who were attending to their husbands (left their service), (काश्चित् च) अश्नन्त्यः भोजनम् अपास्य and some who were themselves eating, left their meal unfinished (and went).

Simple Meaning: The intensity of their devotion was so great that they dropped everything. Some gopis were serving dinner but left the plates right there, some were nursing their babies or serving their husbands, yet they immediately stopped what they were doing. Others were in the middle of their own meal, but they set the food aside and hurried out the moment they heard Krishna’s flute.

लिम्पन्त्यः प्रमृजन्त्योऽन्या अञ्जन्त्यः काश्च लोचने ।

व्यत्यस्तवस्त्राभरणाः काश्चित् कृष्णान्तिकं ययुः ॥10.29.7॥

Word-by-word meaning: लिम्पन्त्यः applying (cosmetics/ointment), प्रमृजन्त्यः washing/cleaning, अन्याः others, अञ्जन्त्यः applying collyrium (kajal), काश्च and some, लोचने on their eyes, व्यत्यस्त-वस्त्र-आभरणाः their clothes and ornaments in disarray (or put on backward/wrongly), काश्चित् some, कृष्ण-अन्तिकम् to the side of Krishna, ययुः went

Anvaya: अन्याः (देहं) लिम्पन्त्यः प्रमृजन्त्यः, काश्च लोचने अञ्जन्त्यः, काश्चित् च व्यत्यस्तवस्त्राभरणाः (सत्यः) कृष्णान्तिकं ययुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अन्याः (देहं) लिम्पन्त्यः प्रमृजन्त्यः Others, while applying ointments to their bodies or washing themselves, काश्च लोचने अञ्जन्त्यः and some while applying collyrium to their eyes, काश्चित् च व्यत्यस्तवस्त्राभरणाः and some with their clothes and ornaments put on all wrong, कृष्णान्तिकं ययुः went to the presence of Krishna.

Simple Meaning: Some gopis were in the middle of applying cosmetics to their bodies or bathing when they heard the flute. Others were applying collyrium to their eyes, but they left immediately, even if they had only finished one eye. In their frantic hurry to reach Krishna, many of them put their clothes and jewelry on backward or in the wrong places, yet they rushed to His side without a second thought.

ता वार्यमाणाः पतिभिः पितृभिर्भ्रातृबन्धुभिः ।

गोविन्दापहृतात्मानो न न्यवर्तन्त मोहिताः ॥10.29.8॥

Word-by-word meaning: ताः they (the gopis), वार्यमाणाः being forbidden/restrained, पतिभिः by their husbands, पितृभिः by fathers, भ्रातृ-बन्धुभिः by brothers and other relatives, गोविन्द-अपहृत-आत्मानः whose hearts/souls were stolen away by Govinda, न न्यवर्तन्त they did not turn back, मोहिताः enchanted/bewildered (by love)

Anvaya: पतिभिः पितृभिः भ्रातृबन्धुभिः (च) वार्यमाणाः (अपि) ताः गोविन्दापहृतात्मानः मोहिताः न न्यवर्तन्त।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: पतिभिः पितृभिः भ्रातृबन्धुभिः (च) वार्यमाणाः (अपि) Although being restrained by their husbands, fathers, brothers, and relatives, ताः गोविन्दापहृतात्मानः those women, whose very souls had been stolen by Govinda, मोहिताः being enchanted, न न्यवर्तन्त did not turn back.

Simple Meaning: Even though their husbands, fathers, brothers, and other relatives tried to stop them from leaving, the gopis could not be held back. Their hearts had been completely captured by Govinda, and in their state of divine enchantment, they ignored every obstacle and refused to return home.

अन्तर्गृहगताः काश्चिद् गोप्योऽलब्धविनिर्गमाः ।

कृष्णं तद्भावनायुक्ता दध्युर्मीलितलोचनाः ॥10.29.9॥

Word-by-word meaning: अन्तर्गृह-गताः having stayed inside their houses, काश्चित् some, गोप्यः gopis, अलब्ध-विनिर्गमाः not finding a way to go out, कृष्णम् upon Krishna, तत्-भावना-युक्ताः possessed by thoughts of Him, दध्युः they meditated, मीलित-लोचनाः with closed eyes

Anvaya: अन्तर्गृहगताः अलब्धविनिर्गमाः काश्चित् गोप्यः मीलितलोचनाः (सत्यः) तद्भावनायुक्ताः कृष्णं दध्युः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अन्तर्गृहगताः Those who had remained inside their homes, अलब्धविनिर्गमाः not being able to exit, काश्चित् गोप्यः some gopis, मीलितलोचनाः (सत्यः) with eyes closed, तद्भावनायुक्ताः being fully absorbed in thoughts of Him, कृष्णं दध्युः meditated upon Krishna.

Simple Meaning: Some gopis, being forcibly kept inside their homes by family members or locked doors, were unable to leave. Finding no way out, they simply closed their eyes and began to meditate upon Krishna with intense devotion, their minds completely absorbed in His divine form and presence.

दुःसहप्रेष्ठविरहतीव्रतापधुताशुभाः ।

ध्यानप्राप्ताच्युताश्लेषनिर्वृत्या क्षीणमङ्गलाः ॥10.29.10॥

Word-by-word meaning: दुःसह-प्रेष्ठ-विरह-तीव्र-ताप-धुत-अशुभाः those (gopis) whose impurities (sins) were shaken off by the intense heat of the unbearable separation from their most beloved (दुःसह- unbearable, प्रेष्ठ- beloved, विरह- separation, तीव्र- intense, ताप- heat, धुत- washed away, अशुभाः the impurities), ध्यान-प्राप्त-अच्युत-आश्लेष-निर्वृत्या by the bliss of embracing the Infallible Lord (Achyuta) attained through meditation (ध्यान- through meditation, प्राप्त- attained, अच्युत- Krishna, आश्लेष- embrace, निर्वृत्या by the bliss), क्षीण-मङ्गलाः those whose material pious credits were exhausted

Anvaya: (ताः गोप्यः) दुःसहप्रेष्ठविरहतीव्रतापधुताशुभाः ध्यानप्राप्ताच्युताश्लेषनिर्वृत्या क्षीणमङ्गलाः (च बभूवुः)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (Those gopis) दुःसहप्रेष्ठविरहतीव्रतापधुताशुभाः had all their impurities burnt away by the agonizing heat of separation from their most beloved, ध्यानप्राप्ताच्युताश्लेषनिर्वृत्या and through the supreme ecstasy of embracing Krishna within their meditation, क्षीणमङ्गलाः they exhausted all their material pious credits.

Simple Meaning: For those gopis who were stuck inside, the unbearable pain of being separated from their most beloved Krishna acted as a purifying fire that destroyed every trace of their past sins (ashubha). Simultaneously, the overwhelming spiritual bliss they felt by embracing Him in deep meditation exhausted all their remaining material merits (mangala). In this way, they became completely purified of all material bondage.

तमेव परमात्मानं जारबुद्ध्यापि सङ्गताः ।

जहुर्गुणमयं देहं सद्यः प्रक्षीणबन्धनाः ॥10.29.11॥

Word-by-word meaning: तम् Him, एव indeed, परम-आत्मानम् the Supreme Soul, जार-बुद्ध्या with the mentality of a paramour/lover, अपि even though, सङ्गताः joined/connected, जहुः they gave up, गुण-मयम् made of the material modes, देहम् the body, सद्यः immediately, प्रक्षीण-बन्धनाः their bonds being completely destroyed

Anvaya: जारबुद्ध्या अपि तम् एव परमात्मानं सङ्गताः (ताः) सद्यः प्रक्षीणबन्धनाः (सत्यः) गुणमयं देहं जहुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: जारबुद्ध्या अपि Even with the idea of Him as a paramour, तम् एव परमात्मानं सङ्गताः having connected with Him who is indeed the Supreme Soul, (ताः) they, सद्यः प्रक्षीणबन्धनाः (सत्यः) immediately having their material bonds destroyed, गुणमयं देहं जहुः gave up their bodies made of the material modes.

Simple Meaning: Even though these gopis approached Krishna with the sentiment of a lover rather than knowing Him as the Absolute, they were nevertheless connecting with the Supreme Soul. Because of this divine connection, all their material bondages were instantly shattered, and they cast off their physical bodies composed of the three material modes to join Him in their pure spiritual forms.

राजा उवाच

कृष्णं विदुः परं कान्तं न तु ब्रह्मतया मुने ।

गुणप्रवाहोपरमस्तासां गुणधियां कथम् ॥10.29.12॥

Word-by-word meaning: राजा उवाच the King (Parikshit) said, कृष्णम् Krishna, विदुः they knew, परम् only/merely, कान्तम् as a lover, न not, तु but, ब्रह्मतया as the Absolute Truth (Brahman), मुने O sage (Shukadeva), गुण-प्रवाह-उपरमः the cessation of the flow of the material modes, तासाम् for them, गुण-धियाम् whose minds were fixed on material qualities, कथम् how

Anvaya: राजा उवाच— मुने! (ताः गोप्यः) कृष्णं परं कान्तं विदुः, न तु ब्रह्मतया। (तर्हि) गुणधियां तासां गुणप्रवाहोपरमः कथम् (अभवत्)?

Literal meaning of the anvaya: राजा उवाच The King said— मुने O sage! (ताः गोप्यः) those gopis, कृष्णं परं कान्तं विदुः knew Krishna only as a lover, न तु ब्रह्मतया and not as the Supreme Brahman. (तर्हि) Then, गुणधियां तासां for those whose minds were focused on material qualities, गुणप्रवाहोपरमः कथम् (अभवत्) how did the cessation of the material modes occur?

Simple Meaning: King Parikshit asked: "O Sage, the gopis perceived Krishna merely as their beloved lover, not as the Supreme Absolute Truth. Since their minds were absorbed in Him through the lens of 'material' qualities (as a human lover), how was it possible for them to transcend the influence of the material modes and attain liberation?"

Understanding the King's Doubt: King Parikshit is raising a logical point here. Usually, liberation requires one to realize the Brahman (the spiritual reality) beyond the Gunas (material qualities like lust, anger, and attachment).

Since the gopis appeared to be driven by kama (desire) for a handsome youth, the King is asking how such "worldly" attachment could result in the total destruction of material bondage. This question sets the stage for Shri Shuka  to explain the unique, purifying nature of Preman (divine love) versus Kama (mundane lust).

श्री शुक उवाच

उक्तं पुरस्तादेतत्ते चैद्यः सिद्धिं यथा गतः ।

द्विषन्नपि हृषीकेशं किमुताधोक्षजप्रियाः ॥10.29.13॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, उक्तम् explained, पुरस्तात् previously, एतत् this, ते to you, चैद्यः the King of Chedi (Shishupala), सिद्धिम् perfection/liberation, यथा how, गतः attained, द्विषन् hating, अपि even, हृषीकेशम् the Lord of the senses, किमुत what then to speak of, अधोक्षज-प्रियाः those who are dear to the Transcendental Lord (Achutya)

Anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच— एतत् ते पुरस्तात् उक्तम्, यथा हृषीकेशं द्विषन् अपि चैद्यः सिद्धिं गतः, किमुत अधोक्षजप्रियाः (ताः गोप्यः)!

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said— एतत् ते पुरस्तात् उक्तम् This has been told to you before, यथा हृषीकेशं द्विषन् अपि चैद्यः how even while hating the Lord of the senses, the King of Chedi, सिद्धिं गतः attained perfection. किमुत अधोक्षजप्रियाः What then to speak of those who are the beloved of the Transcendental Lord!

Simple Meaning: Shri Shuka replied: "I have already explained this to you. Even Shishupala, the King of Chedi, attained spiritual perfection despite his intense hatred for Lord Krishna. If a person who hated the Lord could be liberated by constantly thinking of Him, then what is there to say about the gopis, who possessed nothing but pure, unalloyed love for the Transcendental Lord?"

Contextual Note: Shuka is reminding Parikshit of the story of Shishupala (from the 7th Canto). Shishupala was so obsessed with his enmity toward Krishna that he thought of Him constantly. Because the object of his thoughts was the Supreme Absolute, that constant remembrance purified him.

The logic here is an A Fortiori argument (argument from the stronger):

नृणां निःश्रेयसार्थाय व्यक्तिर्भगवतो नृप ।

अव्ययस्याप्रमेयस्य निर्गुणस्य गुणात्मनः ॥10.29.14॥

Word-by-word meaning: नृणाम् of men, निःश्रेयस-अर्थाय for the sake of the ultimate benefit (liberation), व्यक्तिः the manifestation/appearance, भगवतः of the Supreme Lord, नृप O King, अव्ययस्य of the imperishable, अप्रमेयस्य of the immeasurable, निर्गुणस्य of Him who is free from material qualities, गुण-आत्मनः the controller of the material modes (or the soul of all good qualities)

Anvaya: नृप! अव्ययस्य अप्रमेयस्य निर्गुणस्य गुणात्मनः भगवतः व्यक्तिः नृणां निःश्रेयसार्थाय (भवति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नृप O King! अव्ययस्य अप्रमेयस्य निर्गुणस्य गुणात्मनः भगवतः of the Supreme Lord, who is imperishable, immeasurable, beyond material modes, and the controller of all qualities, व्यक्तिः the appearance, नृणां निःश्रेयसार्थाय (भवति) is for the supreme benefit of all human beings.

Simple Meaning: O King, the appearance of the Supreme Personality of Godhead in this world is intended for the ultimate liberation and highest well-being of all humanity. Although He is imperishable, beyond the grasp of human measurement, and free from the contamination of material modes, He manifests Himself as the controller of these very modes to bless the world.

Key Philosophical Insight : Shri Shuka is addressing the King’s previous doubt about "material qualities." He explains that when the Lord appears, His "body" or "qualities" are not like ours.

कामं क्रोधं भयं स्नेहमैक्यं सौहृदमेव च ।

नित्यं हरौ विदधतो यान्ति तन्मयतां हि ते ॥10.29.15॥

Word-by-word meaning: कामम् lust/desire, क्रोधम् anger, भयम् fear, स्नेहम् affection, ऐक्यम् feelings of oneness (devotion), सौहृदम् friendship, एव च and also, नित्यम् constantly, हरौ in Lord Hari, विदधतः those who direct, यान्ति they attain, तन्मयताम् absorption in Him (a nature like His), हि indeed, ते they

Anvaya: कामं क्रोधं भयं स्नेहम् ऐक्यं सौहृदम् एव च हरौ नित्यं विदधतः ते हि तन्मयतां यान्ति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: कामं क्रोधं भयं स्नेहम् Lust, anger, fear, affection, ऐक्यं सौहृदम् एव च as well as devotion and friendship, हरौ नित्यं विदधतः (those who) constantly direct toward Lord Hari, ते हि तन्मयतां यान्ति they indeed attain a state of absorption in Him.

Simple Meaning: Whether through desire, anger, fear, affection, friendship, or a sense of spiritual oneness, those who constantly focus their minds and emotions on Lord Hari will eventually achieve absorption in Him. Because the object of their focus is the Supreme Divine, the intensity of their emotion—regardless of its initial nature—purifies them and brings them to the state of God-consciousness.

The "Purifying Power" of Krishna: This is one of the most famous verses in the Srimad Bhagavatam regarding the path of Raganuga Bhakti.

न चैवं विस्मयः कार्यो भवता भगवत्यजे ।

योगेश्वरेश्वरे कृष्णे यत एतद् विमुच्यते ॥10.29.11॥

Word-by-word meaning: न not, च and, एवम् in this way, विस्मयः amazement/doubt, कार्यः should be done, भवता by you, भगवति regarding the Supreme Lord, अजे the unborn, योग-ईश्वर-ईश्वरे the Lord of all masters of yoga, कृष्णे in Krishna, यतः from whom, एतत् this (the entire universe), विमुच्यते is liberated/discharged (or by whom liberation is granted)

Anvaya: भवता अजे भगवति योगेश्वरेश्वरे कृष्णे एवं विस्मयः न कार्यः, यतः एतत् (विश्वं) विमुच्यते।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: भवता By you, अजे भगवति योगेश्वरेश्वरे कृष्णे regarding Krishna, the unborn Supreme Personality and the Lord of all lords of yoga, एवं विस्मयः न कार्यः such wonder or doubt should not be harbored. यतः एतत् विमुच्यते Because from Him alone this entire universe is released (created/maintained).

Simple Meaning: O King, you should not be surprised by the liberation of the gopis. Krishna is the unborn Supreme Lord, the Master of all mystic powers and the Lord of all Yogis. Since He is the ultimate source of the creation, maintenance, and liberation of the entire universe, granting salvation to those who love Him—even in the guise of a human lover—is not at all difficult for Him.

Significance of the Verse: Shri Shuka is concluding his defense of the gopis' liberation with a powerful reminder of Krishna’s divinity:

ता दृष्ट्वान्तिकमायाता भगवान् व्रजयोषितः ।

अवदद् वदतां श्रेष्ठो वाचः पेशैर्विमोहयन् ॥10.29.17॥

Word-by-word meaning: ताः them, दृष्ट्वा having seen, अन्तिकम् near, आयाताः arrived, भगवान् the Supreme Lord, व्रज-योषितः the women of Vraja (gopis), अवदत् spoke, वदताम् among those who speak, श्रेष्ठः the best/most eloquent, वाचः of words, पेशैः with clever/artistic flourishes, विमोहयन् bewildering/enchanting

Anvaya: अन्तिकम् आयाताः ताः व्रजयोषितः दृष्ट्वा, वदतां श्रेष्ठः भगवान् वाचः पेशैः (ताः) विमोहयन् अवदत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अन्तिकम् आयाताः ताः व्रजयोषितः दृष्ट्वा Having seen those women of Vraja arrived in His presence, वदतां श्रेष्ठः भगवान् the Supreme Lord, the most eloquent of speakers, वाचः पेशैः (ताः) विमोहयन् bewildering them with the artistry of His words, अवदत् spoke.

Simple Meaning: When Lord Krishna saw that the gopis had arrived near Him, He—the most eloquent and skillful of all speakers—began to address them. Using clever, artistic, and charming turns of phrase, He spoke in a way that further enchanted and bewildered their hearts.

Contextual Note: This verse marks a shift in the mood. The gopis have abandoned everything to be with Krishna, but instead of welcoming them immediately, Krishna uses His supreme eloquence (वाचः पेशैः) to test them. He begins by playing the role of a strictly moral teacher, advising them to go back to their duties. His words are "bewildering" because while they sound like a rejection, they are delivered with such divine charm that the gopis are left both confused and deeply attracted.

श्री भगवान् उवाच

स्वागतं वो महाभागाः प्रियं किं करवाणि वः ।

व्रजस्यानामयं कच्चिद् ब्रूतागमनकारणम् ॥10.29.18॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री भगवान् उवाच the Supreme Lord said, स्वागतम् welcome, वः to you, महा-भागाः O most fortunate ladies, प्रियम् pleasing, किम् what, करवाणि may I do, वः for you, व्रजस्य of Vraja, अनामयम् well-being/health, कच्चित् (is it) whether, ब्रूत explain, आगमन-कारणम् the reason for your coming

Anvaya: श्री भगवान् उवाच— महाभागाः! वः स्वागतम्। वः किं प्रियं करवाणि? व्रजस्य अनामयं कच्चित्? (युष्माकम्) आगमनकारणम् ब्रूत।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री भगवान् उवाच The Supreme Lord said— महाभागाः O most fortunate ones! वः स्वागतम् Welcome to all of you. वः किं प्रियं करवाणि What pleasing service may I perform for you? व्रजस्य अनामयं कच्चित् Is everything well with the inhabitants of Vraja? (युष्माकम्) आगमनकारणम् ब्रूत Please tell Me the reason for your coming here.

Simple Meaning: Lord Krishna said: "Welcome, O most fortunate ladies! What can I do to please you? Is everything well in Vraja? Please tell Me, what is the reason for your coming here at this hour?"

The Divine Irony: In this verse, Krishna begins His "test" of the gopis. Even though He was the one who drew them there with the sound of His flute, He addresses them with formal politeness as if their arrival were a surprise.

रजन्येषा घोररूपा घोरसत्त्वनिषेविता ।

Anvaya: सुमध्यमाः! एषा रजनी घोररूपा घोरसत्त्वनिषेविता (च अस्ति)। (अतः) व्रजं प्रतियात, इह स्त्रीभिः न स्थेयम्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सुमध्यमाः O slender-waisted ones! एषा रजनी घोररूपा this night is of a terrifying nature, घोरसत्त्वनिषेविता (च अस्ति) and is inhabited by fierce animals. (अतः) व्रजं प्रतियात Therefore, return to Vraja, इह स्त्रीभिः न स्थेयम् women should not remain here.

Simple Meaning: Lord Krishna continued: O slender-waisted gopis, this night is very dark and frightening, and the forest is filled with fierce, dangerous creatures. It is not safe or proper for women to be out here alone at this hour. You must return to Vraja immediately.

मातरः पितरः पुत्रा भ्रातरः पतयश्च वः ।

विचिन्वन्ति ह्यपश्यन्तो मा कृढ्वं बन्धुसाध्वसम् ॥10.29.20॥

Word-by-word meaning: मातरः mothers, पितरः fathers, पुत्राः sons, भ्रातरः brothers, पतयः husbands, च and, वः your, विचिन्वन्ति are searching, हि indeed, अपश्यन्तः not seeing (you), मा do not, कृढ्वम् cause, बन्धु-साध्वसम् anxiety to your relatives

Anvaya: वः मातरः पितरः पुत्राः भ्रातरः पतयः च (युष्मान्) अपश्यन्तः हि विचिन्वन्ति। (अतः) बन्धुसाध्वसं मा कृढ्वम्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: वः मातरः पितरः पुत्राः भ्रातरः पतयः च Your mothers, fathers, sons, brothers, and husbands, (युष्मान्) अपश्यन्तः हि विचिन्वन्ति not seeing you, are certainly searching for you. (अतः) बन्धुसाध्वसं मा कृढ्वम् Therefore, do not cause such anxiety to your kinsmen.

Simple Meaning: Your mothers, fathers, sons, brothers, and husbands are surely looking for you now that you are nowhere to be found. You should not cause your family and relatives such unnecessary distress. Go back to them at once.

Word-by-word meaning: यत् which, पति-अपत्य-सुहृदाम् of husbands, children, and relatives, अनुवृत्तिः service/following, अङ्ग O dear one, स्त्रीणाम् for women, स्व-धर्मः one's own duty, इति thus, धर्म-विदा by the Knower of Religion, त्वया by You, उक्तम् spoken, अस्तु let it be, एवम् so, एतत् this, उपदेश-पदे the object of all instruction, त्वयि in You, ईशे the Supreme Lord, प्रेष्ठः most dear, भवान् You, तनु-भृताम् for all embodied beings, किल indeed, बन्धुः friend/kinsman, आत्मा the Self

Anvaya: अङ्ग! 'पत्यपत्यसुहृदामनुवृत्तिः स्त्रीणां स्वधर्मः' इति धर्मविदा त्वया यत् उक्तम्, एतत् एवम् अस्तु। (किन्तु) उपदेशपदे ईशे त्वयि (एतत् घटते), (यतः) भवान् तनुभृतां प्रेष्ठः बन्धुः आत्मा किल।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अङ्ग O dear one! 'पत्यपत्यसुहृदामनुवृत्तिः स्त्रीणां स्वधर्मः' इति "That the service of husbands, children, and relatives is the natural duty of women," धर्मविदा त्वया यत् उक्तम् as was spoken by You, the knower of religion, एतत् एवम् अस्तु let it be so. (किन्तु) उपदेशपदे ईशे त्वयि (But) toward You, the Supreme Lord and the ultimate goal of all instruction, (this applies), (यतः) भवान् तनुभृतां प्रेष्ठः बान्धुः आत्मा किल (since) You are indeed the most beloved friend and the very Soul of all embodied beings.

Simple Meaning: O dear Krishna, You are the Knower of all religions, and You have said that a woman's true duty is to serve her husband, children, and relatives. We accept this as true. However, this service should actually be directed toward You! You are the Supreme Lord, the ultimate goal of all teachings. For every living being, You are the most beloved friend, the closest relative, and the very Soul itself.

The Gopis' "Higher Logic" : In this verse, the gopis brilliantly turn Krishna’s own moral argument back toward Him. They use the philosophical principle of Atma-tattva:

They are essentially saying: "Why should we go to the 'reflections' (family) when we have found the 'Original' (You)?"

कुर्वन्ति हि त्वयि रतिं कुशलाः स्व आत्मन् नित्यप्रिये पतिसुतादिभिरार्तिदैः किम् ।

तन्नः प्रसीद परमेश्वर मा स्म छिन्द्या आशां भृतां त्वयि चिरादरविन्दनेत्र॥10.29.33॥

Word-by-word meaning: कुर्वन्ति perform/do, हि indeed, त्वयि in You, रतिम् love/attachment, कुशलाः those who are expert/wise, स्व own, आत्मन् in the Self, नित्य-प्रिये who is eternally dear, पति-सुत-आदिभिः with husbands, children, and others, आर्ति-दैः who only give misery/pain, किम् what (is the use), तत् therefore, नः unto us, प्रसीद be merciful/pleased, परम-ईश्वर O Supreme Lord, मा स्म छिन्द्याः please do not cut, आशाम् the hope, भृताम् maintained, त्वयि in You, चिरात् for a long time, अरविन्द-नेत्र O lotus-eyed one

Anvaya: कुशलाः हि नित्यप्रिये स्व आत्मन् त्वयि रतिं कुर्वन्ति। आर्तिदैः पतिसुतादिभिः किम्? तत् परमेश्वर! नः प्रसीद। अरविन्दनेत्र! त्वयि चिरात् भृतां नः आशां मा स्म छिन्द्याः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: कुशलाः हि For those who are truly wise indeed, नित्यप्रिये स्व आत्मन् त्वयि in You, who are the eternally dear Dweller of the heart (the Self), रतिं कुर्वन्ति they cultivate their love. आर्तिदैः पतिसुतादिभिः किम्? What is the use of husbands, children, and others who ultimately cause only pain? तत् परमेश्वर! नः प्रसीद Therefore, O Supreme Lord, be merciful to us. अरविन्दनेत्र! O lotus-eyed one! त्वयि चिरात् भृतां नः आशां our hopes which have been cherished in You for a long time, मा स्म छिन्द्याः please do not cut them down.

Simple Meaning: O lotus-eyed Lord, those who are truly wise develop deep love for You, for You are the eternal Soul and the only true object of affection. What is the use of being attached to husbands, children, or relatives who only bring suffering in the end? Therefore, O Supreme Controller, please show us Your mercy. We have cherished the hope of serving You for a very long time, please do not shatter those hopes now.

चित्तं सुखेन भवतापहृतं गृहेषु यन्निर्विशत्युत करावपि गृह्यकृत्ये ।

पादौ पदं न चलतस्तव पादमूलाद् यामः कथं व्रजमथो करवाम किं वा ॥10.29.34॥

Word-by-word meaning: चित्तम् mind, सुखेन easily, भवता by You, अपहृतम् stolen/taken away, गृहेषु in household affairs, यत् which, निर्विशति was deeply absorbed, उत and, करौ two hands, अपि also, गृह्य-कृत्ये in household chores, पादौ two feet, पदम् a single step, न चलतः do not move, तव पाद-मूलात् from the base of Your feet, यामः we shall go, कथम् how, व्रजम् to Vraja (the village), अथः and then, करवाम we shall do, किम् वा what else

Anvaya: गृहेषु निर्विशति यत् चित्तं (तत्) भवता सुखेन अपहृतम्, उत गृह्यकृत्ये (सक्तौ) करौ अपि (अपहृतौ)। पादौ तव पादमूलात् पदं न चलतः। अथः (वयम्) व्रजं कथं यामः? वा किं करवाम?

Literal meaning of the anvaya: गृहेषु निर्विशति यत् चित्तं Our minds, which were once deeply absorbed in household affairs, (तत्) भवता सुखेन अपहृतम् have been easily stolen by You, उत गृह्यकृत्ये (सक्तौ) करौ अपि and even our hands, which were occupied in household chores, (have been taken away). पादौ तव पादमूलात् पदं न चलतः Our feet will not move even one step away from the base of Your lotus feet. अथः (वयम्) व्रजं कथं यामः? So then, how can we go back to Vraja? वा किं करवाम? Or what else could we possibly do?

Simple Meaning: O Krishna, You have easily stolen our minds, which were once busy with our homes and families. Even our hands, which used to be occupied with household work, are now paralyzed, and our feet refuse to move even a single step away from You. Since You have taken everything from us, how can we return to the village? And even if we did return, what work could we possibly perform?

सिञ्चाङ्ग नस्त्वदधरामृतपूरकेण हासावलोककलगीतजहृच्छयाग्निम् ।

नो चेद् वयं विरहजाग्न्युपयुक्तदेहा ध्यानेन याम पदयोः पदवीं सखे ते ॥10.29.35॥

Word-by-word meaning: सिञ्च extinguish/sprinkle, अङ्ग O dear one, नः our, त्वत्-अधर-अमृत-पूरकेण by the flood of the nectar of Your lips (त्वत्- your, अधर- lips, अमृत- nectar, पूरकेण flood), हास-अवलोक-कल-गीत-ज-हृच्छय-अग्निम् the fire of lust/desire in the heart born from Your smiling glance and melodious song (हास- smile, अवलोक- looks, कल- melodious, गीत- song, ज- born, हृच्छय- in the heart, अग्निम् fire), नो चेत् otherwise, वयम् we, विरह-ज-अग्नि-उपयुक्त-देहाः our bodies consumed by the fire born of separation (विरह- separation, ज- born, अग्नि- fire, उपयुक्त- consumed, देहाः bodies), ध्यानेन by meditation, याम shall attain, पदयोः of the feet, पदवीम् the place/site, सखे O friend, ते Your

Anvaya: अङ्ग सखे! हासावलोककलगीतजहृच्छयाग्निं नः त्वदधरामृतपूरकेण सिञ्च। नो चेत् विरहजाग्न्युपयुक्तदेहाः वयं ध्यानेन ते पदयोः पदवीं याम।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अङ्ग सखे! O dear friend! हासावलोककलगीतजहृच्छयाग्निं नः (the fire of desire) in our hearts, born of Your smiling glance and melodious song, त्वदधरामृतपूरकेण सिञ्च extinguish with the flood of the nectar of Your lips. नो चेत् Otherwise, विरहजाग्न्युपयुक्तदेहाः वयं our bodies having been consumed by the fire born of separation from You, ध्यानेन by meditation, ते पदयोः पदवीं याम we shall attain the place of Your lotus feet.

Simple Meaning: O dear friend, Your smiling glances and the sweet music of Your flute have ignited a fire of longing in our hearts. Please extinguish this fire with the nectar of Your lips. If You do not, our bodies will be burnt up by the fire of separation from You, and we will be forced to reach the shelter of Your feet only through meditation.

यर्ह्यम्बुजाक्ष तव पादतलं रमाया दत्तक्षणं क्वचिदरण्यजनप्रियस्य ।

अस्प्राक्ष्म तत्प्रभृति नान्यसमक्षमङ्ग स्थातुं त्वयाभिरमिता बत पारयामः ॥10.29.35॥

Word-by-word meaning: यर्हि when, अम्बुज-अक्ष O lotus-eyed one, तव Your, पाद-तलम् the soles of the feet, रमायाः of the Goddess of Fortune, दत्त-क्षणम् providing a festival/happiness, क्वचित् sometimes, अरण्य-जन-प्रियस्य who are dear to the forest dwellers, अस्प्राक्ष्म we have touched, तत्-प्रभृति since then, न not, अन्य-समक्षम् in the presence of others, अङ्ग O dear one, स्थातुम् to stand, त्वया by You, अभिरमिताः having been enjoyed, बत indeed, पारयामः we are able

क्षणम् - also means A festival, joy, delight. Src, Kosha Ashtadhyayi

Anvaya: अङ्ग अम्बुजाक्ष! अरण्यजनप्रियस्य तव रमायाः दत्तक्षणं पादतलं यर्हि क्वचित् अस्प्राक्ष्म, तत्प्रभृति त्वया अभिरमिताः वयम् अन्यसमक्षं स्थातुं न पारयामः बत।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अङ्ग अम्बुजाक्ष! O dear lotus-eyed one! अरण्यजनप्रियस्य तव (of You) who are dear to the forest dwellers, रमायाः दत्तक्षणं पादतलं the soles of Your feet, which provide a festival of joy for the Goddess of Fortune, यर्हि क्वचित् अस्प्राक्ष्म since the moment we have touched them, तत्प्रभृति from that time onwards, त्वया अभिरमिताः वयम् we, who have been enjoyed by You (since we touched your feet - it is same as You having touched us - is the implied meaning), अन्यसमक्षं स्थातुं न पारयामः बत are indeed no longer able to stand in the presence of any other man.

Simple Meaning: O lotus-eyed Krishna, You are so dear to the people of this forest. Ever since we touched the soles of Your feet—which are the source of all happiness even for Goddess Lakshmi—we have felt so much joy in Your company that we can no longer bear to stand in the presence of any other person. Having been touched and enjoyed by You, we simply cannot return to our ordinary lives.

श्रीर्यत्पादाम्बुजरजश्चकमे तुलस्या लब्ध्वापि वक्षसि पदं किल भृत्यजुष्टम् ।

यस्याः स्ववीक्षणकृतेऽन्यसुरप्रयासस्तद्वद् वयं च तव पादरजः प्रपन्नाः ॥10.29.37॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्रीः the Goddess of Fortune, यत्-पाद-अम्बुज-रजः the dust of whose lotus feet, चकमे desired, तुलस्या with Tulasi, लब्ध्वा having obtained, अपि although, वक्षसि on the chest, पदम् a place, किल indeed, भृत्य-जुष्टम् served by Your devotees, यस्याः for whose, स्व-वीक्षण-कृते for the sake of her glance, अन्य-सुर-प्रयासः the endeavor of other demigods, तत्-वत् in that same way, वयम् we, च also, तव Your, पाद-रजः the dust of the feet, प्रपन्नाः have sought shelter

Anvaya: भृत्यजुष्टं वक्षसि पदं लब्ध्वा अपि श्रीः तुलस्या (सह) यत्पादाम्बुजरजः चकमे, यस्याः स्ववीक्षणकृते अन्यसुरप्रयासः (भवति), तद्वद् वयं च तव पादरजः प्रपन्नाः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: भृत्यजुष्टं वक्षसि पदं लब्ध्वा अपि Although she has obtained a place on Your chest, which is served by Your devotees, श्रीः the Goddess of Fortune, तुलस्या (सह) यत्पादाम्बुजरजः चकमे desired the dust of Your lotus feet along with the Tulasi leaves, यस्याः स्ववीक्षणकृते for whose glance, अन्यसुरप्रयासः (भवति) other demigods make great efforts, तद्वद् in that same way, वयं च we also, तव पादरजः प्रपन्नाः have sought shelter of the dust of Your feet.

Simple Meaning: Even the Goddess of Fortune – who occupies a permanent place of honor on Your chest and for whose mere glance the demigods perform great penances – desired to obtain the dust of Your lotus feet alongside the Tulasi leaves. Just like her, we have also come to seek shelter of the dust of Your lotus feet.

तन्नः प्रसीद वृजिनार्दन तेऽङ्घ्रिमूलं प्राप्ता विसृज्य वसतीस्त्वदुपासनाशाः ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: वृजिनार्दन! O destroyer of all suffering! तत् नः प्रसीद therefore, please be merciful to us. त्वदुपासनाशाः वसतीः विसृज्य having abandoned our homes with the hope of worshiping You, ते अङ्घ्रीमूलं (वयम्) प्राप्ताः we have arrived at the base of Your feet. पुरुषभूषण! O ornament of all men! त्वत्सुन्दरस्मितनिरीक्षणतीव्रकामतप्तात्मनां (नः) to us, whose souls are burning with intense longing caused by Your beautiful smiles and glances, दास्यं देहि please grant the position of Your maidservants.

Simple Meaning: O Krishna, Destroyer of all distress, please be merciful to us! We have left our homes and families behind, driven only by the hope of serving You, and have now reached the shelter of Your feet. O jewel among men, our hearts are burning with an intense desire ignited by Your beautiful, smiling glances. Please accept us and grant us the simple position of being Your maidservants.

का स्त्र्यङ्ग ते कलपदायतमूर्च्छितेन सम्मोहिताऽऽर्यचरितान्न चलेत्त्रिलोक्याम् ।

त्रैलोक्यसौभगमिदं च निरीक्ष्य रूपं यद् गोद्विजद्रुममृगाः पुलकान्यबिभ्रन् ॥10.29.40॥

Word-by-word meaning: का which, स्त्री woman, अङ्ग O dear one, ते Your, कल-पद-आयत-मूर्च्छितेन by the melodious, long-drawn, rising and falling notes of the flute, सम्मोहिता completely enchanted, आर्य-चरितात् from the path of civilized conduct, न चलेत् would not deviate, त्रि-लोक्याम् in the three worlds, त्रैलोक्य-सौभगम् the fortune of the three worlds, इदम् this, च and, निरीक्ष्य observing, रूपम् form, यत् which, गो-द्विज-द्रुम-मृगाः the cows, birds, trees, and deer, पुलकानि hairs standing on end/shivers of joy, अबिभ्रन् have borne

Anvaya: अङ्ग! त्रिलोक्यां का स्त्री ते कलपदायतमूर्च्छितेन (वेणुगीतेन) सम्मोहिता (सती) आर्यचरितात् न चलेत्? यत् इदं त्रैलोक्यसौभगं रूपं निरीक्ष्य गोद्विजद्रुममृगाः (अपि) पुलकानि अबिभ्रन्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अङ्ग! O dear one! त्रिलोक्यां का स्त्री In the three worlds, which woman, ते कलपदायतमूर्च्छितेन (वेणुगीतेन) सम्मोहिता (सती) being completely enchanted by the melodious, long-drawn notes of Your flute, आर्यचरितात् न चलेत् would not deviate from the path of righteous conduct? यत् इदं त्रैलोक्यसौभगं रूपं निरीक्ष्य seeing this form of Yours, which is the beauty and fortune of the three worlds, गोद्विजद्रुममृगाः (अपि) even the cows, birds, trees, and deer, पुलकानि अबिभ्रन् have hairs standing on end out of ecstasy.

Simple Meaning: O dear Krishna, which woman in all the three worlds would not be strayed from her traditional path of duty after being completely enchanted by the sweet, vibrating music of Your flute? Your beauty is the crowning fortune of the entire universe—so much so that even the cows, birds, trees, and deer of the forest tremble with joy and show physical signs of ecstasy just by looking at You.

व्यक्तं भवान् व्रजभयार्तिहरोऽभिजातो देवो यथाऽऽदिपुरुषः सुरलोकगोप्ता ।

तन्नो निधेहि करपङ्कजमार्तबन्धो तप्तस्तनेषु च शिरस्सु च किङ्करीणाम् ॥10.29.41॥

Word-by-word meaning: व्यक्तम् evidently, भवान् You, व्रज-भय-आर्ति-हरः the remover of the fear and distress of Vraja, अभिजातः have taken birth, देवः the Lord, यथा just as, आदि-पुरुषः the Primeval Lord, सुर-लोक-गोप्ता the protector of the world of the demigods, तत् therefore, नः our, निधेहि please place, कर-पङ्कजम् lotus hand, आर्त-बन्धो O friend of the distressed, तप्त-स्तनेषु on the burning breasts, च and, शिरस्सु on the heads, च and, किङ्करीणाम् of Your maidservants

Anvaya: यथा आदिपुरुषः देवः सुरलोकगोप्ता (तथा) भवान् व्यक्तं व्रजभयार्तिहरः अभिजातः। आर्तबन्धो! तत् नः किङ्करीणां तप्तस्तनेषु शिरस्सु च करपङ्कजं निधेहि।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यथा आदिपुरुषः देवः सुरलोकगोप्ता Just as the Primeval Lord is the protector of the realm of the demigods, (तथा) भवान् व्यक्तं व्रजभयार्तिहरः अभिजातः (so) You have evidently appeared as the remover of the fear and suffering of Vraja. आर्तबन्धो! O friend of the distressed! तत् नः किङ्करीणां therefore, on us, Your maidservants, तप्तस्तनेषु शिरस्सु च on our burning breasts and on our heads, करपङ्कजं निधेहि please place Your lotus hand.

Simple Meaning: It is evident that just as the Primeval Lord Vishnu appears to protect the demigods, You have taken birth here to remove the fear and suffering of the people of Vraja. O friend of the afflicted, we are Your maidservants, and our hearts are burning with longing for You. Please, place Your cooling lotus hand upon our heads and our aching breasts to soothe our pain.

श्री शुक उवाच

इति विक्लवितं तासां श्रुत्वा योगेश्वरेश्वरः ।

प्रहस्य सदयं गोपीरात्मारामोऽप्यरीरमत् ॥10.29.42॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, इति thus, विक्लवितम् the pitiable lamentation, तासाम् of them (the gopis), श्रुत्वा having heard, योग-ईश्वर-ईश्वरः the Lord of the lords of mystic power, प्रहस्य laughing, स-दयम् mercifully, गोपीः the gopis, आत्मा-रामः although self-satisfied, अपि even, अरीरमत् He gave delight/reciprocated

Anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच - इति तासां विक्लवितं श्रुत्वा योगेश्वरेश्वरः आत्मारामः अपि प्रहस्य सदयं गोपीः अरीरमत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, इति तासां विक्लवितं श्रुत्वा having heard these pitiable words of theirs, योगेश्वरेश्वरः the Lord of the lords of all mystic power, आत्मारामः अपि although perfectly self-satisfied within Himself, प्रहस्य laughing, सदयं गोपीः अरीरमत् mercifully began to delight the gopis.

Simple Meaning: Shri Shuka said: After hearing this pitiable lamentation of the gopis, Krishna, the Supreme Lord of all mystic masters, smiled mercifully. Although He is completely self-satisfied and lacks nothing, He began to delight them out of His great compassion.

ताभिः समेताभिरुदारचेष्टितः प्रियेक्षणोत्फुल्लमुखीभिरच्युतः।

उदारहासद्विजकुन्ददीधितिर्व्यरोचतैणाङ्क इवोडुभिर्वृतः ॥10.29.43॥

Word-by-word meaning: ताभिः with them, समेताभिः who were assembled, उदार-चेष्टितः whose activities are magnificent, प्रिय-ईक्षण-उत्फुल्ल-मुखीभिः whose faces blossomed due to the sight of their Beloved, अच्युतः the Infallible Lord, उदार-हास-द्विज-कुन्द-दीधितिः whose brilliance of teeth was like jasmine buds revealed by His broad smile, व्यरोचत shone, एण-अङ्कः one which has a mark of a black antelope on it - the moon, इव like, उडुभिः by stars, वृतः surrounded

एणः   एणः, एकणः 1 A kind of black antelope;  src. Kosha Ashtadhyayi

Anvaya: उदारचेष्टितः अच्युतः प्रियेक्षणोत्फुल्लमुखीभिः समेताभिः ताभिः वृतः, उदारहासद्विजकुन्ददीधितिः (सन्) उडुभिः वृतः एणाङ्कः इव व्यरोचत।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: उदारचेष्टितः अच्युतः The Infallible Lord, whose activities are magnificent, प्रियेक्षणोत्फुल्लमुखीभिः समेताभिः ताभिः वृतः surrounded by those assembled gopis whose faces blossomed at the sight of their Beloved, उदारहासद्विजकुन्ददीधितिः (सन्) being possessed of the brilliance of teeth like jasmine buds shown through His broad smile, उडुभिः वृतः एणाङ्कः इव व्यरोचत shone like the moon surrounded by the stars.

Simple Meaning: Lord Krishna, whose pastimes are all-magnificent, stood in the midst of the gopis, whose faces blossomed with joy just by looking at Him. As He smiled broadly, His teeth gleamed like jasmine buds. In that assembly, the Infallible Lord appeared just like the moon surrounded by a cluster of shining stars.

उपगीयमान उद्गायन् वनिताशतयूथपः ।

मालां बिभ्रद् वैजयन्तीं व्यचरन्मण्डयन् वनम् ॥10.29.44॥

Word-by-word meaning: उपगीयमानः being sung about, उद्गायन् singing loudly, वनिता-शत-यूथपः the leader of many hundreds of women, मालाम् a garland, बिभ्रत् wearing, वैजयन्तीम् the Vaijayanti garland, व्यचरत् He moved about, मण्डयन् beautifying, वनम् the forest

Anvaya: वनिताशतयूथपः उपगीयमानः उद्गायन् (च) वैजयन्तीं मालां बिभ्रत् वनं मण्डयन् व्यचरत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: वनिताशतयूथपः The leader of hundreds of women, उपगीयमानः while being sung about by them, उद्गायन् (च) and Himself singing loudly, वैजयन्तीं मालां बिभ्रत् wearing a Vaijayanti garland, वनं मण्डयन् beautifying the forest, व्यचरत् moved about.

Simple Meaning: As the leader of hundreds of young women, Krishna moved through the forest, making it even more beautiful. While the gopis sang His glories, He also sang loudly, wearing His famous Vaijayanti garland. Together, they wandered through the woods, filling the atmosphere with divine music and grace.

नद्याः पुलिनमाविश्य गोपीभिर्हिमवालुकम् ।

रेमे तत्तरलानन्दकुमुदामोदवायुना ॥10.29.45॥

Word-by-word meaning: नद्याः of the river (Yamuna), पुलिनम् the sandy bank, आविश्य entering, गोपीभिः with the gopis, हिम-वालुकम् having cool sands, रेमे He enjoyed, तत्-तरल-आनन्द-कुमुद-आमोद-वायुना by the breeze carrying the fragrance of the joyful, waving lilies of that river

Anvaya: (अच्युतः) गोपीभिः (सह) नद्याः हिमवालुकं पुलिनम् आविश्य तत्तरलानन्दकुमुदामोदवायुना रेमे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (अच्युतः) The Infallible Lord, गोपीभिः (सह) along with the gopis, नद्याः हिमवालुकं पुलिनम् आविश्य having entered the sandy bank of the river which was filled with cool sands, तत्तरलानन्दकुमुदामोदवायुना by the breeze carrying the fragrance of the joyful, waving lilies of that river, रेमे He enjoyed.

Simple Meaning: Lord Krishna, accompanied by the gopis, went to the bank of the Yamuna River, where the sands were pleasantly cool. There, in an atmosphere filled with a breeze carrying the sweet fragrance of blossoming night lilies, He began to enjoy His pastimes with them.

बाहुप्रसारपरिरम्भकरालकोरुनीवीस्तनालभननर्मनखाग्रपातैः ।

क्ष्वेल्यावलोकहसितैर्व्रजसुन्दरीणामुत्तम्भयन् रतिपतिं रमयाञ्चकार ॥10.29.46॥

Word-by-word meaning: बाहु-प्रसार-परिरम्भ-कर-अलक-ऊरु-नीवी-स्तन-आलभन-नर्म-नख-अग्र-पातैः by stretching out His arms to embrace, by touching their hands, hair, thighs, waist-knots, and breasts, by playful scratching with the tips of His fingernails, क्ष्वेल्य-अवलोक-हसितैः by joking conversations, glances, and laughter, व्रज-सुन्दरीणाम् of the beautiful women of Vraja, उत्तम्भयन् stimulating, रति-पतिम् the lord of love (Kamadeva), रमयाञ्चकार He gave them pleasure/He sported

Anvaya: बाहुप्रसारपरिरम्भकरालकोरुनीवीस्तनालभननर्मनखाग्रपातैः क्ष्वेल्यावलोकहसितैः (च) व्रजसुन्दरीणां रतिपतिम् उत्तम्भयन् रमयाञ्चकार।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: बाहुप्रसारपरिरम्भकरालकोरुनीवीस्तनालभननर्मनखाग्रपातैः By stretching out His arms, embracing them, and touching their hands, locks of hair, thighs, waist-knots, and breasts, and by playfully scratching them with His fingernails, क्ष्वेल्यावलोकहसितैः (च) as well as through His joking, glances, and laughter, व्रजसुन्दरीणां रतिपतिम् उत्तम्भयन् stimulating the god of love within the beautiful damsels of Vraja, रमयाञ्चकार He sported.

Simple Meaning: Krishna gave pleasure to the beautiful damsels of Vraja by embracing them, touching their hands, hair, and bodies, and playfully scratching them with His fingernails. Through His charming jokes, affectionate glances, and sweet laughter, He stimulated the feelings of love within them and enjoyed divine pastimes in their company.

एवं भगवतः कृष्णाल्लब्धमाना महात्मनः ।

आत्मानं मेनिरे स्त्रीणां मानिन्योऽभ्यधिकं भुवि ॥10.29.47॥

Word-by-word meaning: एवम् in this way, भगवतः from the Supreme Lord, कृष्णात् from Krishna, लब्ध-मानाः having received such honor, महा-आत्मनः the Great Soul, आत्मानम् themselves, मेनिरे they considered, स्त्रीणाम् of all women, मानिन्यः being proud, अभ्यधिकम् the best/superior, भुवि on the earth

Anvaya: एवं महात्मनः भगवतः कृष्णात् लब्धमानाः (ताः) मानिन्यः भुवि स्त्रीणाम् आत्मानम् अभ्यधिकं मेनिरे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: एवं महात्मनः भगवतः कृष्णात् In this way, from the Great Soul, the Supreme Lord Krishna, लब्धमानाः (ताः) having received such great honor, मानिन्यः those gopis became proud and, भुवि स्त्रीणाम् आत्मानम् अभ्यधिकं मेनिरे considered themselves to be the best among all women on the earth.

Simple Meaning: Having received such extraordinary honor from the Supreme Lord Krishna, the Great Soul, the gopis began to feel a sense of pride. They considered themselves the most fortunate and superior among all women on the entire earth.

तासां तत् सौभगमदं वीक्ष्य मानं च केशवः।

प्रशमाय प्रसादाय तत्रैवान्तरधीयत ॥10.29.48॥

Word-by-word meaning: तासाम् of them (the gopis), तत् that, सौभग-मदम् pride in their great fortune, वीक्ष्य seeing, मानम् false pride/conceit, च and, केशवः Lord Krishna, प्रशमाय for the sake of diminishing, प्रसादाय for showing favor, तत्र एव right there, अन्तरधीयत disappeared

Anvaya: तासां तत् सौभगमदं मानं च वीक्ष्य, केशवः (तयोः) प्रशमाय प्रसादाय (च) तत्र एव अन्तरधीयत।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तासां तत् सौभगमदं मानं च वीक्ष्य Seeing that pride of theirs born of their great fortune and their conceit, केशवः Lord Krishna, (तयोः) प्रशमाय for the sake of diminishing those traits, प्रसादाय (च) and for showing them ultimate favor, तत्र एव अन्तरधीयत disappeared from that very spot.

Simple Meaning: Lord Krishna, seeing that the gopis were becoming proud of their great fortune and were developing a sense of conceit, decided to act. To dispel this pride and to ultimately show them even greater favor, He suddenly disappeared from their sight right then and there.

इति श्रीमद्भागवते महापुराणे पारमहंस्यां सहितायां दशमे स्कन्धे पूर्वार्धे “भगवतो रासक्रीडावर्णनं नाम” एकोनत्रिंशः अध्यायः ॥29॥

Thus ends the twenty-ninth discourse named 'A description of the (celebrated) Rasa Play (of the Lord)' in the first half of Book Ten of the great and glorious Bhagavata-Purana.
