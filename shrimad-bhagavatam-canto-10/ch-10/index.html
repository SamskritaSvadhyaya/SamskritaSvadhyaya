Chapter 10

The curse of Narada on the sons of Kubera and their deliverance (who were in the form of the two Arjuna Trees)

श्रीमद्भागवतमहापुराणम्

दशमः स्कन्धः

नारदशापो नाम दशमः अध्यायः

(43 Shlokas)

राजा उवाच

कथ्यतां भगवन्नेतत्तयोः शापस्य कारणम् ।

यत्तद् विगर्हितं कर्म येन वा देवर्षेस्तमः ॥10.10.1॥

Word-by-word meaning: राजा उवाच the king said, कथ्यताम् please tell, भगवन् O great one/O sage, एतत् this, तयोः of those two, शापस्य of the curse, कारणम् the reason/cause, यत् what, तत् that, विगर्हितम् condemned/reproachable, कर्म deed/action, येन by which, वा or, देवर्षेः of the divine sage Narada, तमः anger/wrath

Anvaya: राजा उवाच — भगवन्! एतत् तयोः शापस्य कारणं कथ्यतां, यत् तत् विगर्हितं कर्म (आसीत्), येन वा देवर्षेः तमः (अभवत्)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: राजा उवाच The king Parikshit said — भगवन् O great sage, एतत् तयोः शापस्य कारणं कथ्यतां  Please narrate the reason for the curse of those two, यत् तत् विगर्हितं कर्म (आसीत्) what was that reproachable deed they committed, येन वा देवर्षेः तमः (अभवत्) by which the anger of the divine sage Narada was provoked.

Simple meaning: King Parikshit said — O great sage Shukadeva, please narrate the reason behind the curse given to those two. What was the reproachable deed they committed, and what was it that provoked the wrath of the divine sage Narada?

श्री शुक उवाच

रुद्रस्यानुचरौ भूत्वा सुदृप्तौ धनदात्मजौ ।

कैलासोपवने रम्ये मन्दाकिन्यां मदोत्कटौ ॥10.10.2॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुक उवाच Shri Shuka said, रुद्रस्य of Rudra/Shiva, अनुचरौ attendants/companions, भूत्वा having become, सु-दृप्तौ very proud/arrogant, धनद-आत्मजौ the two sons of Kubera, कैलास-उपवने in the garden/grove near Kailasa, रम्ये beautiful/pleasant, मन्दाकिन्याम् in the river Mandakini, मद-उत्कटौ intensely intoxicated/overcome with pride

Anvaya: श्री शुक उवाच — धनदात्मजौ रुद्रस्य अनुचरौ भूत्वा, सुदृप्तौ, रम्ये कैलासोपवने मन्दाकिन्यां मदोत्कटौ (क्रीडन्तौ आस्ताम्)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री शुक उवाच Shri Shuka said — धनदात्मजौ The two sons of Kubera, रुद्रस्य अनुचरौ भूत्वा having become attendants of Rudra/Shiva, सुदृप्तौ became very proud. रम्ये कैलासोपवने (Once) in the beautiful grove near Kailasa, मन्दाकिन्यां in the river Mandakini, मदोत्कटौ intensely overcome with intoxication, (क्रीडन्तौ आस्ताम्) were sporting.

Simple meaning: Shri Shuka said — The two sons of Kubera, Nalakubara and Manigriva, having become attendants of Lord Shiva, were filled with great pride. Once intensely overcome with intoxication they were sporting in the river Mandakini in the beautiful grove near Kailasa.

Note: The holy Ganga, while flowing in the heavenly regions; goes by the name of Mandakini.

वारुणीं मदिरां पीत्वा मदाघूर्णितलोचनौ ।

स्त्रीजनैरनुगायद्भिश्चेरतुः पुष्पिते वने ॥10.10.3॥

Word-by-word meaning: वारुणीम् Varuni/a type of intoxicating drink, मदिराम् liquor/wine, पीत्वा having drunk, मद-आघूर्णित-लोचनौ with eyes rolling with intoxication, स्त्री-जनैः with women, अनुगायद्भिः who were singing along, चेरतुः they wandered/roamed, पुष्पिते in the blooming/flowering, वने in the forest

Anvaya: वारुणीं मदिरां पीत्वा, मदाघूर्णितलोचनौ (तौ), अनुगायद्भिः स्त्रीजनैः (सह) पुष्पिते वने चेरतुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: वारुणीं मदिरां पीत्वा Having drunk the Varuni liquor, मदाघूर्णितलोचनौ with their eyes rolling with intoxication, अनुगायद्भिः स्त्रीजनैः (सह) accompanied by women who were singing along, पुष्पिते वने in the blooming forest, चेरतुः they wandered about.

Simple meaning: Having drunk the intoxicating Varuni liquor, with their eyes rolling from intoxication, the two sons of Kubera wandered about in the flowering forest accompanied by women who were singing along with them.

अन्तः प्रविश्य गङ्गायामम्भोजवनराजिनि ।

चिक्रीडतुर्युवतिभिर्गजाविव करेणुभिः ॥10.10.4॥

Word-by-word meaning: अन्तः inside, प्रविश्य having entered, गङ्गायाम् in the Ganga, अम्भोज-वन-राजिनि adorned with rows of clusters of lotuses, चिक्रीडतुः they sported/played, युवतिभिः with young women, गजौ two elephants, इव like, करेणुभिः with female elephants

Anvaya: अम्भोजवनराजिनि गङ्गायाम् अन्तः प्रविश्य, युवतिभिः (तौ) – करेणुभिः गजौ इव – चिक्रीडतुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अम्भोजवनराजिनि गङ्गायाम् अन्तः प्रविश्य Having entered into the Ganga adorned with rows of clusters of lotuses, युवतिभिः with young women, (तौ) they both –  करेणुभिः with female elephants, गजौ इव like two elephants – चिक्रीडतुः sported and played.

Simple meaning: Having entered into the Ganga which was adorned with beautiful rows of clusters of lotuses, the two of them sported and played with the young women just as two mighty elephants frolicking with their female companions.

यदृच्छया च देवर्षिर्भगवांस्तत्र कौरव ।

अपश्यन्नारदो देवौ क्षीबाणौ समबुध्यत ॥10.10.5॥

Word-by-word meaning: यदृच्छया by chance/unexpectedly, च and, देवर्षिः the divine sage, भगवान् the revered one, तत्र there, कौरव O descendant of the Kurus, अपश्यत् saw, नारदः Narada, देवौ the two demigods, क्षीबाणौ intoxicated/inebriated, समबुध्यत understood/perceived

Anvaya: कौरव! यदृच्छया तत्र भगवान् देवर्षिः नारदः देवौ अपश्यत्, क्षीबाणौ समबुध्यत च।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: कौरव O descendant of the Kurus! यदृच्छया By chance, तत्र there arriving, भगवान् देवर्षिः नारदः the revered divine sage Narada, देवौ the two demigods, अपश्यत् saw, क्षीबाणौ समबुध्यत च and fully understood them to be intoxicated.

Simple meaning: O descendant of the Kurus, the revered divine sage Narada happened to arrive there by chance and saw the two demigods — and he understood that they were intoxicated.

तं दृष्ट्वा व्रीडिता देव्यो विवस्त्राः शापशङ्किताः ।

वासांसि पर्यधुः शीघ्रं विवस्त्रौ नैव गुह्यकौ ॥10.10.6॥

Word-by-word meaning: तम् him/Narada, दृष्ट्वा having seen, व्रीडिताः ashamed/embarrassed, देव्यः the divine women, विवस्त्राः unclothed, शाप-शङ्किताः fearful of a curse, वासांसि clothes/garments, पर्यधुः put on/wore, शीघ्रम् quickly, विवस्त्रौ unclothed, न not, एव indeed, गुह्यकौ the two yakshas

Anvaya: विवस्त्राः देव्यः तं दृष्ट्वा व्रीडिताः शापशङ्किताः (सत्यः) शीघ्रं वासांसि पर्यधुः। गुह्यकौ तु विवस्त्रौ एव (आस्ताम्), न (पर्यधुः)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: विवस्त्राः देव्यः The unclothed women, तं दृष्ट्वा having seen him Narada, व्रीडिताः ashamed, शापशङ्किताः fearful of a curse, शीघ्रं वासांसि पर्यधुः quickly put on their garments. गुह्यकौ तु The two yakshas however, विवस्त्रौ एव (आस्ताम्) remained unclothed indeed, न (पर्यधुः) and did not put on their garments.

Simple meaning: The unclothed women, upon seeing Narada, were overcome with shame and fearing a curse quickly put on their garments. However the two yakshas, in their extreme intoxication, remained unclothed and did not bother to cover themselves.

तौ दृष्ट्वा मदिरामत्तौ श्रीमदान्धौ सुरात्मजौ ।

तयोरनुग्रहार्थाय शापं दास्यन्निदं जगौ ॥10.10.7॥

Word-by-word meaning: तौ those two, दृष्ट्वा having seen, मदिरा-मत्तौ intoxicated with liquor, श्री-मद-अन्धौ blinded by the pride of wealth, सुर-आत्मजौ sons of a demigod, तयोः of those two, अनुग्रह-अर्थाय for the purpose of bestowing grace/blessing, शापम् curse, दास्यन् giving, इदम् this, जगौ spoke

Anvaya: मदिरामत्तौ श्रीमदान्धौ तौ सुरात्मजौ दृष्ट्वा, (नारदः) तयोः अनुग्रहार्थाय शापं दास्यन् इदं जगौ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: मदिरामत्तौ श्रीमदान्धौ तौ सुरात्मजौ दृष्ट्वा Having seen those two sons of a demigod intoxicated with liquor and blinded by the pride of wealth, (नारदः) Narada, तयोः अनुग्रहार्थाय for the purpose of bestowing grace upon them, शापं दास्यन् giving a curse, इदं जगौ spoke thus.

Simple meaning: Having seen those two sons of a demigod — blinded by the pride of wealth and intoxicated with liquor — Narada, with the intention of actually bestowing grace and blessing upon them, spoke the following words as he prepared to curse them.

नारद उवाच

न ह्यन्यो जुषतो जोष्यान् बुद्धिभ्रंशो रजोगुणः ।

श्रीमदादाभिजात्यादिर्यत्र स्त्री द्यूतमासवः ॥10.10.8॥

Word-by-word meaning: नारद उवाच Narada said, न not, हि indeed, अन्यः other, जुषतः while enjoying, जोष्यान् the one enjoying the pleasures, बुद्धि-भ्रंशः destruction of intelligence, रजो-गुणः the mode of passion, श्री-मदात् from the pride of wealth, आभिजात्य-आदिः being born in a good family and so on, यत्र where, स्त्री women, द्यूतम् gambling, आसवः liquor/intoxicating drinks

Anvaya: नारद उवाच — जोष्यान् जुषतः श्रीमदात् अन्यः आभिजात्यादिः बुद्धिभ्रंशः रजोगुणः न हि, यत्र स्त्री, द्यूतम्, आसवः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नारद उवाच Narada said — जोष्यान् जुषतः For the one indulging  in objects of enjoyment, श्रीमदात् अन्यः apart from the pride of wealth, आभिजात्यादिः and being born in an esteemed family, बुद्धिभ्रंशः रजोगुणः the mode of passion that destroys intelligence, न हि there is no other reason, यत्र where (there is wealth and position, there are), स्त्री women, द्यूतम् gambling, आसवः liquor.

Simple meaning: Narada said — For one who indulges in objects of sensual enjoyment, apart from the pride of wealth and being born in a powerful family is the mode of passion that destroys the intelligence — there is no other cause. And wherever there is pride of wealth, there are women, gambling and liquor.

हन्यन्ते पशवो यत्र निर्दयैरजितात्मभिः।

मन्यमानैरिमं देहमजरामृत्यु नश्वरम् ॥10.10.9॥

Word-by-word meaning: हन्यन्ते are killed/slaughtered, पशवः animals, यत्र where, निर्दयैः by merciless ones, अ-जित-आत्मभिः by those who have not conquered their senses/self, मन्य-मानैः by those who consider, इमम् this, देहम् body, अ-जर-अ-मृत्युः not subject to old age and death, नश्वरम् perishable

Anvaya: यत्र निर्दयैः अजितात्मभिः, इमं नश्वरं देहम् अजरामृत्युः मन्यमानैः पशवः हन्यन्ते।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यत्र Where, निर्दयैः अजितात्मभिः by those merciless ones who have not conquered their senses, इमं नश्वरं देहम् this perishable body, अजरामृत्युः not subject to old age and death, मन्यमानैः considering, पशवः animals, हन्यन्ते slaughter.

Simple meaning: Where merciless people who have not conquered their senses, considering this perishable body to be free from old age and death, slaughter innocent animals for their pleasure.

देवसंज्ञितमप्यन्ते कृमिविड्भस्मसंज्ञितम् ।

भूतध्रुक् तत्कृते स्वार्थं किं वेद निरयो यतः ॥10.10.10॥

Word-by-word meaning: देव-संज्ञितम् known as/called a deva/god, अपि even, अन्ते in the end, कृमि-विड्-भस्म-संज्ञितम् known as worm, stool or ashes, भूत-ध्रुक् one who is cruel to other living beings, तत्-कृते for the sake of that (body), स्व-अर्थम् his own true interest, किम् what, वेद knows, निरयः hell, यतः from which/wherefrom

Anvaya: देवसंज्ञितम् अपि अन्ते कृमिविड्भस्मसंज्ञितं (भवति)। तत्कृते भूतध्रुक् (जनः) स्वार्थं किं वेद, यतः निरयः (एव तस्य गतिः भवति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: देवसंज्ञितम् अपि Even the body called that of a deva/god, अन्ते in the end, कृमिविड्भस्मसंज्ञितं (भवति) becomes known as worm, stool or ashes. तत्कृते For the sake of that body, भूतध्रुक् (जनः) one who is cruel to other living beings, स्वार्थं किं वेद what does he know of his own true interest? यतः निरयः (एव तस्य गतिः भवति) Since hell alone becomes his destination.

Simple meaning: Even a body that is called divine ultimately ends up as worms, stool or ashes. What does a person who is cruel to other living beings for the sake of such a body truly know of his own real interest — for hell alone awaits him in the end.

देहः किमन्नदातुः स्वं निषेक्तुर्मातुरेव च ।

मातुः पितुर्वा बलिनः क्रेतुरग्नेः शुनोऽपि वा ॥10.10.11॥

Word-by-word meaning: देहः the body, किम् whose, अन्न-दातुः of the one who feeds/provides food, स्वम् own, निषेक्तुः of the one who sows the seed/father, मातुः of the mother, एव indeed, च and, मातुः of the mother, पितुः of the father, वा or, बलिनः of the powerful one who takes it by force, क्रेतुः of the one who purchases/buys it, अग्नेः of the fire, शुनः of the dog, अपि also, वा or

Anvaya: देहः किं स्वम्? (किम्) अन्नदातुः, निषेक्तुः, मातुः एव च, (किं) मातुः पितुः वा, बलिनः, क्रेतुः, अग्नेः, शुनः अपि वा?

Literal meaning of the anvaya: देहः किं स्वम् Whose is this body really? (किम्) अन्नदातुः Is it of the one who feeds it, निषेक्तुः of the father who sows the seed, मातुः एव च or of the mother, (किं) मातुः पितुः वा or of the mother's father (because sometimes the father of the mother takes a grandson as an adopted son), बलिनः of the powerful one who seizes it by force, क्रेतुः of the one who purchases it, अग्नेः of the fire that will consume it, शुनः अपि वा or even of the dog (that may devour it)?

Simple meaning: To whom does this body really belong? Is it of the one who feeds it, or of the father who begot it, or of the mother who bore it, or of the mother's father, or of the powerful one who seizes it by force, or of the one who buys it, or of the fire that will ultimately consume it, or even of the dog that may devour it? In truth this body belongs to no one — so what is the value of pride based on such a body?

एवं साधारणं देहमव्यक्तप्रभवाप्ययम् ।

को विद्वानात्मसात् कृत्वा हन्ति जन्तूनृतेऽसतः ॥10.10.12॥

Word-by-word meaning: एवम् thus, साधारणम् belonging to no one in particular, देहम् the body, अव्यक्त-प्रभव-अप्ययम् whose origin and dissolution are in the unmanifest, कः who, विद्वान् truly wise/learned, आत्मसात् कृत्वा claiming as one's own, हन्ति kills/slaughters, जन्तून् living beings, ऋते except, असतः one who is ignorant/unwise

अप्ययम्—3 Entrance into, vanishing, disappearance; absorption, dissolution into oneself. Src. Kosha Ashtadhyayi

Anvaya: एवं साधारणम् अव्यक्तप्रभवाप्ययं देहम् आत्मसात् कृत्वा कः विद्वान् जन्तून् हन्ति? असतः ऋते।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: एवं Thus, साधारणम् belonging to no one in particular,, अव्यक्तप्रभवाप्ययं whose origin and dissolution both lie in the unmanifest, देहम् this body, आत्मसात् कृत्वा claiming as one's own, कः विद्वान् what wise person, जन्तून् हन्ति would slaughter living beings, असतः ऋते except for the ignorant/unwise.

Simple meaning: This body belongs to no one in particular, and both its origin and its dissolution lie in the unmanifest — knowing this, what truly wise person would claim such a body as his own and slaughter innocent living beings for its sake? None but the ignorant would do so.

असतः श्रीमदान्धस्य दारिद्र्यं परमञ्जनम् ।

आत्मौपम्येन भूतानि दरिद्रः परमीक्षते ॥10.10.13॥

Word-by-word meaning: असतः of the unwise/ignoble one, श्री-मद-अन्धस्य of one blinded by pride of wealth, दारिद्र्यम् poverty, परमम् best/greatest, अञ्जनम् collyrium/eye-salve, आत्म-औपम्येन by comparison with oneself, भूतानि all living beings, दरिद्रः the poor one, परम् truly/rightly, ईक्षते sees/perceives

औपम्य   औपम्यं [उपमा-ष्यञ्] Comparison, resemblance, analogy; src. Kosha Ashtadhyayi

Anvaya: श्रीमदान्धस्य असतः (पुरुषस्य) दारिद्र्यं परमञ्जनम् (अस्ति)। दरिद्रः आत्मौपम्येन भूतानि परम् ईक्षते।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्रीमदान्धस्य असतः (पुरुषस्य) For an ignoble person blinded by pride of wealth, दारिद्र्यं poverty, परमञ्जनम् (अस्ति) is the greatest eye-salve/collyrium. दरिद्रः The poor one, आत्मौपम्येन by comparison with himself, भूतानि all living beings, परम् ईक्षते truly sees and perceives rightly.

Simple meaning: For an ignoble person blinded by pride of wealth, poverty is the greatest eye-salve — for a poor person, comparing all other living beings with himself, truly sees and perceives them rightly with empathy and compassion. In other words, poverty removes the blindness caused by wealth and makes one see others as equal to oneself.

यथा कण्टकविद्धाङ्गो जन्तोर्नेच्छति तां व्यथाम् ।

जीवसाम्यं गतो लिङ्गैर्न तथाविद्धकण्टकः ॥10.10.14॥

Word-by-word meaning: यथा just as, कण्टक-विद्ध-अङ्गः one whose body has been pierced by a thorn, जन्तोः for any living being, न इच्छति does not wish, ताम् that, व्यथाम् pain/suffering, जीव-साम्यम् equality/sameness with all living beings, गतः having attained, लिङ्गैः by the bodily features/signs, न not, तथा in the same way, अ-विद्ध-कण्टकः one who has not been pierced by a thorn

Anvaya: यथा कण्टकविद्धाङ्गः लिङ्गैः जीवसाम्यं गतः जन्तोः तां व्यथां न इच्छति, तथा अविद्धकण्टकः न।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यथा Just as, कण्टकविद्धाङ्गः one whose body has been pierced by a thorn, लिङ्गैः जीवसाम्यं गतः attains equality and sameness with all living beings through bodily experience, जन्तोः तां व्यथां न इच्छति does not wish that same pain for any other living being, तथा अविद्धकण्टकः न one who has not been pierced by a thorn does not attain such sameness in the same way.

Simple meaning: Just as a person whose body has been pierced by a thorn, naturally attains a sense of equality and empathy with all living beings through his own experience of suffering, does not wish that same pain upon any other living being — so too one who has never been pierced by a thorn does not develop such empathy in the same way. The point being that poverty and suffering naturally cultivate compassion and equality of vision.

Literal meaning of the anvaya: निरहंस्तम्भः Free from ego and arrogance, दरिद्रः the poor person, सर्वमदैः मुक्तः liberated from all kinds of pride, इह  in this world, यदृच्छया कृच्छ्रम् आप्नोति whatever hardship he undergoes by chance, तत् हि तस्य परं तपः (अस्ति) that indeed is his supreme austerity.

Simple meaning: A poor person is free from ego and arrogance and becomes liberated from all kinds of pride in this world. Whatever hardship he undergoes by the force of circumstances — that itself becomes his supreme austerity. Thus poverty, though apparently a misfortune, is in reality a great spiritual blessing.

Simple meaning: In the case of a poor person whose body is constantly emaciated by hunger and who longs only for food, the senses naturally become weakened and dried up — and even the tendency towards violence and cruelty naturally ceases in him. Thus poverty involuntarily brings about sense control and non-violence, which are the very foundations of spiritual life.

Literal meaning of the anvaya: समदर्शिनः साधवः Saintly persons of equal vision, दरिद्रस्य एव युज्यन्ते associate with the poor one indeed. सद्भिः (सङ्गेन) By the association of such saintly ones, तं तर्षं क्षिणोति that craving and desire diminishes, ततः आरात् thereafter very soon, विशुद्ध्यति he becomes purified.

Simple meaning: Saintly persons of equal vision naturally associate with the poor — for they find in the poor a receptive and humble soul. Through the association of such saintly persons, the poor person's cravings and desires gradually diminish, and thereafter he very quickly becomes spiritually purified. Thus poverty opens the door to saintly association which leads to swift purification.

Anvaya: नः वाणी तव गुणानुकथने (अस्तु), श्रवणौ (तव) कथायाम् (अस्ताम्), हस्तौ च (तव) कर्मसु (अस्ताम्), मनः तव पादयोः स्मृत्याम् (अस्तु), शिरः तव निवासजगत्प्रणामे (अस्तु), दृष्टिः भवत्तनूनां सतां दर्शने अस्तु।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नः Our, वाणी speech, तव गुणानुकथने (अस्तु) let it be engaged in narrating Your glories, श्रवणौ the two ears, (तव) कथायाम् (अस्ताम्) let them be engaged in hearing Your stories, हस्तौ च the two hands, (तव) कर्मसु (अस्ताम्) let them be engaged in Your service, मनः the mind, तव पादयोः स्मृत्याम् (अस्तु) let it be engaged in remembrance of Your feet, शिरः the head, तव निवासजगत्प्रणामे (अस्तु) let it be engaged in bowing to the world which is Your abode, दृष्टिः our vision, भवत्तनूनां सतां दर्शने अस्तु let it be engaged in beholding the saints who are Your very embodiments.

Simple meaning: Let our speech be engaged in narrating Your glories, let our ears be engaged in hearing Your stories, let our hands be engaged in Your service, let our mind be engaged in remembrance of Your lotus feet, let our head be engaged in bowing to this world which is Your very abode, and let our eyes be engaged in beholding the saints who are Your own embodiments. This is a beautiful prayer of total surrender of all the senses to the Lord.

श्रीशुक उवाच

इत्थं संकीर्तितस्ताभ्यां भगवान् गोकुलेश्वरः ।

दाम्ना चोलूखले बद्धः प्रहसन्नाह गुह्यकौ ॥10.10.39॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुक उवाच Shri Shuka said, इत्थम् thus/in this manner, संकीर्तितः having been glorified/praised, ताभ्याम् by those two, भगवान् the Supreme Lord, गोकुल-ईश्वरः the lord of Gokula, दाम्ना with a rope, च and, उलूखले to the mortar, बद्धः bound, प्रहसन् smiling/laughing, आह said, गुह्यकौ to the two yakshas

Anvaya: श्री शुक उवाच — ताभ्याम् इत्थं संकीर्तितः गोकुलेश्वरः भगवान्, दाम्ना उलूखले बद्धः (सन्) प्रहसन् गुह्यकौ आह।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री शुक उवाच Shri Shuka said — ताभ्याम् इत्थं संकीर्तितः having been glorified thus by those two, गोकुलेश्वरः भगवान् the Supreme Lord the master of Gokula, दाम्ना उलूखले बद्धः (सन्) still bound to the mortar with a rope, प्रहसन् smiling, गुह्यकौ आह spoke to the two yakshas.

Simple meaning: Shri Shuka said — Having been glorified thus by those two, the Supreme Lord — the master of Gokula — still bound to the mortar with a rope, smiled and spoke to the two yakshas.

भगवान् उवाच

ज्ञातं मम पुरैवैतदृषिणा करुणात्मना ।

यच्छ्रीमदान्धयोर्वाग्भिर्विभ्रंशोऽनुग्रहः कृतः ॥10.10.40॥

Word-by-word meaning: भगवान् उवाच the Supreme Lord said, ज्ञातम् known, मम by me, पुरा previously/beforehand, एव indeed, एतत् this, ऋषिणा by the sage, करुण-आत्मना by the compassionate one, यत् that, श्री-मद-अन्धयोः of the two blinded by pride of wealth, वाग्भिः by words, विभ्रंशः the fall/degradation, अनुग्रहः grace/blessing, कृतः was done/performed

Anvaya: भगवान् उवाच — एतत् मम पुरा एव ज्ञातम् (आसीत्)। करुणात्मना ऋषिणा यत् श्रीमदान्धयोः वाग्भिः विभ्रंशः (कृतः) — (सः) अनुग्रहः कृतः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: भगवान् उवाच the Supreme Lord said — एतत् This, मम पुरा एव ज्ञातम् (आसीत्) was already known to Me beforehand. करुणात्मना ऋषिणा By the compassionate sage Narada, यत् श्रीमदान्धयोः  of you two who were blinded by pride of wealth, वाग्भिः by his words, विभ्रंशः (कृतः) the fall/degradation was brought about — (सः) अनुग्रहः कृतः that was indeed an act of grace.

Simple meaning: The Supreme Lord said — This was already known to Me beforehand. What the compassionate sage Narada did by bringing about your fall through his words — that was indeed an act of pure grace towards you two who were blinded by pride of wealth.

साधूनां समचित्तानां सुतरां मत्कृतात्मनाम् ।

दर्शनान्नो भवेद् बन्धः पुंसोऽक्ष्णोः सवितुर्यथा ॥10.10.41॥

Word-by-word meaning: साधूनाम् of the saintly persons, सम-चित्तानाम् of those with equanimous minds, सुतराम् especially/most certainly, मत्-कृत-आत्मनाम् of those who have dedicated their very self to Me, दर्शनात् from the vision/sight/association, न no/not, भवेत् would be, बन्धः bondage, पुंसः for a person, अक्ष्णोः for the eyes, सवितुः of the sun, यथा just as

Anvaya: यथा पुंसः अक्ष्णोः सवितुः — समचित्तानां मत्कृतात्मनां साधूनां सुतरां दर्शनात् (जनस्य) बन्धः न भवेत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यथा पुंसः अक्ष्णोः सवितुः Just as of a man’s eyes, by the sight of the Sun, — समचित्तानां मत्कृतात्मनां साधूनां सुतरां दर्शनात् from the sight and association of those with equanimous minds, who have dedicated their very selves to Me, of such saintly persons, (जनस्य) बन्धः न भवेत् for a person there shall be no bondage.

Simple meaning: From the vision and association of saintly persons — those with equanimous minds who have especially dedicated their very selves to Me — a person shall never remain in bondage, just as when one's eyes behold the sun there can be no bondage of darkness. Thus the Lord confirms that the darshan of true devotees is itself liberating.

तद् गच्छतं मत्परमौ नलकूबर सादनम् ।

सञ्जातो मयि भावो वामीप्सितः परमोऽभवः ॥10.10.42॥

Word-by-word meaning: तत् therefore, गच्छतम् go/depart, मत्-परमौ having Me as the supreme/highest, नलकूबर Nalakubara, सादनम् to your abode/home, सञ्जातः has arisen/been born, मयि towards Me, भावः devotion/feeling, वाम् your, ईप्सितः desired, परमः supreme, अ-भवः absolution/final beatitude/supreme blessedness

Anvaya: तत् नलकूबर! मत्परमौ (सन्तौ) सादनं गच्छतम्। वाम् ईप्सितः अभवः, मयि परमः भावः सञ्जातः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तत् therefore, नलकूबर O Nalakubara (and Manigriva), मत्परमौ (सन्तौ) having Me as the supreme goal, सादनं गच्छतम् go to your abode. वाम् Your two, ईप्सितः अभवः desired (goal of) cessation of birth, मयि towards Me, परमः भावः the supreme devotion, सञ्जातः has now been achieved.

Simple meaning: Therefore O Nalakubara and Manigriva! Go now to your abode with Me as your supreme goal. The liberation from the cycle of birth and death that has been desired by you both, and supreme devotion towards Me has now been fully achieved by you — the very purpose of Narada's curse has been fulfilled.

श्री शुक उवाच

इत्युक्तौ तौ परिक्रम्य प्रणम्य च पुनः पुनः ।

बद्धोलूखलमामन्त्र्य जग्मतुर्दिशमुत्तराम् ॥10.10.43॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुक उवाच Shri Shuka said, इति thus, उक्तौ having been spoken to, तौ those two, परिक्रम्य having circumambulated, प्रणम्य having bowed/offered obeisance, च and, पुनः पुनः again and again, बद्ध-उलूखलम् the one bound to the mortar, आमन्त्र्य having taken leave, जग्मतुः went/departed, दिशम् direction, उत्तराम् northern

Anvaya: श्री शुक उवाच — इति उक्तौ तौ, बद्धोलूखलं (कृष्णं) पुनः पुनः परिक्रम्य प्रणम्य च, आमन्त्र्य उत्तरां दिशं जग्मतुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री शुक उवाच Shri Shuka said — इति उक्तौ तौ those two having been thus spoken to, बद्धोलूखलं (कृष्णं) Krishna who was still bound to the mortar, पुनः पुनः again and again, परिक्रम्य having circumambulated, प्रणम्य च and having offered obeisance, आमन्त्र्य having taken leave, उत्तरां दिशं जग्मतुः departed towards the northern direction.

Simple meaning: Shri Shuka said — Having been thus spoken to by the Lord, those two circumambulated and offered their obeisance again and again to Krishna who was still bound to the mortar, and taking leave of Him, departed towards the northern direction.

श्रीमद्भागवते महापुराणे पारमहंस्यां संहितायां दशमस्कन्धे पूर्वार्धे

नारदशापो नाम दशमः अध्यायः ॥10॥

Thus ends the tenth discourse, 'Imprecation pronounced (on Nalakubara and Manigriva) by Narada', in the first half of Book Ten of the great and glorious Bhagavata-Purana.
