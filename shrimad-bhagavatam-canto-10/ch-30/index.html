Chapter 30

The Gopis' Quest for Shri Krishna during the Rasa-Play

श्रीमद्भागवतमहापुराणम्

दशमः स्कन्धः

रासक्रीडायां कृष्णान्वेषणं नाम त्रिंशः अध्यायः

(45 Shlokas)

श्री शुक उवाच

अन्तर्हिते भगवति सहसैव व्रजाङ्गनाः ।

अतप्यंस्तमचक्षाणाः करिण्य इव यूथपम् ॥10.30.1॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, अन्तर्हिते having disappeared, भगवति the Supreme Lord, सहसा एव suddenly, व्रज-अङ्गनाः the women of Vraja (the gopis), अतप्यन् suffered/lamented, तम् Him, अचक्षाणाः not seeing, करिण्यः female elephants, इव like, यूथ-पम् the leader of the herd

Anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच - भगवति सहसा एव अन्तर्हिते (सति), तम् अचक्षाणाः व्रजाङ्गनाः यूथपं (विना) करिण्यः इव अतप्यन्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, भगवति सहसा एव अन्तर्हिते (सति) when the Supreme Lord had suddenly disappeared, तम् अचक्षाणाः no longer seeing Him, व्रजाङ्गनाः the ladies of Vraja, यूथपं (विना) करिण्यः इव like female elephants who have lost the leader of their herd, अतप्यन् began to suffer in great distress.

Simple Meaning: Shri Shuka said: When Lord Krishna suddenly vanished, the gopis were overcome with grief. No longer able to see Him, they felt just like a herd of female elephants who have been separated from their leader, wandering in deep distress.

गत्यानुरागस्मितविभ्रमेक्षितैर्मनोरमालापविहारविभ्रमैः ।

आक्षिप्तचित्ताः प्रमदा रमापतेस्तास्ता विचेष्टा जगृहुस्तदात्मिकाः ॥10.30.2॥

Word-by-word meaning: गति-अनुराग-स्मित-विभ्रम-ईक्षितैः with His gait and His loving smiles and playful glances, मनोरम-आलाप-विहार-विभ्रमैः by His enchanting conversation and playful movements, आक्षिप्त-चित्ताः whose minds were stolen/captivated, प्रमदाः the young women, रमा-पतेः of the Lord of Rama (Krishna), ताः ताः those various, विचेष्टाः activities, जगृहुः they imitated, तत्-आत्मिकाः becoming absorbed in Him

Anvaya: रमापतेः गत्यानुरागस्मितविभ्रमेक्षितैः मनोरमालापविहारविभ्रमैः आक्षिप्तचित्ताः प्रमदाः तदात्मिकाः (सत्यः) ताः ताः विचेष्टाः जगृहुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: रमापतेः Of the Lord of Lakshmi (Krishna), गत्यानुरागस्मितविभ्रमेक्षितैः by His graceful walk, His loving smiles, His playful glances, मनोरमालापविहारविभ्रमैः with His enchanting talk, (and) through His playful activities, आक्षिप्तचित्ताः whose minds were completely stolen, प्रमदाः the young women (gopis), तदात्मिकाः (सत्यः) having become fully absorbed in Him, ताः ताः विचेष्टाः जगृहुः began to imitate His various actions and gestures.

Simple Meaning: The gopis’ minds were so completely stolen by Krishna’s graceful walk, His loving smiles, His playful glances, and His charming way of talking that they became entirely absorbed in Him. In their state of intense separation, they began to act just like Him—imitating His movements, His laughter, and His pastimes as if they themselves were Krishna.

गतिस्मितप्रेक्षणभाषणादिषु प्रियाः प्रियस्य प्रतिरूढमूर्तयः ।

असावहं त्वित्यबलास्तदात्मिका न्यवेदिषुः कृष्णविहारविभ्रमाः ॥10.30.3॥

Word-by-word meaning: गति-स्मित-प्रेक्षण-भाषण-आदिषु in His gait, smiles, glances, speech, and so on, प्रियाः the beloved gopis, प्रियस्य of their Beloved (Krishna), प्रतिरूढ-मूर्तयः having the forms of Krishna imprinted within them, असौ अहम् "I am He", तु indeed, इति thus, अबलाः the women, तत्-आत्मिकाः absorbed in Him, न्यवेदिषुः they announced, कृष्ण-विहार-विभ्रमाः acting out the playful pastimes of Krishna

Anvaya: गतिस्मितप्रेक्षणभाषणादिषु प्रियस्य प्रतिरूढमूर्तयः तदात्मिकाः अबलाः कृष्णविहारविभ्रमाः (सत्यः) "असौ अहम्" इति न्यवेदिषुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: गतिस्मितप्रेक्षणभाषणादिषु In His way of walking, smiling, glancing, and speaking, प्रियस्य प्रतिरूढमूर्तयः having the image of their Beloved firmly imprinted (within their hearts and bodies), तदात्मिकाः being fully absorbed in Him, अबलाः the women, कृष्णविहारविभ्रमाः (सत्यः) while imitating the playful movements of Krishna, "असौ अहम्" "I am He (Krishna)," इति न्यवेदिषुः thus declared.

Simple Meaning: Because the gopis were so deeply absorbed in Krishna, His very image—His walk, His smiles, and His voice—became firmly imprinted in their hearts. Lost in this state of divine union through separation, they began to mimic His playful pastimes and, in their ecstasy, actually declared, "I am Krishna Himself!"

Insight: This verse describes a profound psychological and spiritual state called Sayujya-bhava within the context of Preman (divine love). Their identification with Him was so intense that they forgot their own identities as gopis and began to perceive themselves as the very object of their love.

गायन्त्य उच्चैरमुमेव संहता विचिक्युरुन्मत्तकवद् वनाद् वनम् ।

पप्रच्छुराकाशवदन्तरं बहिर्भूतेषु सन्तं पुरुषं वनस्पतीन् ॥10.30.4॥

Word-by-word meaning: गायन्त्यः singing, उच्चैः loudly, अमुम् Him (Krishna), एव only, संहताः together/in a group, विचिक्युः they searched, उन्मत्तक-वत् like madwomen, वनात् वनम् from forest to forest, पप्रच्छुः they asked, आकाश-वत् like the sky, अन्तरम् inside, बहिः outside, भूतेषु in all living beings, सन्तम् existing, पुरुषम् the Supreme Person, वनस्पतीन् the trees/lords of the forest

Anvaya: (ताः) संहताः उच्चैः अमुम् एव गायन्त्यः उन्मत्तकवत् वनात् वनं विचिक्युः। आकाशवत् भूतेषु अन्तः बहिः च सन्तं पुरुषं (कृष्णं) वनस्पतीन् पप्रच्छुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (ताः) संहताः They, joining together, उच्चैः अमुम् एव गायन्त्यः singing loudly about Him alone, उन्मत्तकवत् वनात् वनं विचिक्युः searched from one forest to another like madwomen. आकाशवत् भूतेषु अन्तः बहिः च सन्तं पुरुषं (कृष्मं) (Searching for) that Supreme Person who, like the sky, exists both inside and outside of all living beings, वनस्पतीन् पप्रच्छुः they questioned the trees.

Simple Meaning: Singing loudly of Krishna in unison, the gopis wandered from one forest to another, searching for Him like women who had lost their minds. Even though Krishna is the Supreme Soul who pervades everything inside and out—just like the sky—the gopis, in their intense longing, began to ask the trees of the forest if they had seen Him.

Key Takeaway: This verse beautifully highlights the paradox of Bhakti:

दृष्टो वः कच्चिदश्वत्थ प्लक्ष न्यग्रोध नः मनः ।

नन्दसूनुर्गतः हृत्वा प्रेमहासावलोकनैः ॥10.30.5॥

Word-by-word meaning: दृष्टः seen, वः by you, कच्चित् whether, अश्वत्थ O holy fig tree, प्लक्ष O wave-leafed fig tree, न्यग्रोध O banyan tree, नः our, मनः mind/heart, नन्द-सूनुः the son of Nanda (Krishna), गतः has gone, हृत्वा having stolen, प्रेम-हास-अवलोकनैः by His loving smiles and glances

Anvaya: अश्वत्थ! प्लक्ष! न्यग्रोध! कच्चित् वः नन्दसूनुः दृष्टः? (यः) नः मनः प्रेमहासावलोकनैः हृत्वा गतः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अश्वत्थ! प्लक्ष! न्यग्रोध! O Ashvattha! O Plaksha! O Nyagrodha! कच्चित् वः नन्दसूनुः दृष्टः? Has the son of Nanda been seen by you? (यः) नः मनः प्रेमहासावलोकनैः हृत्वा गतः He who has gone away, having stolen our hearts with His loving smiles and glances.

Simple Meaning: The gopis address the great trees of the forest: "O Ashvattha, O Plaksha, O Nyagrodha, have you seen the son of Nanda? He has stolen our hearts with His enchanting smiles and loving glances and then disappeared. Did He pass by this way?"

Emotional Nuance: The gopis begin their inquiry with the greatest trees of the forest. In their state of prema-unmada (madness of love), they assume the trees are conscious witnesses to Krishna's movements. They aren't just looking for a person, they are looking for the "thief" who has physically carried away their very capacity to think and feel (मनः).

कच्चित् कुरबकाशोकनागपुन्नागचम्पकाः ।

रामानुजो मानिनीनामितो दर्पहरस्मितः ॥10.30.6॥

Word-by-word meaning: कच्चित् whether, कुरबक-अशोक-नाग-पुन्नाग-चम्पकाः O Kurabaka, Ashoka, Naga, Punnaga, and Champaka trees, राम-अनुजः the younger brother of Balarama (Krishna), मानिनीनाम् of those who are proud, इतः this way, दर्प-हर-स्मितः whose smile removes the pride

Anvaya: कुरबकाशोकनागपुन्नागचम्पकाः! कच्चित् मानिनीनां दर्पहरस्मितः रामानुजः इतः (गतः)?

Literal meaning of the anvaya: कुरबकाशोकनागपुन्नागचम्पकाः! O Kurabaka, Ashoka, Naga, Punnaga, and Champaka trees! कच्चित् मानिनीनां दर्पहरस्मितः रामानुजः इतः (गतः)? Has the younger brother of Balarama—whose smile shatters the pride of those who feel conceited—passed by this way?

Simple Meaning: The gopis now turn to the flowering trees: "O Kurabaka, Ashoka, Naga, Punnaga, and Champaka! Have you seen Krishna pass by? He is the one whose enchanting smile has the power to steal away the pride of women like us. Did He go this way?"

Insight: In the previous verse (10.30.5), the gopis addressed the "masculine" giants of the forest (the Banyan and Fig trees). Here, they address the flowering trees, which are often associated with beauty and romance.

Notice how they refer to Him as "the younger brother of Rama" (Ramanuja) and mention His "pride-removing smile" (darpa-hara-smitah). They are acknowledging that Krishna saw their pride (mana) in the previous chapter and used His disappearance as a "smile" to humble them and intensify their love.

कच्चित्तुलसि कल्याणि गोविन्दचरणप्रिये ।

सह त्वालिकुलैर्बिभ्रद् दृष्टस्तेऽतिप्रियोऽच्युतः ॥10.30.7॥

Word-by-word meaning: कच्चित् whether, तुलसि O Tulasi, कल्याणि O auspicious one, गोविन्द-चरण-प्रिये who are very dear to the feet of Govinda, सह with, त्वा you, अलि-कुलैः with swarms of bees, बिभ्रत् carrying/wearing, दृष्टः seen, ते your, अति-प्रियः most beloved, अच्युतः the Infallible Lord

Anvaya: कल्याणि गोविन्दचरणप्रिये तुलसि! कच्चित् अलिकुलैः सह त्वाम् (माला-रूपेण) बिभ्रत् ते अतिप्रियः अच्युतः दृष्टः?

Literal meaning of the anvaya: कल्याणि गोविन्दचरणप्रिये तुलसि! O auspicious Tulasi, you who are so dear to the feet of Govinda! कच्चित् अलिकुलैः सह त्वाम् (माला-रूपेण) बिभ्रत् has He, who wears you (in the form of a garland) along with swarms of bees, ते अतिप्रियः अच्युतः दृष्टः? your most beloved Infallible Lord, been seen by you?

Simple Meaning: "O most auspicious Tulasi, you who always find shelter at Govinda's lotus feet! Have you seen your most beloved Achyuta? We know He is carrying you in His garland, surrounded by humming bees. Has He passed by this way?"

Emotional Nuance: After asking the majestic trees and the flowering plants, the gopis now turn to Tulasi. Their tone changes slightly here:

मालत्यदर्शि वः कच्चिन्मल्लिके जाति यूथिके ।

प्रीतिं वो जनयन् यातः करस्पर्शेन माधवः ॥10.30.8॥

Word-by-word meaning: मालति O Malati, अदर्शि seen, वः by you, कच्चित् whether, मल्लिके O Mallika, जाति O Jati, यूथिके O Yuthika, प्रीतिम् pleasure, वः to you, जनयन् giving/generating, यातः has gone, कर-स्पर्शेन by the touch of His hand, माधवः Krishna (the husband of the Goddess of Fortune)

Anvaya: मालति! मल्लिके! जाति! यूथिके! वः (किं) अदर्शि? कच्चित् वः करस्पर्शेन प्रीतिं जनयन् माधवः इतः यातः?

Literal meaning of the anvaya: मालति! मल्लिके! जाति! यूथिके! O Malati, Mallika, Jati, and Yuthika jasmine flowers! वः (किं) अदर्शि? Has anything been seen by you? कच्चित् वः करस्पर्शेन प्रीतिं जनयन् माधवः इतः यातः? Has Madhava, who gives you such pleasure by the touch of His hand, passed by this way?

Simple Meaning: The gopis now speak to the various species of jasmine: "O Malati, Mallika, Jati, and Yuthika! Have you seen Madhava? We know He is very dear to you and gives you joy by tenderly touching you with His hand. Did He happen to go this way?"

Deepening the Search: The gopis move from the tall trees to the smaller, fragrant flowering vines.

चूतप्रियालपनसासनकोविदारजम्ब्वर्कबिल्वबकुलाम्रकदम्बनीपाः ।

येऽन्ये परार्थभवका यमुनोपकूलाः शंसन्तु कृष्णपदवीं रहितात्मनां नः ॥10.30.9॥

Word-by-word meaning: चूत-प्रियाल-पनस-आसन-कोविदार-जम्बू-अर्क-बिल्व-बकुल-आम्र-कदम्ब-नीपाः O Mango, Priyala, Jackfruit, Asana, Kovidara, Jambu, Arka, Bilva, Bakula, Amra, Kadamba, and Nipa trees, ये अन्ये and all others, पर-अर्थ-भवकाः whose existence is for the sake of others, यमुना-उपकूलाः growing on the banks of the Yamuna, शंसन्तु please tell, कृष्ण-पदवीम् the path taken by Krishna, रहितात्मनाम् for us whose souls have been taken away, नः our

Anvaya: चूतप्रियालपनसासनकोविदारजम्ब्वर्कबिल्वबकुलाम्रकदम्बनीपाः! ये अन्ये परार्थभवकाः यमुनोपकूलाः (सन्ति), (ते सर्वे) रहितात्मनां नः कृष्णपदवीं शंसन्तु।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: चूतप्रियालपनसासनकोविदारजम्ब्वर्कबिल्वबकुलाम्रकदम्बनीपाः! O Mango, Priyala, Jackfruit, Asana, Kovidara, Jambu, Arka, Bilva, Bakula, Amra, Kadamba, and Nipa trees! ये अन्ये परार्थभवकाः यमुनोपकूलाः (सन्ति) And all you other trees who exist for the benefit of others and live on the banks of the Yamuna, (ते सर्वे) (all of you), रहितात्मनां नः to us who have lost our very souls, कृष्णपदवीं the path of Krishna, शंसन्तु please tell.

Simple Meaning: The gopis address all the trees standing along the banks of the Yamuna—the Mango, Jackfruit, Bakula, Kadamba, and many others: "O noble trees, your entire lives are dedicated to the service of others. We have lost our very souls because Krishna has left us. Since you are so selfless, please show us the path He has taken."

Key Highlights:

किं ते कृतं क्षिति तपो बत केशवाङ्घ्रिस्पर्शोत्सवोत्पुलकिताङ्गरुहैर्विभासि ।

अप्यङ्घ्रिसम्भव उरुक्रमविक्रमाद् वा आहो वराहवपुषः परिरम्भणेन ॥10.30.10॥

Word-by-word meaning: किम् what, ते by you, कृतम् performed, क्षिति O Earth, तपः austerity, बत oh!, केशव-अङ्घ्रि-स्पर्श-उत्सव-उत्पुलकित-अङ्गरुहैः with your body hairs (the grass and plants) standing on end due to the festival of touching Kesava’s feet, विभासि you look so beautiful/radiant, अपि whether, अङ्घ्रि-सम्भवः from the touch of His feet, उरुक्रम-विक्रमात् from the stride of the Great-Stepper (Vamana), वा or, आहो or else, वराह-वपुषः of the one with the form of a boar (Varaha), परिरम्भणेन by the embrace

Anvaya: क्षिति! बत ते किं तपः कृतम्? (यतः) केशवाङ्घ्रिस्पर्शोत्सवोत्पुलकिताङ्गरुहैः विभासि। (एषा विभूतिः) उरुक्रमविक्रमात् अङ्घ्रिसम्भवः अपि वा आहो वराहवपुषः परिरम्भणेन (अस्ति)?

Literal meaning of the anvaya: क्षिति! O Earth! बत ते किं तपः कृतम्? Oh, what great penance have you performed? केशवाङ्घ्रिस्पर्शोत्सवोत्पुलकिताङ्गरुहैः With your hair standing on end in ecstasy from the festival of touching Kesava’s feet, विभासि you look so radiant. (एषा विभूतिः) Is this glory of yours उरुक्रमविक्रमात् from the strides of Vamana, अङ्घ्रिसम्भवः अपि derived from the touch of His feet, वा or आहो वराहवपुषः परिरम्भणेन by the embrace of the Lord in His Boar incarnation?

Simple Meaning: "O Earth! What incredible austerities have you performed? You look so beautiful right now, with your grass and plants standing on end like hair on a body experiencing a thrill of ecstasy. Is this joy because of Krishna's recent footsteps? Word-by-word meaning: रेमे He enjoyed, तया with her, च and, आत्म-रतः delighted in Himself, आत्मा-रामः satisfied within Himself, अपि although, अखण्डितः perfectly complete/unbroken, कामिनाम् of lusty persons, दर्शयन् showing, दैन्यम् the wretchedness/weakness, स्त्रीणाम् of women, च and, एव indeed, दुरात्मताम् the self-centeredness/difficulty of nature

Anvaya: (सः) आत्मरतः आत्मारामः अखण्डितः अपि तया (सह) रेमे, कामिनां दैन्यं स्त्रीणां च एव दुरात्मतां दर्शयन्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (सः) He, आत्मरतः who delights in His own Self, आत्मारामः satisfied within, अखण्डितः and perfectly complete, अपि although, तया (सह) रेमे enjoyed with her, कामिनां दैन्यं the wretched state of lusty lovers, स्त्रीणां च एव दुरात्मतां and the wayward or controlling nature of women, दर्शयन् (thereby) showing.

Simple Meaning: Lord Krishna is perfectly complete in Himself, He is Atmarama, finding all His pleasure within His own being. Yet, He played the role of an ordinary lover and enjoyed with that gopi to demonstrate two things: how weak and helpless men become when captivated by lust, and how demanding and self-centered women can be in their dealings with their lovers.

इत्येवं दर्शयन्त्यस्ताश्चेरुर्गोप्यो विचेतसः ।

यां गोपीमनयत् कृष्णो विहायान्याः स्त्रियो वने ॥10.30.36॥

Word-by-word meaning: इति एवम् in this manner, दर्शयन्त्यः pointing out, ताः those, चेरुः wandered, गोप्यः the gopis, विचेतसः bewildered/lost in thought, याम् whom, गोपीम् the gopi, अनयत् led away, कृष्णः Krishna, विहाय leaving behind, अन्याः the other, स्त्रियः women, वने in the forest

Anvaya: इति एवं (पदानि) दर्शयन्त्यः ताः विचेतसः गोप्यः वने चेरुः, यां गोपीं कृष्णः अन्याः स्त्रियः विहाय अनयत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: इति एवं In this manner, (पदानि) दर्शयन्त्यः pointing out the footprints, ताः विचेतसः गोप्यः those bewildered gopis, वने चेरुः wandered in the forest, यां गोपीं (looking for) that gopi whom, कृष्णः Krishna, अन्याः स्त्रियः विहाय अनयत् led away after leaving the other women behind.

Simple Meaning: In this state of total bewilderment, the gopis wandered through the forest, pointing out different sets of footprints and searching for that specific gopi whom Krishna had taken away alone, abandoning all the others.

सा च मेने तदाऽऽत्मानं वरिष्ठं सर्वयोषिताम् ।

हित्वा गोपीः कामयाना मामसौ भजते प्रियः ॥10.30.37॥

Word-by-word meaning: सा she (the specific gopi), च and, मेने thought, तदा then, आत्मानम् herself, वरिष्ठम् the best/greatest, सर्व-योषिताम् among all women, हित्वा leaving aside, गोपीः the (other) gopis, कामयानाः who desire Him, माम् me, असौ that, भजते honors/is devoted to, प्रियः the lover

Anvaya: तदा सा च आत्मानं सर्वयोषितां वरिष्ठं मेने—"असौ प्रियः कामयानाः गोपीः हित्वा माम् (एव) भजते।"

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तदा Then, सा च and she, आत्मानं herself, सर्वयोषितां वरिष्ठं मेने thought of as the best of all women (thinking)—"असौ प्रियः "This lover, कामयानाः गोपीः हित्वा leaving behind the gopis who desire Him, माम् (एव) भजते is devoted to me alone."

Simple Meaning: "At that moment, that gopi began to feel quite proud, considering herself the best of all women. She thought, 'My lover has abandoned all the other gopis, even though they desire Him, just to be with me and honor me alone.'"

Note: This verse marks a turning point in the Rasa Lila narrative. It highlights the onset of Saubhagya-mada (the pride of good fortune) in the gopi who was taken away, which leads to the events in the following verses.

ततोगत्वा वनोद्देशं दृप्ता केशवमब्रवीत् ।

न पारयेऽहं चलितुं नय मां यत्र ते मनः ॥10.30.38॥

Word-by-word meaning: ततः then, गत्वा having gone, वन-उद्देशम् to a spot in the forest, दृप्ता proud/haughty, केशवम् to Keshava, अब्रवीत् said, न not, पारये I am able, अहम् I, चलितुम् to walk, नय take/carry, माम् me, यत्र where, ते your, मनः mind (desires)

पारये पृ , णिजन्ते - कर्तरि लट्लकारः (आत्मनेपदम्) , उत्तमपुरुषः , एकवचनम् src. Dhatupatha, Ashtadhyayi

Anvaya: ततः वनोद्देशं गत्वा दृप्ता (सा) केशवम् अब्रवीत्— "अहं चलितुं न पारये, यत्र ते मनः (अस्ति) (तत्र) मां नय।"

Literal meaning of the anvaya: ततः Then, वनोद्देशं गत्वा having gone to a spot in the forest, दृप्ता (सा) that proud gopi, केशवम् अब्रवीत् said to Keshava— "अहं चलितुं न पारये 'I am not able to walk,' यत्र ते मनः (तत्र) मां नय 'take me wherever your heart desires.'"

Simple Meaning: "Then, after walking further into the forest, the gopi became intoxicated with pride. She told Lord Keshava, 'I am not able to walk another step. Please, just carry me wherever you want to go.'"

Insight: This verse beautifully illustrates the shift from pure devotion to a momentary sense of superiority. Krishna, sensing this pride, prepares to respond in the next verse.

एवमुक्तः प्रियामाह स्कन्ध आरुह्यतामिति ।

ततश्चान्तर्दधे कृष्णः सा वधूरन्वतप्यत ॥10.30.39॥

Word-by-word meaning: एवम् उक्तः thus addressed, प्रियाम् to His beloved, आह He said, स्कन्धः (My) shoulder, आरुह्यताम् please climb, इति so, ततः then, च and, अन्तर्दधे disappeared, कृष्णः Krishna, सा that, वधूः bride, अन्वतप्यत felt great remorse

Anvaya: एवम् उक्तः (कृष्णः) प्रियाम् आह— "स्कन्धः आरुह्यताम्" इति। ततः च कृष्णः अन्तर्दधे, सा वधूः अन्वतप्यत।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: एवम् उक्तः Thus addressed, (कृष्णः) Krishna, प्रियाम् आह said to His beloved— "स्कन्धः आरुह्यताम्" "Please climb upon My shoulder," इति so. ततः च And then, कृष्णः अन्तर्दधे Krishna disappeared, सा वधूः that bride, अन्वतप्यत felt deep regret/remorse.

Simple Meaning: When she spoke to Him in that way, Krishna replied, 'Very well, then—just climb onto My shoulder.' But as soon as she reached out to do so, Krishna vanished from her sight. Left all alone, the young bride was instantly filled with overwhelming regret and sorrow.

Insight: This is one of the most poignant moments in the Bhagavata Purana. Krishna, the "Great Soul" mentioned earlier, humors her request just long enough to show her how much she depends on Him. The moment her pride (mana) manifested as a demand, He disappeared to restore her humility and intensify her longing (viraha).

हा नाथ रमण प्रेष्ठ क्वासि क्वासि महाभुज ।

दास्यास्ते कृपणाया मे सखे दर्शय सन्निधिम् ॥10.30.40॥

Word-by-word meaning: हा Alas!, नाथ O Master, रमण O Lover, प्रेष्ठ O Dearest, क्व where, असि are You, क्व where, असि are You, महा-भुज O mighty-armed one, दास्याः of the maidservant, ते Your, कृपणायाः who is wretched/miserable, मे for me, सखे O Friend, दर्शय please show, सन्निधिम् Your presence

Anvaya: हा नाथ! रमण! प्रेष्ठ! महाभुज! क्व असि? क्व असि? सखे! ते कृपणायाः मे दास्याः सन्निधिं दर्शय।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: हा नाथ! Alas, O Master! रमण! O Lover! प्रेष्ठ! O Dearest! महाभुज! O mighty-armed one! क्व असि? क्व असि? Where are You? Where are You? सखे! O Friend! ते कृपणायाः मे दास्याः to me, Your wretched maidservant, सन्निधिं दर्शय please show Your presence.

Simple Meaning: "Alas, my Lord! My Lover, my Dearest! O mighty-armed one, where are You? Where are You? My Friend, please reveal Yourself to me, Your poor, miserable maidservant."

Key Highlights of this Verse:

अन्विच्छन्त्यो भगवतो मार्गं गोप्योऽविदूरतः ।

ददृशुः प्रियविश्लेषमोहितां दुःखितां सखीम् ॥10.30.41॥

Word-by-word meaning: अन्विच्छन्त्यः following/searching, भगवतः of the Lord, मार्गम् the path, गोप्यः the gopis, अविदूरतः not very far away, ददृशुः they saw, प्रिय-विश्लेष-मोहिताम् bewildered by separation from her lover, दुःखिताम् distressed, सखीम् their friend

Anvaya: भगवतः मार्गम् अन्विच्छन्त्यः ताः गोप्यः अविदूरतः प्रियविश्लेषमोहितां दुःखितां सखीं ददृशुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: भगवतः मार्गम् अन्विच्छन्त्यः While searching for the path of the Lord, ताः गोप्यः those gopis, अविदूरतः not far away, प्रियविश्लेषमोहितां (she who was) bewildered by the separation from her beloved, दुःखितां सखीं ददृशुः saw their distressed friend.

Simple Meaning: While following the Lord’s trail, the other gopis soon came upon their friend. She was standing not far off, overwhelmed by grief and completely bewildered by the pain of being separated from her beloved Krishna.

Contextual Note: The gopis who were left behind in the beginning have now caught up to the one gopi Krishna took with Him. They find her in the same state they are in—suffering from the pangs of separation (viraha). This brings the group back together in their shared devotion and longing.

तया कथितमाकर्ण्य मानप्राप्तिं च माधवात् ।

अवमानं च दौरात्म्याद् विस्मयं परमं ययुः॥10.30.42॥

Word-by-word meaning: तया by her, कथितम् what was told, आकर्ण्य hearing, मान-प्राप्तिम् the attainment of special honor, च and, माधवात् from Madhava, अवमानम् dishonor/disrespect, च and, दौरात्म्यात् due to her own wickedness/improper behavior, विस्मयम् astonishment, परमम् extreme, ययुः they reached (felt)

Anvaya: तया कथितं माधवात् मानप्राप्तिं च दौरात्म्यात् अवमानं च आकर्ण्य (ताः) परमं विस्मयं ययुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तया कथितं Hearing what was told by her, माधवात् मानप्राप्तिं च the special honor received from Madhava, दौरात्म्यात् अवमानं च and the subsequent dishonor due to her own improper conduct, (ताः) those gopis, परमं विस्मयं ययुः were struck with extreme amazement.

Simple Meaning: When the gopis heard her story—how Madhava had shown her such extraordinary favor and how she then suffered His disappearance due to her own prideful behavior—they were all filled with utter astonishment.

Key Takeaways:

ततोऽविशन् वनं चन्द्रज्योत्सना यावद् विभाव्यते ।

तमः प्रविष्टमालक्ष्य ततो निववृतुः स्त्रियः ॥10.30.43॥

Word-by-word meaning: ततः then, अविशन् they entered, वनम् the forest, चन्द्र-ज्योत्सना the moonlight, यावत् as long as, विभाव्यते could be perceived, तमः darkness, प्रविष्टम् entered (dense), आलक्ष्य noticing, ततः then, निववृतुः they turned back, स्त्रियः the women (gopis)

Anvaya: ततः यावत् चन्द्रज्योत्सना विभाव्यते (तावत् ताः) वनम् अविशन्। तमः प्रविष्टम् आलक्ष्य ततः स्त्रियः निववृतुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: ततः Then, यावत् चन्द्रज्योत्सना विभाव्यते as long as the moonlight was visible, (तावत् ताः) वनम् अविशन् they entered deeper into the forest. तमः प्रविष्टम् आलक्ष्य Noticing that they had entered into (thick) darkness, ततः स्त्रियः निववृतुः then those women turned back.

Simple Meaning: The gopis continued to push into the forest as long as there was enough moonlight to see the way. But when they reached the dense thickets where the moonlight could not penetrate and only darkness remained, they were forced to stop and turn back.

Reflection on the Verse: This verse marks the physical limit of their search. The "darkness" (tamah) symbolizes the boundary where human effort alone—even effort driven by intense love—reaches its end. Having lost both Krishna's footprints and the light of the moon, the gopis are now led toward the only path remaining: internalizing their search through meditation and singing, which leads into the famous Gopi Gita.

तन्मनस्कास्तदालापास्तद्विचेष्टास्तदात्मिकाः ।

तद्गुणानेव गायन्त्यो नात्मागाराणि सस्मरुः ॥10.30.44॥

Word-by-word meaning: तत्-मनस्काः whose minds were fixed on Him, तत्-आलापाः whose conversations were about Him, तत्-विचेष्टाः whose movements/gestures imitated His, तत्-आत्मिकाः whose very souls were absorbed in Him, तत्-गुणान् His qualities, एव only, गायन्त्यः singing, न not, आत्म-अगाराणि their own homes, सस्मरुः remembered

Anvaya: तन्मनस्काः तदालापाः तद्विचेष्टाः तदात्मिकाः (ताः) तद्गुणान् एव गायन्त्यः आत्मागाराणि न सस्मरुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तन्मनस्काः With their minds fixed on Him, तदालापाः with their talk centered on Him, तद्विचेष्टाः imitating His pastimes, तदात्मिकाः and feeling themselves to be one with Him, (ताः) those gopis, तद्गुणान् एव गायन्त्यः singing only of His transcendental qualities, आत्मागाराणि न सस्मरुः did not remember their own homes (or even their own bodies/selves).

Simple Meaning: Their minds were absorbed in Him, their talk was only of Him, they mimicked His playful actions, and they felt entirely one with Him. Lost in the singing of His glorious qualities, they completely forgot their homes, their families, and even their own physical existence.

Significance of this Verse: This verse describes the state of Samadhi or Parama-bhakti (supreme devotion).

This leads directly into the next phase of the Rasa Lila, where the gopis return to the banks of the Yamuna to sing the Gopi Gita.

पुनः पुलिनमागत्य कालिन्द्याः कृष्णभावनाः ।

समवेता जगुः कृष्णं तदागमनकाङ्क्षिताः ॥10.30.45॥

Word-by-word meaning: पुनः again, पुलिनम् to the sandy bank, आगत्य having returned, कालिन्द्याः of the river Yamuna (Kalindi), कृष्ण-भावनाः whose consciousness was fully absorbed in Krishna, समवेताः gathering together, जगुः they sang, कृष्णम् about Krishna, तत्-आगमन-काङ्क्षिताः desiring/longing for His arrival

Anvaya: कृष्णभावनाः (ताः) पुनः कालिन्द्याः पुलिनम् आगत्य समवेताः (सत्यः) तदागमनकाङ्क्षिताः कृष्णं जगुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: कृष्णभावनाः With their consciousness absorbed in Krishna, (ताः) those gopis, पुनः कालिन्द्याः पुलिनम् आगत्य returning again to the sandy bank of the Yamuna, समवेताः gathering together, तदागमनकाङ्क्षिताः and intensely longing for His arrival, कृष्णं जगुः sang about Krishna.

Simple Meaning: Returning to the sandy banks of the Yamuna, the gopis—their hearts and minds completely absorbed in Krishna—gathered together as one. Filled with a desperate longing for Him to return, they began to sing of Him and His glories.

Significance of this Verse:

इति श्रीमद्भागवते महापुराणे पारमहंस्यां संहितायां दशमस्कन्धे पूर्वार्धे “रासक्रीडायां कृष्णान्वेषणं नाम” त्रिंशः अध्यायः ॥30॥

Thus ends the thirtieth discourse named, 'The Gopis' Quest for Shri Krishna during the Rasa Play,' in the first half of Book Ten of the great and glorious Bhagavata-Purana.

*Verse 25 talks about the marks on the feet of Krishna. It is explained as follows:

“Thus the Skanda Purāṇa describes six marks on Krishna's right foot — the disc, flag, lotus, thunderbolt, elephant goad and barleycorn. And the Vaiṣṇava-toṣaṇī mentions even more marks — a vertical line beginning at the middle of His foot and continuing as far as the juncture between His big toe and second toe, an umbrella below the disc, at the base of the middle of His foot, a group of four svastikas in the four cardinal directions, at the four points where each svastika meets the next, four rose apples, and in the middle of the svastikas, an octagon. This makes eleven marks on Krishna's right foot.”

Śrīla Viśvanātha Cakravartī describes the marks on Krishna's left foot as follows: “At the base of the large toe is a conchshell with its mouth facing the toe. At the base of the middle toe are two concentric circles, representing the inner and outer sky. Below this mark is Cupid’s unstrung bow, at the base of the bow is a triangle, and surrounding the triangle is a group of four waterpots. At the base of the triangle is a half-moon with two more triangles touching its points, and below the half-moon is a fish.

“All together, then, there are nineteen distinguishing marks on the soles of Lord Krishna's lotus feet.”
