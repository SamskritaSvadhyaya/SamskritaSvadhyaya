Shrimad Bhagwatam - Book 1

Canto 10

Chapter 37

Krishna slays Keshi and Vyomasura

श्रीमद्भागवतमहापुराणम्

दशमः स्कन्धः

केशीव्योमासुरवधो नाम सप्तत्रिंशः अध्यायः

(34 Shlokas)

श्री शुक उवाच

केशी तु कंसप्रहितः खुरैर्महीं महाहयो निर्जरयन् मनोजवः ।

सटावधूताभ्रविमानसङ्कुलं कुर्वन् नभो हेषितभीषिताखिलः ॥10.37.1॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, केशी Keshi, तु indeed, कंस-प्रहितः sent by Kamsa, खुरैः with his hooves, महीम् the earth, महा-हयः the great horse, निर्जरयन् tearing apart, मनोजवः swift as the mind, सटा-अवधूत-अभ्र-विमान-सङ्कुलम् making the sky crowded with clouds and airplanes scattered by his mane, कुर्वन् making, नभः the sky, हेषित-भीषित-अखिलः frightening everyone with his neighing

Anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच — कंसप्रहितः मनोजवः महाहयः केशी तु खुरैः महीं निर्जरयन्, सटावधूताभ्रविमानसङ्कुलं नभः कुर्वन्, हेषितभीषिताखिलः (सन् आगतः)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, कंसप्रहितः sent by Kamsa, मनोजवः swift as the mind, महाहयः the great horse, केशी तु indeed the asura Keshi, खुरैः महीं निर्जरयन् tearing the earth with his hooves, सटावधूताभ्रविमानसङ्कुलं नभः कुर्वन् making the sky crowded with clouds and celestial vehicles tossed about by his mane, हेषितभीषिताखिलः frightening all living beings with his neighing (arrived).

Simple Meaning: Shri Shuka said: Sent by Kamsa, the asura Keshi, appearing as a massive horse as swift as thought, arrived. He tore up the earth with his hooves and tossed the clouds and celestial airplanes about with his mane. With his terrifying neighing, he struck fear into the hearts of everyone.

विशालनेत्रो विकटास्यकोटरो बृहद्गलो नीलमहाम्बुदोपमः ।

दुराशयः कंसहितं चिकीर्षुर्व्रजं स नन्दस्य जगाम कम्पयन् ॥10.37.2॥

Word-by-word meaning: विशाल-नेत्रः having giant eyes, विकट-आस्य-कोटरः with a mouth as deep and fearsome as a mountain cave, बृहत्-गलः having a massive neck, नील-महा-अम्बुद-उपमः resembling a huge dark-blue cloud, दुराशयः evil-minded, कंस-हितम् the benefit of Kamsa, चिकीर्षुः desiring to do, व्रजम् the cowherd settlement, सः he (Keshi), नन्दस्य of Nanda, जगाम went, कम्पयन् making tremble

Anvaya: विशालनेत्रः विकटास्यकोटरः बृहद्गलः नीलमहाम्बुदोपमः दुराशयः सः कंसहितं चिकीर्षुः नन्दस्य व्रजं कम्पयन् जगाम।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: विशालनेत्रः Possessing huge eyes, विकटास्यकोटरः with a mouth like a frightening cave, बृहद्गलः with a large neck, नीलमहाम्बुदोपमः resembling a great dark cloud, दुराशयः the evil-minded one, सः he (Keshi), कंसहितं चिकीर्षुः desiring to perform what is beneficial for Kamsa, नन्दस्य व्रजं कम्पयन् making Nanda's village tremble, जगाम went.

Simple Meaning: With massive eyes, a mouth as gaping and fearsome as a cave, and a thick neck, that evil-minded asura resembled a giant dark cloud. Desiring to please Kamsa, he charged into Nanda's village, making the entire place tremble as he moved.

तं त्रासयन्तं भगवान् स्वगोकुलं तद्धेषितैर्वालविघूर्णिताम्बुदम् ।

आत्मानमाजौ मृगयन्तमग्रणीरुपाह्वयत् स व्यनदन्मृगेन्द्रवत् ॥10.37.3॥

Word-by-word meaning: तम् him (Keshi), त्रासयन्तम् frightening, भगवान् the Supreme Lord, स्व-गोकुलम् His own cowherd village, तत्-हेषितैः by his neighing, वाल-विघूर्णित-अम्बुदम् whose tail tossed the clouds, आत्मानम् Himself, आजौ in battle, मृगयन्तम् searching for, अग्रणीः the foremost leader, उपाह्वयत् challenged, सः He (Krishna), व्यनदन् roared, मृगेन्द्र-वत् like a lion

Anvaya: तद्धेषितैः वालविघूर्णिताम्बुदं स्वगोकुलं त्रासयन्तं आजौ आत्मानं मृगयन्तं तम् उपाह्वयत् भगवान् अग्रणीः सः मृगेन्द्रवत् व्यनदन्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तद्धेषितैः By that (horse's) neighing, वालविघूर्णिताम्बुदं whose tail scattered the clouds, स्वगोकुलं त्रासयन्तं who was frightening His own village, आजौ आत्मानं मृगयन्तं searching for Him in battle, तम् उपाह्वयत् challenged him, भगवान् अग्रणीः The Supreme Lord, the leader of all, सः He (Krishna), मृगेन्द्रवत् व्यनदन् roared like the king of beasts (a lion).

Simple Meaning: The Supreme Lord, the leader of all souls, saw how Keshi was terrifying the village of Gokula with his neighing and tossing the clouds with his tail. Seeing that the asura was searching for Him to fight, Lord Krishna challenged him and let out a roar as powerful as a lion's.

स तं निशाम्याभिमुखो मुखेन खं पिबन्निवाभ्यद्रवदत्यमर्षणः।

जघान पद्भ्यामरविन्दलोचनं दुरासदश्चण्डजवो दुरत्ययः ॥10.37.4॥

Word-by-word meaning: सः he (Keshi), तम् Him (Krishna), निशाम्य seeing, अभिमुखः facing Him, मुखेन with his mouth, खम् the sky, पिबन् इव as if drinking, अभ्यद्रवत् charged, अति-अमर्षणः extremely intolerant, जघान struck, पद्भ्याम् with his legs, अरविन्द-लोचनम् the lotus-eyed Lord, दुरासदः difficult to approach, चण्ड-जवः possessing fierce speed, दुरत्ययः invincible

Anvaya: अत्यमर्षणः दुरासदः चण्डजवः दुरत्ययः सः तम् अभिमुखः निशाम्य मुखेन खं पिबन् इव अभ्यद्रवत् अरविन्दलोचनं पद्भ्यां जघान।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अत्यमर्षणः  Extremely angry/intolerant, दुरासदः difficult to confront, चण्डजवः having fierce speed, दुरत्ययः and invincible, सः he (Keshi), तम् अभिमुखः निशाम्य seeing Him in front of him, मुखेन खं पिबन् इव as if drinking the sky with his mouth, अभ्यद्रवत् charged towards Him, अरविन्दलोचनं पद्भ्यां जघान and struck the lotus-eyed Lord with his legs.

Simple Meaning: Seeing the Lord, the extremely intolerant Keshi—who was invincible and moved with terrifying speed—charged directly at Him as if devouring the sky with his open mouth. He then struck the lotus-eyed Lord with his legs.

तद् वञ्चयित्वा तमधोक्षजो रुषा प्रगृह्य दोर्भ्यां परिविध्य पादयोः ।

सावज्ञमुत्सृज्य धनुःशतान्तरे यथोरगं तार्क्ष्यसुतो व्यवस्थितः ॥10.37.5॥

Word-by-word meaning: तत् that (blow), वञ्चयित्वा dodging, तम् him (Keshi), अधोक्षजः the transcendental Lord, रुषा with anger, प्रगृह्य seizing, दोर्भ्याम् with His two arms, परिविध्य whirling him around, पादयोः by the two legs, स-अवज्ञम् contemptuously, उत्सृज्य throwing away, धनुः-शत-अन्तरे at a distance of one hundred bow-lengths, यथा just like, उरगम् a snake, तार्क्ष्य-सुतः the son of Tarkshya (Garuda), व्यवस्थितः stood

Anvaya: अधोक्षजः तत् वञ्चयित्वा रुषा पादयोः प्रगृह्य दोर्भ्यां परिविध्य तं धनुःशतान्तरे सावज्ञम् उत्सृज्य, तार्क्ष्यसुतः उरगं यथा, व्यवस्थितः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अधोक्षजः The transcendental Lord, तत् वञ्चयित्वा dodging that (attack), रुषा पादयोः प्रगृह्य seizing him by the legs with anger, दोर्भ्यां परिविध्य whirling him with His arms, तं धनुःशतान्तरे सावज्ञम् उत्सृज्य contemptuously threw him to a distance of one hundred bows, तार्क्ष्यसुतः उरगं यथा just like the son of Tarkshya (Garuda) throws a snake, व्यवस्थितः and stood there.

Simple Meaning: Lord Adhokshaja dodged the horse's kick and, seizing Keshi by the legs with His arms, whirled him around in anger. He then contemptuously threw the asura a distance of eight hundred cubits (one hundred bow-lengths) and stood His ground, just as Garuda would toss aside a snake.

स लब्धसंज्ञः पुनरुत्थितो रुषा व्यादाय केशी तरसाऽऽपतद्धरिम् ।

सोऽप्यस्य वक्त्रे भुजमुत्तरं स्मयन् प्रवेशयामास यथोरगं बिले ॥10.37.6॥

Word-by-word meaning: सः he (Keshi), लब्ध-संज्ञः regaining consciousness, पुनः again, उत्थितः standing up, रुषा with anger, व्यादाय opening wide (his mouth), केशी Keshi, तरसा with great speed, आपतत् charged, हरिम् at Lord Hari, सः He (Krishna), अपि also, अस्य of him, वक्त्रे in the mouth, भुजम् arm, उत्तरम् the left, स्मयन् smiling, प्रवेशयामास thrust in, यथा just like, उरगम् a snake, बिले into a hole

Anvaya: लब्धसंज्ञः सः केशी पुनः उत्थितः रुषा (मुखं) व्यादाय तरसा हरिम् आपतत्, सः अपि स्मयन् अस्य वक्त्रे उत्तरं भुजं बिले उरगं यथा प्रवेशयामास।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: लब्धसंज्ञः सः केशी That Keshi having regained consciousness, पुनः उत्थितः stood up again, रुषा व्यादाय opening (his mouth) in anger, तरसा हरिम् आपतत् charged at Lord Hari with speed, सः अपि स्मयन् and He (Krishna) also smiling, अस्य वक्त्रे in his mouth, उत्तरं भुजं His left arm, बिले उरगं यथा like a snake into a hole, प्रवेशयामास thrust in.

Simple Meaning: After regaining consciousness, Keshi stood up and, in a fit of rage, charged at Lord Hari with his mouth wide open. Smiling calmly, the Lord thrust His left arm into the horse’s mouth as easily as one would slide a snake into its hole.

दन्ता निपेतुर्भगवद्भुजस्पृशस्ते केशिनस्तप्तमयः स्पृशो यथा ।

बाहुश्च तद्देहगतो महात्मनो यथाऽऽमयः संववृधे उपेक्षितः ॥10.37.7॥

Word-by-word meaning: दन्ताः teeth, निपेतुः fell out, भगवत्-भुज-स्पृशः touched by the arm of the Supreme Lord, ते those, केशिनः of Keshi, तप्त-मयः-स्पृशः as if touching glowing-hot iron, यथा just as, बाहुः the arm, च and, तत्-देह-गतः having entered his body, महात्मनः of the Great Soul (Krishna), यथा like, आमयः a disease, संववृधे swelled, उपेक्षितः neglected

Anvaya: भगवद्भुजस्पृशः ते केशिनः दन्ताः तप्तमयःस्पृशः यथा निपेतुः, महात्मनो बाहुः च ततद्देहगतः उपेक्षितः आमयः यथा संववृधे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: भगवद्भुजस्पृशः Touched by the arm of the Lord, ते केशिनः दन्ताः those teeth of Keshi, तप्तमयःस्पृशः यथा as if they had touched red-hot iron, निपेतुः fell out, महात्मनो बाहुः च and the arm of the Great Soul, तद्देहगतः having entered his (Keshi's) body, उपेक्षितः आमयः यथा like a neglected disease, संववृधे swelled up.

Simple Meaning: As soon as they touched the Lord’s arm, Keshi’s teeth fell out, feeling as if they had struck glowing-hot iron. Then, the arm of the Supreme Lord began to swell within the asura's body, expanding rapidly like a disease that has been left untreated.

समेधामानेन स कृष्णबाहुना निरुद्धवायुश्चरणांश्च विक्षिपन् ।

प्रस्विन्नगात्रः परिवृत्तलोचनः पपात लेण्डं विसृजन् क्षितौ व्यसुः ॥10.37.8॥

Word-by-word meaning: समेधमानेन by the expanding, सः he (Keshi), कृष्ण-बाहुना by the arm of Krishna, निरुद्ध-वायुः having his breath choked, चरणान् the legs, च and, विक्षिपन् kicking, प्रस्विन्न-गात्रः his body covered in perspiration, परिवृत्त-लोचनः his eyes rolling, पपात fell down, लेण्डम् stool, विसृजन् passing, क्षितौ on the earth, व्यसुः lifeless

Anvaya: समेधमानेन कृष्णबाहुना निरुद्धवायुः सः चरणान् विक्षिपन् प्रस्विन्नगात्रः परिवृत्तलोचनः लेण्डं विसृजन् व्यसुः (सन्) क्षितौ पपात।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: समेधमानेन कृष्णबाहुना By the expanding arm of Krishna, निरुद्धवायुः having his breath blocked, सः he (Keshi), चरणान् विक्षिपन् kicking his legs, प्रस्विन्नगात्रः with a perspiring body, परिवृत्तलोचनः with rolling eyes, लेण्डं विसृजन् passing stool, व्यसुः lifeless, क्षितौ पपात fell to the ground.

Simple Meaning: As Krishna's arm expanded within him, Keshi's windpipe was completely choked. Kicking his legs frantically, his body soaked in sweat and his eyes rolling in his head, the asura passed stool and fell to the ground dead.

तद्देहतः कर्कटिकाफलोपमाद् व्यसोरपाकृष्य भुजं महाभुजः ।

अविस्मितोऽयत्नहतारिरुत्स्मयैः प्रसूनवर्षैर्दिविषद्भिरीडितः ॥10.37.9॥

Word-by-word meaning: तत्-देहतः from that body, कर्कटिका-फल-उपमात् which resembled a ripe karkatika (cucumber) fruit, व्यसोः which was lifeless, अपाकृष्य pulling out, भुजम् His arm, महा-भुजः the mighty-armed Lord, अविस्मितः unperturbed, अ-यत्न-हत-अरिः having killed His enemy without any effort, उत्स्मयैः with joyful smiles, प्रसून-वर्षैः with showers of flowers, दिविषद्भिः by the demigods, ईडितः was glorified

Anvaya: महाभुजः (कृष्णः) व्यसोः कर्कटिकाफलोपमात् तद्देहतः भुजम् अपाकृष्य, अयत्नहतारिः अविस्मितः (सन्) दिविषद्भिः उत्स्मयैः प्रसूनवर्षैः ईडितः (बभूव)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: महाभुजः The mighty-armed Lord, व्यसोः कर्कटिकाफलोपमात् तद्देहतः from that lifeless body which (burst) like a cucumber fruit, भुजम् अपाकृष्य pulling out His arm, अयत्नहतारिः having killed the enemy effortlessly, अविस्मितः remained unperturbed, दिविषद्भिः by the demigods, उत्स्मयैः प्रसूनवर्षैः with joyful smiles and showers of flowers, ईडितः was glorified.

Simple Meaning: The mighty-armed Lord Krishna pulled His arm out of the asura's lifeless body, which had burst open like a ripe cucumber. Having killed His enemy without the slightest effort, the Lord remained calm and unperturbed, while the demigods in the sky glorified Him with joyful smiles and showered Him with flowers.

देवर्षिरुपसङ्गम्य भागवतप्रवरो नृप ।

कृष्णमक्लिष्टकर्माणं रहस्येतदभाषत ॥10.37.10॥

Word-by-word meaning: देवर्षिः the sage among the gods (Narada), उपसङ्गम्य approaching, भागवत-प्रवरः the best among the devotees of the Lord, नृप O King (Parikshit), कृष्णम् to Krishna, अक्लिष्ट-कर्माणम् whose actions are performed without any effort, रहसि in a secluded place, एतत् this, अभाषत spoke

Anvaya: नृप! भागवतप्रवरः देवर्षिः उपसङ्गम्य, अक्लिष्टकर्माणं कृष्णं रहसि एतत् अभाषत।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नृप O King, भागवतप्रवरः the greatest of the devotees, देवर्षिः the divine sage Narada, उपसङ्गम्य having approached, अक्लिष्टकर्माणं कृष्णं to Krishna who performs His tasks without effort, रहसि in private, एतत् अभाषत spoke this.

Simple Meaning: O King Parikshit, the great celestial sage Narada, the foremost of all devotees, approached Lord Krishna—who performs all His divine activities effortlessly—and spoke to Him in a secluded place as follows.

कृष्ण कृष्णाप्रमेयात्मन् योगेश जगदीश्वर ।

वासुदेवाखिलावास सात्वतां प्रवर प्रभो ॥10.37.11॥

Word-by-word meaning: कृष्ण Krishna, कृष्ण O Krishna, अप्रमेय-आत्मन् O immeasurable Self, योग-ईश O Lord of all mystic power, जगत्-ईश्वर O Lord of the universe, वासुदेव O son of Vasudeva, अखिल-आवास O shelter of all existence, सात्वताम् of the devotees (Yadavas), प्रवर O chief, प्रभो O Master

Anvaya: कृष्ण ! कृष्ण ! अप्रमेयात्मन् ! योगेश ! जगदीश्वर ! वासुदेव ! अखिलावास ! सात्वतां प्रवर ! प्रभो ! (इति सम्बोधनम्)

Literal meaning of the anvaya: कृष्ण Krishna, कृष्ण O Krishna, अप्रमेयात्मन् O You of unfathomable nature, योगेश O Master of all yoga, जगदीश्वर O Controller of the universe, वासुदेव O Vasudeva, अखिलावास O Indweller of everything, सात्वतां प्रवर O best among the Satvatas, प्रभो O Almighty Lord.

Simple Meaning: O Krishna, O Krishna! O immeasurable Self, Lord of all mystic power and Master of the universe! O Vasudeva, the resting place of all existence and the jewel of the devotees, O Almighty Lord!

त्वमात्मा सर्वभूतानामेको ज्योतिरिवैधसाम् ।

गूढो गुहाशयः साक्षी महापुरुष ईश्वरः ॥10.37.12॥

Word-by-word meaning: त्वम् You, आत्मा the Self, सर्व-भूतानाम् of all living entities, एकः one, ज्योतिः fire, इव like, एधसाम् of firewood, गूढः hidden, गुह्-आशयः dwelling in the heart, साक्षी the witness, महा-पुरुषः the Supreme Person, ईश्वरः the Supreme Controller

Anvaya: त्वम् एधसां ज्योतिः इव सर्वभूतानाम् एकः आत्मा, गूढः गुहाशयः साक्षी महापुरुषः ईश्वरः (असि)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: त्वम् You, एधसां ज्योतिः इव like fire within firewood, सर्वभूतानाम् एकः आत्मा the one Soul of all living beings, गूढः hidden, गुहाशयः dwelling in the cave of the heart, साक्षी the witness, महापुरुषः the Supreme Person, ईश्वरः the Supreme Controller (are).

Simple Meaning: Just as fire is present within all firewood, You are the one Supreme Soul residing within all living beings. You are the hidden Witness dwelling in the cave of the heart, the Supreme Person and the ultimate Controller of all.

आत्मनाऽऽत्माश्रयः पूर्वं मायया ससृजे गुणान् ।

तैरिदं सत्यसंकल्पः सृजस्यत्स्यवसीश्वरः ॥10.37.13॥

Word-by-word meaning: आत्मना by Yourself, आत्मा-आश्रयः the self-supported, पूर्वम् in the beginning, मायया by Your material energy, ससृजे created, गुणान् the modes of nature, तैः by them (the modes), इदम् this (universe), सत्य-संकल्पः You whose will is always fulfilled, सृजसि You create, अत्सि You destroy, अवसि You maintain, ईश्वरः the Supreme Controller

Anvaya: आत्माश्रयः (त्वं) पूर्वम् आत्मना मायया गुणान् ससृजे, सत्यसंकल्पः ईश्वरः (त्वं) तैः इदं सृजसि अवसि अत्सि च।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: आत्माश्रयः The self-supported one, पूर्वम् in the beginning, आत्मना by Yourself, मायया through Your power of Maya, गुणान् ससृजे created the modes of nature; सत्यसंकल्पः ईश्वरः the Supreme Controller whose desire is truth, तैः by those modes, इदं this universe, सृजसि create, अवसि maintain, अत्सि and destroy.

Simple Meaning: You are self-supported and independent. In the beginning, You created the modes of nature through Your own material energy. O Supreme Controller, whose every will is truth, You utilize those modes to create, maintain, and eventually destroy this entire universe.

स त्वं भूधरभूतानां दैत्यप्रमथरक्षसाम् ।

अवतीर्णो विनाशाय सेतूनां रक्षणाय च ॥10.37.14॥

Word-by-word meaning: सः He, त्वम् You, भू-धर-भूतानाम् of those who are a burden to the earth, दैत्य-प्रमथ-रक्षसाम् of the Daityas, Pramathas, and Rakshasas, अवतीर्णः have descended, विनाशाय for the destruction, सेतूनाम् of the bridges (of dharma/religious principles), रक्षणाय for the protection, च and

Anvaya: सः त्वं भूधरभूतानां दैत्यप्रमथरक्षसां विनाशाय सेतूनां रक्षणाय च अवतीर्णः (असि)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सः त्वं That same You, भूधरभूतानां of those who are a burden to the earth, दैत्यप्रमथरक्षसां consisting of the Daityas, Pramathas, and Rakshasas, विनाशाय for the destruction, सेतूनां रक्षणाय च and for the protection of the boundaries of dharma, अवतीर्णः have descended.

Simple Meaning: That same Supreme Lord, You have now descended to this world to destroy the asuras, spirits, and monsters who have become a heavy burden to the earth, and to protect the sacred boundaries of religious principles.

दिष्ट्या ते निहतो दैत्यो लीलयायं हयाकृतिः ।

यस्य हेषितसंत्रस्तास्त्यजन्त्यनिमिषा दिवम् ॥10.37.15॥

Word-by-word meaning: दिष्ट्या by good fortune, ते by You, निहतः killed, दैत्यः the daitya, लीलया as a matter of play (effortlessly), अयम् this, हय-आकृतिः having the form of a horse, यस्य of whom, हेषित-सन्त्रस्ताः terrified by the neighing, त्यजन्ति leave, अनिमिषाः the demigods (the "non-blinking" ones), दिवम् heaven

Anvaya: यस्य हेषितसन्त्रस्ताः अनिमिषाः दिवं त्यजन्ति, अयं हयाकृतिः दैत्यः ते दिष्ट्या लीलया निहतः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यस्य Of whom, हेषितसन्त्रस्ताः terrified by the neighing, अनिमिषाः the demigods, दिवं त्यजन्ति abandon the heavens; अयं हयाकृतिः दैत्यः this daitya in the form of a horse, ते by You, दिष्ट्या by good fortune, लीलया निहतः has been killed effortlessly.

Simple Meaning: It is our great fortune that You have effortlessly killed this daitya in the form of a horse. His neighing was so terrifying that even the demigods would flee from heaven in fear of him.

चाणूरं मुष्टिकं चैव मल्लानन्यांश्च हस्तिनम् ।

कंसं च निहतं द्रक्ष्ये परश्वोऽहनि ते विभो ॥10.37.16॥

Word-by-word meaning: चाणूरम् Chanura, मुष्टिकम् Mushtika, च and, एव indeed, मल्लान् wrestlers, अन्यान् other, च and, हस्तिनम् the elephant (Kuvalayapida), कंसम् Kamsa, च and, निहतम् killed, द्रक्ष्ये I shall see, परश्वः-अहनि the day after tomorrow, ते by You, विभो O Almighty Lord

Anvaya: विभो ! परश्वोऽहनि ते (द्वारा) चाणूरं मुष्टिकं च अन्यान् मल्लान् एव च हस्तिनं कंसं च निहतं द्रक्ष्ये।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: विभो O Almighty Lord, परश्वोऽहनि on the day after tomorrow, ते by You, चाणूरं मुष्टिकं च Chanura and Mushtika, अन्यान् मल्लान् एव and other wrestlers, च हस्तिनं and the elephant, कंसं च and Kamsa, निहतं killed, द्रक्ष्ये I shall see.

Simple Meaning: O Almighty Lord, on the day after tomorrow, I shall witness You slaying the wrestlers Chanura and Mushtika, along with their associates, the great elephant Kuvalayapida, and finally King Kamsa himself.

तस्यानु शङ्खयवनमुराणां नरकस्य च ।

पारिजातापहरणमिन्द्रस्य च पराजयम् ॥10.37.17॥

Word-by-word meaning: तस्य-अनु after that, शङ्ख-यवन-मुराणाम् of Shankha, Kalayavana, and Mura, नरकस्य of Narakasura, च and, पारिजात-अपहरणम् the taking away of the Parijata tree, इन्द्रस्य of Indra, च and, पराजयम् the defeat

Anvaya: तस्यानु शङ्खयवनमुराणां नरकस्य च (वधं), पारिजातापहरणम् इन्द्रस्य पराजयं च (द्रक्ष्ये)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तस्यानु Following that, शङ्खयवनमुराणाम् of Shankha, Yavana, and Mura, नरकस्य च and of Naraka (the killing), पारिजातापहरणम् the carrying away of the Parijata tree, इन्द्रस्य पराजयं च and the defeat of Indra (I shall see).

Simple Meaning: After that, I shall see the destruction of the asuras Shankha, Kalayavana, Mura, and Narakasura. I shall also witness You taking the Parijata tree from the celestial gardens after defeating Lord Indra.

उद्वाहं वीरकन्यानां वीर्यशुल्कादिलक्षणम् ।

नृगस्य मोक्षणं पापाद् द्वारकायां जगत्पते ॥10.37.18॥

Word-by-word meaning: उद्वाहम् the marriage, वीर-कन्यानाम् of the daughters of heroes, वीर्य-शुल्क-आदि-लक्षणम् characterized by the payment of the price of valor and so on, नृगस्य of King Nriga, मोक्षणम् the deliverance, पापात् from the sin (curse), द्वारकायाम् in Dwaraka, जगत्-पते O Lord of the universe

Anvaya: जगत्पते ! (तथा च) वीर्यशुल्कादिलक्षणं वीरकन्यानाम् उद्वाहं, द्वारकायां नृगस्य पापात् मोक्षणं (द्रक्ष्ये)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: जगत्पते O Lord of the universe, (तथा च and also), वीर्यशुल्कादिलक्षणं marked by feats of prowess as the dowry, वीरकन्यानाम् उद्वाहं the marriage of heroic princesses, द्वारकायां in Dwaraka, नृगस्य पापात् मोक्षणं the liberation of King Nriga from his sin, (द्रक्ष्ये I shall see).

Simple Meaning: O Lord of the universe, I shall also see Your marriages to the daughters of heroes, won by Your own displays of valor. Furthermore, I will witness You delivering King Nriga from his curse while You are residing in Dwaraka.

स्यमन्तकस्य च मणेरादानं सह भार्यया ।

मृतपुत्रप्रदानं च ब्राह्मणस्य स्वधामतः॥10.37.19॥

Word-by-word meaning: स्यमन्तकस्य of the Syamantaka, च and, मणेः of the jewel, आदानम् the taking, सह with, भार्यया (Your) wife (Jambavati), मृत-पुत्र-प्रदानम् the giving back of the dead sons, च and, ब्राह्मणस्य of the Brahmana, स्व-धामतः from Your own abode (or from the abode of death)

Anvaya: स्यमन्तकस्य मणेः भार्यया सह आदानं, ब्राह्मणस्य च मृतपुत्रप्रदानं स्वधामतः (द्रक्ष्ये)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: स्यमन्तकस्य मणेः The taking of the Syamantaka jewel, भार्यया सह along with a wife, च and, ब्राह्मणस्य मृतपुत्रप्रदानं the returning of the Brahmana's dead sons, स्वधामतः from Your own divine realm (I shall see).

Simple Meaning: I shall see You recover the Syamantaka jewel and wed Your wife Jambavati. I shall also witness You bringing back the deceased sons of a Brahmana, retrieving them even from the realm of the beyond.

Context Note: In this sequence, Narada Muni is offering a "preview" of Lord Krishna's future pastimes. This specific verse refers to:

पौण्ड्रकस्य वधं पश्चात् काशिपुर्याश्च दीपनम् ।

दन्तवक्त्रस्य निधनं चैद्यस्य च महाक्रतौ ॥10.37.20॥

Word-by-word meaning: पौण्ड्रकस्य of Paundraka, वधम् the killing, पश्चात् afterward, काशि-पुर्याः of the city of Kashi (Varanasi), च and, दीपनम् the burning, दन्तवक्त्रस्य of Dantavaktra, निधनम् the death, चैद्यस्य of the King of Chedi (Shishupala), च and, महा-क्रतौ during the great sacrifice (Rajasuya)

Anvaya: पश्चात् पौण्ड्रकस्य वधं काशिपुर्याः दीपनं च, महाक्रतौ चैद्यस्य च दन्तवक्त्रस्य निधनं (द्रक्ष्ये)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: पश्चात् Afterward, पौण्ड्रकस्य वधं the killing of Paundraka, काशिपुर्याः दीपनं च and the burning of the city of Kashi; महाक्रतौ during the great sacrifice (Rajasuya), चैद्यस्य of Shishupala, च and, दन्तवक्त्रस्य निधनं the death of Dantavaktra (I shall see).

Simple Meaning: Later, I shall see You slay the false king Paundraka and set fire to the city of Kashi. I shall also witness the death of Shishupala during the great Rajasuya sacrifice, as well as the slaying of Dantavaktra.

Historical Note:

यानि चान्यानि वीर्याणि द्वारकामावसन् भवान् ।

कर्ता द्रक्ष्याम्यहं तानि गेयानि कविभिर्भुवि ॥10.37.21॥

Word-by-word meaning: यानि which, च and, अन्यानि other, वीर्याणि heroic deeds, द्वारकाम् आवसन् residing in Dwaraka, भवान् You, कर्ता will perform, द्रक्ष्यामि I shall see, अहम् I, तानि them, गेयानि sung, कविभिः by poets, भुवि on the earth

Anvaya: द्वारकाम् आवसन् भवान् यानि अन्यानि च वीर्याणि कर्ता, भुवि कविभिः गेयानि तानि (सर्वाणि) अहं द्रक्ष्यामि।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: द्वारकाम् आवसन् While residing in Dwaraka, भवान् You, यानि अन्यानि च वीर्याणि whatever other heroic deeds, कर्ता will perform; भुवि on the earth, कविभिः गेयानि which are to be sung by poets, तानि all of those, अहं द्रक्ष्यामि I shall see.

Simple Meaning: I shall also witness all those other heroic deeds You will perform while residing in Dwaraka—deeds that will be celebrated in song by the greatest poets throughout the world.

Reflection on Narada's Vision: Narada Muni is essentially providing a "trailer" for the rest of the Srimad Bhagavatam. Having seen the Lord's childhood pastimes in Vraja (ending here with the killing of Keshi), he looks forward with divine vision to the Dwaraka-lila, where Krishna acts as a King, a diplomat, and the supreme protector of Dharma.

अथ ते कालरूपस्य क्षपयिष्णोरमुष्य वै ।

अक्षौहिणीनां निधनं द्रक्ष्याम्यर्जुनसारथेः ॥10.37.22॥

Word-by-word meaning: अथ and then, ते of You, काल-रूपस्य in the form of Time, क्षपयिष्णोः of the destroyer, अमुष्य of this (world), वै indeed, अक्षौहिणीनाम् of the massive military divisions, निधनम् the destruction, द्रक्ष्यामि I shall see, अर्जुन-सारथेः of the chariot driver of Arjuna

Anvaya: अथ कालरूपस्य अमुष्य क्षपयिष्णोः ते (द्वारा) अर्जुनसारथेः (सतः) अक्षौहिणीनां निधनं द्रक्ष्यामि।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अथ And then, कालरूपस्य in the form of all-devouring Time, अमुष्य क्षपयिष्णोः of the destroyer of this world, ते by You, अर्जुनसारथेः acting as the chariot driver of Arjuna, अक्षौहिणीनां निधनं the destruction of the vast armies, द्रक्ष्यामि I shall see.

Simple Meaning: And then, I shall witness You in Your form as Time, the ultimate destroyer of this world. Acting as the charioteer for Arjuna, You will bring about the total annihilation of the massive military divisions gathered on the battlefield.

Contextual Notes:

विशुद्धविज्ञानघनं स्वसंस्थया समाप्तसर्वार्थममोघवाञ्छितम् ।

स्वतेजसा नित्यनिवृत्तमायागुणप्रवाहं भगवन्तमीमहि ॥10.37.23॥

Word-by-word meaning: विशुद्ध-विज्ञान-घनम् the embodiment of pure, concentrated knowledge, स्व-संस्थया by Your own spiritual nature, समाप्त-सर्व-अर्थम् who has attained all possible desires/goals, अमोघ-वाञ्छितम् whose will is never frustrate, स्व-तेजसा by Your own brilliance, नित्य-निवृत्त-माया-गुण-प्रवाहम् who has eternally cast off the flow of the material modes of Maya, भगवन्तम् the Supreme Lord, ईमहि we approach/bow down/worship

Anvaya: विशुद्धविज्ञानघनं स्वसंस्थया समाप्तसर्वार्थम् अमोघवाञ्छितं स्वतेजसा नित्यनिवृत्तमायागुणप्रवाहं भगवन्तं (त्वां) ईमहि।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: विशुद्धविज्ञानघनं The concentrated form of pure knowledge, स्वसंस्थया by Your own being, समाप्तसर्वार्थम् having achieved all purposes, अमोघवाञ्छितं whose desires are always fulfilled, स्वतेजसा by Your own power, नित्यनिवृत्तमायागुणप्रवाहं having eternally repelled the flow of the material modes, भगवन्तं to the Supreme Lord (You), ईमहि we offer our obeisances/approach for shelter.

Simple Meaning: We offer our respectful obeisances unto You, the Supreme Lord, who is the concentrated essence of pure consciousness. By Your own spiritual nature, You are eternally complete, having achieved all possible goals, and Your will is never obstructed. Through Your own divine brilliance, You have forever cast aside the influence of the material modes of nature.

Key Concepts in this Verse:

Note: This verse concludes Narada Muni's prayer before he departs, having successfully "forecasted" the rest of Krishna's mission on Earth.

त्वामीश्वरं स्वाश्रयमात्ममायया विनिर्मिताशेषविशेषकल्पनम् ।

क्रीडार्थमद्यात्तमनुष्यविग्रहं नतोऽस्मि धुर्यं यदुवृष्णिसात्वताम् ॥10.37.24॥

Word-by-word meaning: त्वाम् to You, ईश्वरम् the Supreme Controller, स्व-आश्रयम् self-supported, आत्म-मायया by Your own internal potency, विनिर्मित-अशेष-विशेष-कल्पनम् who has manifested the endless variety of this universe, क्रीडा-अर्थम् for the sake of pastimes, अद्य at this time, आत्त-मनुष्य-विग्रहम् who has assumed a human form, नतः अस्मि I bow down, धुर्यम् the leader/chief, यदु-वृष्णि-सात्वताम् of the Yadu, Vrishni, and Satvata clans

Anvaya: आत्ममायया विनिर्मिताशेषविशेषकल्पनं स्वाश्रयम् ईश्वरं त्वाम् (अहं) नतः अस्मि, यः अद्य क्रीडार्थम् आत्तमनुष्यविग्रहं यदुवृष्णिसात्वतां धुर्यम् (असि)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: आत्ममायया Through Your own power, विनिर्मिताशेषविशेषकल्पनं having fashioned the infinite variety of the universe, स्वाश्रयम् ईश्वरं to the self-dependent Lord, त्वाम् to You, (अहं) नतः अस्मि I am bowed down; (You) यः who, अद्य now, क्रीडार्थम् for the purpose of play, आत्तमनुष्यविग्रहं have taken a human form, यदुवृष्णिसात्वतां धुर्यम् the leader of the Yadus, Vrishnis, and Satvatas.

Simple Meaning: I offer my obeisances unto You, the self-supported Supreme Controller, who has manifested the infinite variety of this entire universe through Your own divine potency. For the sake of Your pastimes, You have now assumed this human form and appeared as the illustrious leader of the Yadu, Vrishni, and Satvata dynasties.

Analysis & Significance:

Note: This verse marks the completion of Narada Muni's stuti (hymn of praise). After this, the Bhagavata Purana describes Narada taking leave of Krishna and departing, while Krishna continues His mission in Vraja before heading to Mathura.

श्री शुक उवाच

एवं यदुपतिं कृष्णं भागवतप्रवरो मुनिः।

प्रणिपत्याभ्यनुज्ञातो ययौ तद्दर्शनोत्सवः ॥10.37.25॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुकः उवाच Sri Shuka said, एवम् in this way, यदु-पतिम् the Lord of the Yadus, कृष्णम् Krishna, भागवत-प्रवरः the best among the devotees, मुनिः the sage (Narada), प्रणिपत्य bowing down, अभ्यनुज्ञातः having been permitted to leave, ययौ went, तत्-दर्शन-उत्सवः he for whom the sight (of Krishna) was a festival

Anvaya: तद्दर्शनोत्सवः भागवतप्रवरः मुनिः एवं यदुपतिं कृष्णं प्रणिपत्य अभ्यनुज्ञातः (सन्), ययौ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तद्दर्शनोत्सवः He who felt the festival of seeing Him, भागवतप्रवरः मुनिः the best among devotees, the sage Narada, एवं in this manner, यदुपतिं कृष्णं to Krishna, the Lord of the Yadus, प्रणिपत्य having offered obeisances, अभ्यनुज्ञातः and being permitted to depart, ययौ went away.

Simple Meaning: Shri Shuka said: After offering his respectful obeisances in this way to Lord Krishna, the Master of the Yadus, the great sage Narada—the foremost of devotees—received permission to leave. Having experienced the great festival of seeing the Lord, he departed.

Contextual Highlights:

भगवानपि गोविन्दो हत्वा केशिनमाहवे ।

पशूनपालयत् पालैः प्रीतैर्व्रजसुखावहः ॥10.37.26॥

Word-by-word meaning: भगवान् the Supreme Lord, अपि also, गोविन्दः Govinda (the protector of cows), हत्वा having killed, केशिनम् Keshi, आहवे in the battle, पशून् the animals (cows), अपालयत् tended/protected, पालैः with the cowherd boys, प्रीतैः who were pleased, व्रज-सुख-आवहः bringing happiness to the land of Vraja

Anvaya: आहवे केशिनं हत्वा भगवान् गोविन्दः अपि प्रीतैः पालैः (सह) पशून् अपालयत्, व्रजसुखावहः (च अभवत्)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: आहवे In the battle, केशिनं हत्वा having killed Keshi, भगवान् गोविन्दः Lord Govinda, अपि also, प्रीतैः पालैः with the delighted cowherd boys, पशून् अपालयत् tended to the cows, (च and), व्रजसुखावहः brought joy to Vraja.

Simple Meaning: After slaying Keshi in battle, the Supreme Lord Govinda returned to His daily activity of tending the cows along with His delighted cowherd friends. In this way, He continued to bring transcendental happiness to all the residents of Vraja.

एकदा ते पशून् पालाश्चारयन्तोऽद्रिसानुषु ।

चक्रुर्निलायनक्रीडाश्चोरपालापदेशतः ॥10.37.27॥

Word-by-word meaning: एकदा once, ते those, पशून् the animals, पालाः the cowherd boys, चारयन्तः grazing, अद्रि-सानुषु on the peaks/slopes of the mountain (Govardhana), चक्रुः they performed, निलायन-क्रीडाः the game of "hide and seek" (lit: "hiding-game"), चोर-पाल-अपदेशतः under the pretext of being "thieves and guardians."

Anvaya: एकदा अद्रिसानुषु पशून् चारयन्तः ते पालाः चोरपालापदेशतः निलायनक्रीडाः चक्रुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: एकदा Once, अद्रिसानुषु on the mountain slopes, पशून् चारयन्तः while grazing the animals, ते पालाः those cowherd boys, चोरपालापदेशतः through the role-play of thieves and protectors, निलायनक्रीडाः hide-and-seek games, चक्रुः performed.

Simple Meaning: One day, while those cowherd boys were grazing their animals on the slopes of the mountain, they began playing a game of hide-and-seek, acting out the roles of "thieves" and "protectors."

तत्रासन् कतिचिच्चोराः पालाश्च कतिचिन्नृप ।

मेषायिताश्च तत्रैके विजह्रुरकुतोभयाः ॥10.37.28॥

Word-by-word meaning: तत्र there, आसन् were, कतिचित् some, चोराः thieves, पालाः protectors/guardians, च and, कतिचित् some, नृप O King (Parikshit), मेषायिताः acting as sheep, च and, तत्र there, एके some, विजह्रुः they played, अकुतः-भयाः without any fear

Anvaya: नृप ! तत्र कतिचित् चोराः कतिचित् च पालाः आसन्। तत्र एके मेषायिताः (आसन्)। (एवं सर्वे) अकुतोभयाः विजह्रुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya:  नृप ! O King, तत्र कतिचित् चोराः there (in that game) some were thieves, कतिचित् च पालाः आसन् and some were protectors. तत्र एके मेषायिताः (आसन्) Some were acting as sheep. (एवं सर्वे) अकुतोभयाः विजह्रुः In this way, they all played, free from any fear.

Simple Meaning: O King, in that game, some boys took the role of thieves while others acted as guardians. Some of the boys even pretended to be sheep. Thus, they all played together joyfully, feeling completely secure and fearless.

Key Observations:

मयपुत्रो महामायो व्योमो गोपालवेषधृक् ।

मेषायितानपोवाह प्रायश्चोरायितो बहून् ॥10.37.29॥

Word-by-word meaning: मय-पुत्रः the son of Maya Danava, महा-मायः a great magician (master of illusion), व्योमः (named) Vyoma, गोपाल-वेष-धृक् wearing the guise of a cowherd boy, मेषायितान् those who were acting as sheep, अपोवाह carried away, प्रायः mostly, चोरायितः acting as a thief, बहून् many

Anvaya: मयपुत्रः महामायः व्योमः गोपालवेषधृक् (सन्) चोरायितः (भूत्वा) मेषायितान् बहून् प्रायः अपोवाह।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: मयपुत्रः The son of Maya, महामायः the great illusionist, व्योमः Vyoma, गोपालवेषधृक् wearing the dress of a cowherd, चोरायितः acting as a thief (in the game), मेषायितान् बहून् many of those acting as sheep, प्रायः for the most part, अपोवाह carried away.

Simple Meaning: A powerful magician named Vyomasura, the son of the asura Maya, arrived there disguised as a cowherd boy. Joining the game in the role of a "thief," he began carrying away many of the boys who were playing the part of "sheep."

Significance of the Names:

गिरिदर्यां विनिक्षिप्य नीतं नीतं महासुरः ।

शिलया पिदधे द्वारं चतुःपञ्चावशेषिताः ॥10.37.30॥

Word-by-word meaning: गिरि-दर्याम् in a mountain cave, विनिक्षिप्य having thrown/placed, नीतम् नीतम् each one that was taken, महा-असुरः the great asura, शिलया with a boulder, पिदधे blocked/sealed, द्वारम् the entrance, चतुः-पञ्च-अवशेषिताः only four or five (boys) were left

Anvaya: महासुरः नीतं नीतं (बालं) गिरिदर्यां विनिक्षिप्य शिलया द्वारं पिदधे। (एवं तत्र) चतुःपञ्चावशेषिताः (आसन्)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: महासुरः The great asura, नीतं नीतं (बालं) each boy that was led away, गिरिदर्यां in a mountain cave, विनिक्षिप्य having placed, शिलया with a rock, द्वारं पिदधे sealed the entrance. (In this way), चतुःपञ्चावशेषिताः only four or five (boys) remained.

Simple Meaning: The great asura took the boys one by one and trapped them inside a mountain cave, sealing the entrance with a massive boulder. He did this until only four or five cowherd boys were left in the clearing.

Narrative Note: Vyomasura was essentially playing a "deadly" version of the game. By using a cave as a holding cell and a boulder as a door, he intended to kidnap all of Krishna's friends. The phrase "नीतं नीतं" (taken and taken) emphasizes the repetitive, cold efficiency with which the demon was operating while the boys were distracted by their play.

तस्य तत् कर्म विज्ञाय कृष्ण शणदः सताम् ।

गोपान् नयन्तं जग्राह वृकं हरिरिवौजसा ॥10.37.31॥

Word-by-word meaning: तस्य his (Vyomasura's), तत् that, कर्म action, विज्ञाय understanding, कृष्णः Krishna, शरण-दः the giver of shelter, सताम् to the virtuous/devotees, गोपान् the cowherd boys, नयन्तम् (who was) carrying away, जग्राह seized, वृकम् a wolf, हरिः a lion, इव like, ओजसा with great force

Anvaya: सतां शरणदः कृष्णः तस्य तत् कर्म विज्ञाय, गोपान् नयन्तं (तं) ओजसा, वृकं हरिः इव, जग्राह।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सतां शरणदः The giver of shelter to the virtuous, कृष्णः Krishna, तस्य तत् कर्म विज्ञाय having understood his (the asura's) activity, गोपान् नयन्तं (तं) him who was leading away the cowherds, ओजसा with might, वृकं हरिः इव like a lion (seizing) a wolf, जग्राह seized.

Simple Meaning: Lord Krishna, the ultimate shelter of the virtuous, understood exactly what the asura was doing. Just as a lion powerfully seizes a wolf, Krishna grabbed the asura with great force while he was in the act of carrying away the cowherd boys.

स निजं रूपमास्थाय गिरीन्द्रसदृशं बली ।

इच्छन् विमोक्तुमात्मानं नाशक्नोद् ग्रहणातुरः ॥10.37.32॥

Word-by-word meaning: सः he (Vyomasura), निजम् his own, रूपम् form, आस्थाय assuming, गिरि-इन्द्र-सदृशम् like a great mountain, बली powerful, इच्छन् desiring, विमोक्तुम् to release, आत्मानम् himself, न अशक्नोत् was not able, ग्रहण-आतुरः suffering from the grip

Anvaya: बली सः गिरिन्द्रसदृशं निजं रूपम् आस्थाय आत्मानं विमोक्तुम् इच्छन् (अपि), ग्रहणातुरः (सन्) न अशक्नोत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: बली सः That powerful asura, गिरिन्द्रसदृशं निजं रूपम् आस्थाय assuming his own form which was like a great mountain, आत्मानं विमोक्तुम् इच्छन् desiring to free himself, ग्रहणातुरः being distressed by the grip (of Krishna), न अशक्नोत् was not able.

Simple Meaning: The powerful asura then assumed his original form, which was as massive as a great mountain. He struggled with all his might to free himself from Krishna's grasp, but being pinned down and distressed by that invincible grip, he was unable to move.

Highlights:

तं निगृह्याच्युतो दोर्भ्यां पातयित्वा महीतले ।

पश्यतां दिवि देवानां पशुमारममारयत् ॥10.37.33॥

Word-by-word meaning: तम् him (Vyomasura), निगृह्य seizing/restraining, अच्युतः the Infallible Lord (Krishna), दोर्भ्याम् with His two arms, पातयित्वा throwing down, मही-तले on the surface of the earth, पश्यताम् while they were watching, दिवि in the sky, देवानाम् of the demigods, पशु-मारम् as one kills an animal (specifically a sacrificial animal), अमारयत् He killed

Anvaya: अच्युतः तं दोर्भ्यां निगृह्य महीतले पातयित्वा, दिवि देवानां पश्यतां (सतां), पशुमारम् अमारयत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अच्युतः The Infallible Lord, तं him, दोर्भ्यां निगृह्य seizing with two arms, महीतले पातयित्वा having thrown him on the ground, दिवि देवानां पश्यतां while the gods in heaven were watching, पशुमारम् in the manner of slaughtering an animal, अमारयत् killed him.

Simple Meaning: Lord Achyuta seized the asura with His two arms and hurled him to the ground. Then, while the demigods watched from the heavens, He killed him just as one would slaughter a sacrificial animal.

गुहापिधानं निर्भिद्य गोपान् निःसार्य कृच्छ्रतः ।

स्तूयमानः सुरैर्गोपैः प्रविवेश स्वगोकुलम् ॥10.37.34॥

Word-by-word meaning: गुहा-पिधानम् the covering of the cave, निर्भिद्य breaking to pieces, गोपान् the cowherd boys, निःसार्य bringing out, कृच्छ्रतः from their difficult situation/distress, स्तूयमानः being praised, सुरैः by the demigods, गोपैः (and) by the cowherd boys, प्रविवेश He re-entered, स्व-गोकुलम् His own village of Gokula

Anvaya: (कृष्णः) गुहापिधानं निर्भिद्य गोपान् कृच्छ्रतः निःसार्य, सुरैः गोपैः (च) स्तूयमानः स्वगोकुलं प्रविवेश।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (कृष्णः) गुहापिधानं निर्भिद्य (Krishna), having shattered the cave's entrance-block, गोपान् कृच्छ्रतः निःसार्य and having led the cowherd boys out from their distress, सुरैः गोपैः (च) स्तूयमानः being praised by the demigods and the cowherd boys, स्वगोकुलं प्रविवेश entered His own Gokula.

Simple Meaning: After shattering the massive boulder that blocked the cave, Krishna rescued the cowherd boys from their life-threatening predicament. Then, as the demigods and His friends sang His praises, He returned to His home in Gokula.

इति श्रीमद्भागवते महापुराणे पारमहंस्यां संहितायां दशमस्कन्धे पूर्वार्धे “केशीव्योमासुरवधो नाम” सप्तत्रिंशः अध्यायः ॥37॥

Thus ends the thirty-seventh discourse named “Krishna slays Keshi and Vyomasura”, in the first half of Book Ten of the great and glorious Bhagavata-Purana.
