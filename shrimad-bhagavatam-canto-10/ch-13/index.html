श्री शुक उवाच

साधु पृष्टं महाभाग त्वया भागवतोत्तम ।

यन्नूतनयसीशस्य शृण्वन्नपि कथां मुहुः ॥10.13.1॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुक उवाच Shri Shuka said, साधु well done/excellent, पृष्टम् asked/questioned, महाभाग O most fortunate one, त्वया by you, भागवत-उत्तम O best among devotees, यत् that which, नूतनयसि you make fresh/new, ईशस्य of the Lord, शृण्वन् hearing, अपि even, कथाम् the stories/topics, मुहुः again and again

Anvaya: श्री शुक उवाच — महाभाग! भागवतोत्तम! त्वया साधु पृष्टम्। यत् ईशस्य कथां मुहुः शृण्वन् अपि नूतनयसि।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री शुक उवाच Shri Shuka said — महाभाग O most fortunate one!, भागवतोत्तम O best among devotees!, त्वया By you, साधु पृष्टम् excellently asked. यत् That, ईशस्य कथां the stories of the Lord, मुहुः again and again, शृण्वन् अपि even while hearing, नूतनयसि you make fresh and new.

Simple meaning: Shri Shuka said — O most fortunate one!, O best among devotees Parikshit! You have asked excellently. For even while hearing the stories of the Lord again and again, you make them ever fresh and new.

Such is the nature of the true devotee for whom Krishna-Katha never grows stale.

सतामयं सारभृतां निसर्गो यदर्थवाणीश्रुतिचेतसामपि ।

प्रतिक्षणं नव्यवदच्युतस्य यत् स्त्रिया विटानामिव साधुवार्ता ॥10.13.2॥

Word-by-word meaning: सताम् of the saintly/good persons, अयम् this, सार-भृताम् of those who carry the essence, निसर्गः nature/natural disposition, यत् that, अर्थ-वाणी-श्रुति-चेतसाम् of those whose speech ears and minds are engaged (in The Eternal Lord), अपि even, प्रति-क्षणम् every moment, नव्य-वत् as if new/fresh, अच्युतस्य of Achyuta, यत् that which, स्त्रियाः of a woman, विटानाम् of libertines/lovers, इव like, साधु-वार्ता good/pleasing talk

Anvaya: सारभृतां सताम् अयं निसर्गः — यत् अर्थवाणीश्रुतिचेतसाम् अपि अच्युतस्य (कथा) प्रतिक्षणं नव्यवत् (भाति)  — यत् विटानां स्त्रियाः साधुवार्ता इव — (भाति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सारभृतां सताम् Of saintly persons who carry the essence, अयं निसर्गः this is the natural disposition — यत् that, अर्थवाणीश्रुतिचेतसाम् अपि even for those whose speech ears and minds are engaged in The Eternal Lord, अच्युतस्य (कथा) the stories of Achyuta, प्रतिक्षणं नव्यवत् (भाति) appear fresh and new every moment — यत् just as, विटानां स्त्रियाः साधुवार्ता for lovers the pleasing talk of their beloved woman, इव (भाति) appears ever fresh.

Simple meaning: This is the natural disposition of saintly persons who carry the essence — that the stories of Achyuta appear fresh and new every single moment even for those whose speech, ears and minds are engaged in The Eternal Lord — just as the pleasing talk of their beloved appears ever fresh and new to those who are enamored of her.

शृणुष्वाहितो राजन्नपि गुह्यं वदामि ते ।

ब्रूयुः स्निग्धस्य शिष्यस्य गुरवो गुह्यमप्युत ॥10.13.3॥

Word-by-word meaning: शृणुष्व listen, आहितः with full attention/carefully, राजन् O king, अपि even, गुह्यम् secret/confidential, वदामि I shall tell, ते to you, ब्रूयुः would tell, स्निग्धस्य of an affectionate/devoted, शिष्यस्य of a disciple, गुरवः the teachers/gurus, गुह्यम् secret/confidential, अपि even, उत indeed

Anvaya: राजन्! आहितः शृणुष्व। ते गुह्यम् अपि वदामि। गुरवः स्निग्धस्य शिष्यस्य गुह्यम् अपि उत ब्रूयुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: राजन् O king!, आहितः With full attention, शृणुष्व listen. ते To you, गुह्यम् अपि even what is secret and confidential, वदामि I shall tell. गुरवः Teachers, स्निग्धस्य शिष्यस्य of an affectionate and devoted disciple, गुह्यम् अपि उत even confidential matters, ब्रूयुः would indeed tell.

Simple meaning: O king! Listen with full attention — I shall tell you even what is secret and confidential. For teachers and gurus do indeed reveal even confidential matters to a devoted and affectionate disciple.

तथाघवदनान्मृत्यो रक्षित्वा वत्सपालकान् ।

सरित्पुलिनमानीय भगवानिदमब्रवीत् ॥10.13.4॥

Word-by-word meaning: तथा thus, अघ-वदनात् from the mouth of Agha, मृत्योः from death, रक्षित्वा having protected, वत्स-पालकान् the cowherd boys (protectors of calves), सरित्-पुलिनम् to the riverbank, आनय having brought, भगवान् the Lord, इदम् this, अब्रवीत् said

Anvaya: तथा भगवान् अघवदनात् मृत्योः वत्सपालकान् रक्षित्वा सरित्पुलिनम् आनय इदम् अब्रवीत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तथा भगवान् Thus the Lord, अघ-वदनात् from the mouth of Agha, मृत्योः from death, वत्स-पालकान् the cowherd boys, रक्षित्वा having protected, सरित्-पुलिनम् to the riverbank, आनय having brought, इदम् this, अब्रवीत् said

Simple meaning: Thus, the Lord, after rescuing the cowherd boys from the mouth of Aghasura (death), brought them to the bank of the river and spoke these words.

अहोऽतिरम्यं पुलिनं वयस्याः स्वकेलिसम्पन्मृदुलाच्छवालुकम् ।

स्फुटत्सरोगन्धहृतालिपत्रिकध्वनिप्रतिध्वानलसद्द्रुमाकुलम् ॥10.13.5॥

Word-by-word meaning: अहो oh, अति-रम्यम् very delightful, पुलिनम् riverbank, वयस्याः O friends, स्व-केलि-सम्पत् enriched for our play, मृदुल-आच्छ-वालुकम् soft, clean sand, स्फुटत् clearly manifest, सरः-गन्ध-हृत-अलि-पत्रिक-ध्वनि-प्रतिध्वान-लसत् the fragrance of the river with its blooming lotuses, which draws the bees, and resounding with the cooing of the birds, द्रुम-आकुलम् filled with trees

Anvaya: अहो वयस्याः! अतिरम्यं पुलिनं स्वकेलिसम्पत् मृदुलाच्छवालुकं, स्फुटत् सरोगन्धहृतालिपत्रिकध्वनिप्रतिध्वानलसत् द्रुमाकुलम्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अहो वयस्याः O friends!, अतिरम्यं Very delightful, पुलिनं riverbank, स्वकेलिसम्पत् enriched for our play, मृदुलाच्छवालुकं soft, clean sand, स्फुटत् clearly manifest, सरोगन्धहृतालिपत्रिकध्वनिप्रतिध्वानलसत् the fragrance of the river with its blooming lotuses, which draws the bees, and resounding with the cooing of the birds, द्रुमाकुलम् filled with trees.

Simple meaning: O friends! Just see this wonderfully delightful riverbank, perfect for our play, with soft and clean sand. It is filled with trees and resounds with the echoes of the sounds of bees and birds, all attracted by the fragrance of the nearby lake.

अत्र भोक्तव्यमस्माभिर्दिवा रूढं क्षुधार्दिताः ।

वत्साः समीपेऽपः पीत्वा चरन्तु शनकैस्तृणम् ॥10.13.6॥

Word-by-word meaning: अत्र here, भोक्तव्यम् should be eaten, अस्माभिः by us, दिवा at midday, रूढम् arrived, क्षुधा-अर्दिताः afflicted by hunger, वत्साः the calves, समीपे nearby, अपः water, पीत्वा having drunk, चरन्तु let them graze, शनकैः slowly, तृणम् grass

Anvaya: दिवा रूढम्। अत्र क्षुधार्दिताः अस्माभिः भोक्तव्यम्। वत्साः समीपे अपः पीत्वा शनकैः तृणं चरन्तु।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: दिवा रूढम् Day has advanced. अत्र Here, क्षुधार्दिताः afflicted by hunger, अस्माभिः by us, भोक्तव्यम् should be eaten. वत्साः The calves, समीपे nearby, अपः water, पीत्वा having drunk, शनकैः slowly, तृणं grass, चरन्तु let them graze

Simple meaning: Since it is now midday and we are hungry, we should take our meal here. Let the calves, after drinking water nearby, slowly graze on the grass.

तथेति पाययित्वार्भा वत्सानारुध्य शाद्वले ।

मुक्त्वा शिक्यानि बुभुजुः समं भगवता मुदा ॥10.13.7॥

Word-by-word meaning: तथा इति thus saying, पाययित्वा having made drink, अर्भाः the boys, वत्सान् the calves, आरुध्य let them out, शाद्वले on the grassy ground, मुक्त्वा having released, शिक्यानि lunch bags, बुभुजुः ate, समम् together, भगवता with Bhagavan, मुदा joyfully

Anvaya: अर्भाः तथा इति वत्सान् पाययित्वा शाद्वले आरुध्य, शिक्यानि मुक्त्वा भगवता समं मुदा बुभुजुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अर्भाः The boys, तथा इति thus, वत्सान् पाययित्वा having made the calves drink, शाद्वले आरुध्य letting them out on the grassy ground, शिक्यानि मुक्त्वा opening the lunch bags, भगवता समं together with Bhagavan, मुदा बुभुजुः joyfully ate.

Simple meaning: Thus, the boys made the calves drink water, let them out on the grassy ground, and then, opening their lunch bags, joyfully ate together with Bhagavan Krishna.

कृष्णस्य विष्वक् पुरुराजिमण्डलैरभ्याननाः फुल्लदृशो व्रजार्भकाः ।

सहोपविष्टा विपिने विरेजुश्छदा यथाम्भोरुहकर्णिकायाः ॥10.13.8॥

Word-by-word meaning: कृष्णस्य of Krishna, विष्वक् all around, पुरु-राजि-मण्डलैः by many rows/circles, अभ्याननाः facing towards, फुल्ल-दृशः with blooming eyes, व्रज-अर्भकाः the boys of Vraja, सह-उपविष्टाः sitting together, विपिने in the forest, विरेजुः shone, छदाः like the petals, यथा just as, अम्भोरुह-कर्णिकायाः of the lotus whorl

Anvaya: विपिने व्रजार्भकाः फुल्लदृशः कृष्णस्य विष्वक् पुरुराजिमण्डलैः अभ्याननाः सहोपविष्टाः अम्भोरुहकर्णिकायाः छदाः यथा विरेजुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: विपिने In the forest, व्रजार्भकाः the boys of Vraja, फुल्लदृशः with blooming eyes, कृष्णस्य विष्वक् all around Krishna, पुरुराजिमण्डलैः by many rows/circles, अभ्याननाः facing towards, सहोपविष्टाः sitting together, अम्भोरुहकर्णिकायाः छदाः यथा just as the petals of the lotus whorl, विरेजुः shone

Simple meaning: The boys of Vraja, with joyful blooming eyes, sat together in the forest in many circles around Krishna, all facing Him. They shone beautifully like the petals surrounding the central whorl of a lotus.

केचित् पुष्पैर्दलैः केचित् पल्लवैरङ्कुरैः फलैः ।

शिग्भिस्त्वग्भिर्दृषद्भिश्च बुभुजुः कृतभाजनाः ॥10.13.9॥

Word-by-word meaning: केचित् some, पुष्पैः with flowers, दलैः with leaves, केचित् some, पल्लवैः with tender leaves, अङ्कुरैः with sprouts, फलैः with fruits, शिग्भिः with bags, त्वग्भिः with bark, दृषद्भिः with stones, च and, बुभुजुः ate, कृत-भाजनाः having made utensils

Anvaya: केचित् पुष्पैः दलैः, केचित् पल्लवैः अङ्कुरैः फलैः, शिग्भिः त्वग्भिः दृषद्भिः च कृतभाजनाः बुभुजुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: केचित् पुष्पैः दलैः Some with flowers and leaves, केचित् पल्लवैः अङ्कुरैः फलैः some with tender leaves, sprouts, and fruits, शिक्-भिः with bags, त्वग्भिः with bark, दृषद्भिः with stones, च and, कृतभाजनाः having made utensils, बुभुजुः ate

Simple meaning: Some boys made their eating plates from flowers and leaves, others from tender sprouts and fruits, and some from bags, bark, and stones. Using these as utensils, they happily took their meal.

सर्वे मिथो दर्शयन्तः स्वस्वभोज्यरुचिं पृथक् ।

हसन्तो हासयन्तश्चाभ्यवजह्रुः सहेश्वराः ॥10.13.10॥

Word-by-word meaning: सर्वे all, मिथः mutually, दर्शयन्तः showing, स्व-स्व-भोज्य-रुचिम् their own respective food tastes, पृथक् separately, हसन्तः laughing, हासयन्तः making others laugh, च and, अभ्यवजह्रुः ate together, सह-ईश्वराः along with the Lord

Anvaya: सर्वे सहेश्वराः मिथः स्वस्वभोज्यरुचिं पृथक् दर्शयन्तः हसन्तः हासयन्तः च अभ्यवजह्रुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सर्वे सहेश्वराः Everyone along with the Lord, मिथः mutually, स्वस्वभोज्यरुचिं their own respective food tastes, पृथक् separately, दर्शयन्तः showing, हसन्तः laughing, हासयन्तः making others laugh, च and, अभ्यवजह्रुः ate together

Simple meaning: All the boys, along with the Lord, ate together while showing each other their different kinds of food, laughing and making one another laugh.

विभ्रद् वेणुं जठरपटयोः शृङ्गवेत्रे च कक्षे वामे पाणौ मसृणकवलं तत्फलान्यङ्गुलीषु।

तिष्ठन् मध्ये स्वपरिसुहृदो हासयन् नर्मभिः स्वैः स्वर्गे लोके मिषति बुभुजे यज्ञभुग् बालकेलिः ॥10.13.11॥

Word-by-word meaning: विभ्रत् holding, वेणुम् flute, जठर-पटयोः in the cloth around the waist, शृङ्ग-वेत्रे horn and stick, च and, कक्षे in the armpit/side, वामे in the left, पाणौ hand, मसृण-कवलम् soft morsel of food, तत्-फलानि its fruits, अङ्गुलीषु between the fingers, तिष्ठन् situated/seated, मध्ये in the middle, स्व-परि-सुहृदः of His own surrounding friends, हासयन् making laugh, नर्मभिः by jokes, स्वैः His own, स्वर्गे लोके in the heavenly world, मिषति while watching, बुभुजे enjoyed/ate, यज्ञ-भुक् the enjoyer of sacrifices, बाल-केलिः performing childlike pastimes

Anvaya: यज्ञभुक् बालकेलिः जठरपटयोः वेणुं विभ्रत्, कक्षे शृङ्गवेत्रे च, वामे पाणौ मसृणकवलं, अङ्गुलीषु तत्फलानि, स्वपरिसुहृदः मध्ये तिष्ठन् स्वैः नर्मभिः हासयन्, स्वर्गे लोके मिषति बुभुजे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यज्ञभुक् बालकेलिः The enjoyer of sacrifices, performing childlike pastimes, जठरपटयोः वेणुं विभ्रत् holding the flute in the cloth around the waist, कक्षे शृङ्गवेत्रे च and horn and stick in the armpits, वामे पाणौ मसृणकवलं in the left hand, soft morsel of food, अङ्गुलीषु तत्फलानि its fruits (pickle) between the fingers, स्वपरिसुहृदः of His own surrounding friends, मध्ये तिष्ठन् seated in the middle, स्वैः नर्मभिः हासयन् making laugh by His own jokes, स्वर्गे लोके मिषति while the denizens of the heavens watched, बुभुजे enjoyed/ate.

Simple meaning: Krishna, the enjoyer of sacrifices, engaged in childlike pastimes, sat  in the midst of His friends. Holding a flute in the cloth around his waist, with His horn and stick tucked at His side, and a morsel of food in His left hand with fruits between His fingers, He jokingly made His friends laugh while eating. The residents of heaven watched this wonderful scene.

भारतैवं वत्सपेषु भुञ्जानेष्वच्युतात्मसु ।

वत्सास्त्वन्तर्वने दूरं विविशुस्तृणलोभिताः ॥10.13.12॥

Word-by-word meaning: भारत O descendant of Bharata, एवम् thus, वत्सपेषु the boys who were the protector of the calves, भुञ्जानेषु while eating, अच्युत-आत्मसु whose minds were absorbed in Acyuta, वत्साः the calves, तु however, अन्तः-वने into the inner forest, दूरम् far, विविशुः entered, तृण-लोभिताः attracted by grass

Anvaya: भारत! एवम् अच्युतात्मसु वत्सपेषु भुञ्जानेषु वत्साः तु तृणलोभिताः दूरम् अन्तर्वने विविशुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: भारत O descendant of Bharata!, एवम् Thus, अच्युतात्मसु whose minds were absorbed in Acyuta, वत्सपेषु the boys who were the protector of the calves, भुञ्जानेषु while eating, वत्साः the calves, तु however, तृणलोभिताः attracted by grass, दूरम् far, अन्तर्वने into the inner forest, विविशुः entered.

Simple meaning: O descendant of Bharat! While the boys—whose minds were absorbed in Krishna—were thus eating, the calves, attracted by fresh grass, wandered far into the inner forest.

तान् दृष्ट्वा भयसंत्रस्तानूचे कृष्णोऽस्य भीभयम् ।

मित्राण्याशान्मा विरमतेहानेष्ये वत्सकानहम् ॥10.13.13॥

Word-by-word meaning: तान् them, दृष्ट्वा having seen, भय-संत्रस्तान् troubled with fear, ऊचे said, कृष्णः Krishna, अस्य of this (world), भी-भयम् the fear of fear (i.e. the one who removes all fear), मित्राणि O friends, आशान् your meal, मा विरमत do not stop, इह here, आनेष्ये I will bring, वत्सकान् the calves, अहम् I

Anvaya: अस्य भीभयं कृष्णः भयसंत्रस्तान् तान् दृष्ट्वा ऊचे — मित्राणि! आशान् मा विरमत, अहम् इह वत्सकान् आनेष्ये।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अस्य भीभयं कृष्णः The fear of all fear of this world, Krishna, भयसंत्रस्तान् तान् them who were troubled with fear, दृष्ट्वा having seen, ऊचे said, मित्राणि O friends!, आशान् मा विरमत Do not stop your meal, अहम् इह वत्सकान् आनेष्ये I will bring the the calves here.

Simple meaning: Krishna, the remover of all fear, seeing the boys frightened with anxiety, said — "O friends! Do not stop your meal. I myself will go and bring the calves back here."

इत्युक्त्वाद्रिदरीकुञ्जगह्वरेष्वात्मवत्सकान् ।

विचिन्वन् भगवान् कृष्णः सपाणिकवलो ययौ ॥10.13.14॥

Word-by-word meaning: इति thus, उक्त्वा having said, अद्रि-दरी-कुञ्ज-गह्वरेषु in the caves, thickets and hollows of the mountains, आत्म-वत्सकान् his own calves, विचिन्वन् searching for, भगवान् the Supreme Lord, कृष्णः Krishna, स-पाणि-कवलः with a morsel of food still in his hand, ययौ went

Anvaya: भगवान् कृष्णः इति उक्त्वा सपाणिकवलः अद्रिदरीकुञ्जगह्वरेषु आत्मवत्सकान् विचिन्वन् ययौ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: भगवान् कृष्णः The Supreme Lord Krishna, इति उक्त्वा having said thus, सपाणिकवलः with a morsel of food still in his hand, अद्रिदरीकुञ्जगह्वरेषु in the caves, thickets and hollows of the mountains, आत्मवत्सकान् his own calves, विचिन्वन् searching for, ययौ went.

Simple meaning: Having said thus, the Supreme Lord Krishna went off with a morsel of food still in his hand, searching for his calves in the caves, thickets and hollows of the mountains.

अम्भोजन्मजनिस्तदन्तरगतो मायार्भकस्येशितुर्द्रष्टुं मञ्जु महित्वमन्यदपि तद्वत्सानितो वत्सपान् ।

नीत्वान्यत्र कुरूद्वहान्तरदधात् खेऽवस्थितो यः पुरा दृष्ट्वाघासुरमोक्षणं प्रभवतः प्राप्तः परं विस्मयम् ॥10.13.15॥

Word-by-word meaning: अम्भोज-जन्म-जनिः one whose birth place is the lotus (i.e. Brahma), तत्-अन्तर-गतः having entered into that situation (where Krishna was eating lunch with His friends), माया-अर्भकस्य of the one who is a child by his own maya, ईशितुः of the supreme controller, द्रष्टुम् in order to see, मञ्जु beautiful, महित्वम् greatness, अन्यत् other, अपि also, तत् those, वत्सान् calves, इतः from here, वत्सपान् the cowherd boys, नीत्वा having taken, अन्यत्र to another place, कुरु-उद्वह O best of the Kurus, अन्तरदधात् vanished, खे in the sky, अवस्थितः remaining, यः who, पुरा previously, दृष्ट्वा having seen, अघासुर-मोक्षणम् the liberation of Aghasura, प्रभवतः of the almighty Lord, प्राप्तः having attained, परम् great, विस्मयम् wonder

Anvaya: कुरूद्वह! खे अवस्थितः यः पुरा प्रभवतः अघासुरमोक्षणं दृष्ट्वा परं विस्मयं प्राप्तः, सः अम्भोजन्मजनिः मायार्भकस्य ईशितुः मञ्जु महित्वं द्रष्टुं तदन्तरगतः, तत् वत्सान् वत्सपान् च इतः अन्यत्र नीत्वा अन्तरदधात्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: कुरूद्वह O best of the Kurus!, खे अवस्थितः While stationed in the sky, यः who, पुरा previously, प्रभवतः of the almighty Lord, अघासुरमोक्षणं the liberation of Aghasura, दृष्ट्वा having seen, परं विस्मयं प्राप्तः had felt great wonder, सः अम्भोजन्मजनिः he whose birthplace is the lotus (Brahma), मायार्भकस्य of the one who is a child by his own maya, ईशितुः of the supreme controller, मञ्जु महित्वं द्रष्टुं in order to see the greatness which is pleasing to the mind, तदन्तरगतः having entered into that situation, तत् वत्सान् वत्सपान् च those calves and cowherd boys, इतः from here, अन्यत्र नीत्वा to another place, having taken, अन्तरदधात् vanished.

Simple meaning: O best of the Kurus! Brahma, who had previously witnessed the liberation of Aghasura by the almighty Lord from the sky, and had been struck with great wonder, now wished to see further the beautiful greatness of Krishna — who, though the supreme controller, appeared as a child by his own maya. Seizing this opportunity, he took both the calves and the cowherd boys from there to another place and vanished.

ततो वत्सानदृष्ट्वैत्य पुलिनेऽपि च वत्सपान् ।

उभावपि वने कृष्णो विचिकाय समन्ततः ॥10.13.16॥

Word-by-word meaning: ततः then, वत्सान् the calves, अदृष्ट्वा not finding, एत्य having come back, पुलिने on the riverbank, अपि also, च and, वत्सपान् the cowherd boys, उभौ both, अपि also, वने in the forest, कृष्णः Krishna, विचिकाय searched, समन्ततः everywhere

Anvaya: ततः कृष्णः वत्सान् अदृष्ट्वा एत्य पुलिने वत्सपान् अपि च अदृष्ट्वा उभौ अपि वने समन्ततः विचिकाय।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: ततः कृष्णः Then Krishna, वत्सान् the calves, अदृष्ट्वा not finding, एत्य having come back, पुलिने on the riverbank, वत्सपान् अपि च अदृष्ट्वा and not finding the cowherd boys, also, उभौ both, अपि also, वने in the forest, समन्ततः विचिकाय everywhere, searched.

Simple meaning: Then Krishna, not finding the calves, came back to the riverbank, and not finding the cowherd boys there either, searched everywhere throughout the forest for both of them.

क्वाप्यदृष्ट्वान्तर्विपिने वत्सान् पालांश्च विश्ववित् ।

सर्वं विधिकृतं कृष्णः सहसावजगाम ह ॥10.13.17॥

Word-by-word meaning: क्व अपि anywhere, अदृष्ट्वा not finding, अन्तर्विपिने within the forest, वत्सान् the calves, पालान् the cowherd boys, च and, विश्ववित् the knower of everything, सर्वम् everything, विधि-कृतम् done by Brahma, कृष्णः Krishna, सहसा immediately, अवजगाम understood, ह indeed

Anvaya: विश्ववित् कृष्णः अन्तर्विपिने क्व अपि वत्सान् पालान् च अदृष्ट्वा सर्वं विधिकृतम् इति सहसा अवजगाम ह।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: विश्ववित् कृष्णः The knower of everything, Krishna, अन्तर्विपिने within the forest, क्व अपि anywhere, वत्सान् पालान् च the calves and the cowherd boys, अदृष्ट्वा not finding, सर्वं everything, विधिकृतम् done by Brahma, इति thus, सहसा अवजगाम ह immediately understood, indeed.

Simple meaning: Not finding the calves or the cowherd boys anywhere within the forest, Krishna — the knower of everything — immediately understood that all of this was indeed the doing of Brahma.

ततः कृष्णो मुदं कर्तुं तन्मातृणां च कस्य च ।

उभयायितमात्मानं चक्रे विश्वकृदीश्वरः ॥10.13.18॥

Word-by-word meaning: ततः then, कृष्णः Krishna, मुदम् joy, कर्तुम् in order to give, तत्-मातृणाम् of the mothers of those boys and calves, च and, कस्य च of Brahma as well, उभयायितम् having become both (i.e. both the calves and the cowherd boys), आत्मानम् himself, चक्रे made, विश्वकृत् the creator of the universe, ईश्वरः the supreme controller

क अकारान्तः, पुंलिङ्गः ब्रह्मा, सूर्यः, वायुः src. Shabdapatha, Ashtadhyayi (Brahma-  in this context)

Anvaya: ततः विश्वकृत् ईश्वरः कृष्णः तन्मातृणां च कस्य च मुदं कर्तुम् आत्मानम् उभयायितं चक्रे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: ततः Then, विश्वकृत् ईश्वरः कृष्णः the creator of the universe, the supreme controller, Krishna, तन्मातृणां च and of the mothers of those boys and calves, कस्य च of Brahma as well, मुदं कर्तुम् in order to give joy, आत्मानम् himself, उभयायितं having become both, चक्रे made.

Simple meaning: Then Krishna, the creator of the universe and the supreme controller, in order to give joy to the mothers of those boys and calves — and also to Brahma — expanded himself and took the form of both the calves and the cowherd boys.

यावद् वत्सपवत्सकाल्पकवपुर्यावत् कराङ्घ्य्रादिकं यावद् यष्टिविषाणवेणुदलशिग् यावद् विभूषाम्बरम् ।

यावच्छीलगुणाभिधाकृतिवयो यावद् विहारादिकं सर्वं विष्णुमयं गिरोऽङ्गवदजः सर्वस्वरूपो बभौ ॥10.13.19॥

Word-by-word meaning: यावत् as many, वत्सप-वत्सक-अल्पक-वपुः the small forms of the cowherd boys and calves, यावत् as many, कर-अङ्घ्रि-आदिकम् hands, feet and other limbs, यावत् as many, यष्टि-विषाण-वेणु-दल-शिक् sticks, horns, flutes, leaves and bags, यावत् as many, विभूषा-अम्बरम् ornaments and garments, यावत् as many, शील-गुण-अभिधा-आकृति-वयः character, qualities, names, forms and ages, यावत् as many, विहार-आदिकम् play and other activities, सर्वम् विष्णु-मयम् everything is full of Vishnu, गिरः of the words, अङ्गवत् like having a body (personification), अजः the unborn one (Krishna), सर्व-स्वरूपः having all forms, बभौ shone

Anvaya: यावत् वत्सपवत्सकाल्पकवपुः, यावत् कराङ्घ्र्यादिकं, यावत् यष्टिविषाणवेणुदलशिक्, यावत् विभूषाम्बरं, यावत् शीलगुणाभिधाकृतिवयः, यावत् विहारादिकं — “सर्वं विष्णुमयं” गिरः अङ्गवत् सर्वस्वरूपः अजः बभौ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यावत् वत्सपवत्सकाल्पकवपुः As many small forms of the cowherd boys and calves, यावत् कराङ्घ्र्यादिकं as many hands, feet and other limbs, यावत् यष्टिविषाणवेणुदलशिक् as many sticks, horns, flutes, leaves and baskets, यावत् विभूषाम्बरं as many ornaments and garments, यावत् शीलगुणाभिधाकृतिवयः as many character, qualities, names, forms and ages, यावत् विहारादिकं as much play and other activities  — “सर्वं विष्णुमयं” everything is full of Vishnu,  गिरः अङ्गवत् as it were the personification of these words, सर्वस्वरूपः अजः The unborn one who is in all forms of life, बभौ shone.

Simple meaning: The unborn Krishna, having assumed all forms, shone forth as everything — as many small bodies of the cowherd boys and calves, their hands, feet and all other limbs, their sticks, horns, flutes, leaves and ropes, their ornaments and garments, their character, qualities, names, appearances and ages, their play and all other activities. It was as if it were the personification of the words “Everything is full of Vishnu”.

स्वयमात्माऽऽत्मगोवत्सान् प्रतिवार्यात्मवत्सपैः ।

क्रीडन्नात्मविहारैश्च सर्वात्मा प्राविशद् व्रजम् ॥10.13.20॥

Word-by-word meaning: स्वयम् himself, आत्मा the Self of all, आत्म-गो-वत्सान् the calves who were his own expansions, प्रतिवार्य herding back, आत्म-वत्सपैः with the cowherd boys who were also his own expansions, क्रीडन् playing, आत्म-विहारैः with pastimes that were also himself, च and, सर्व-आत्मा the soul of all, प्राविशत् entered, व्रजम् Vraja

Anvaya: सर्वात्मा आत्मा स्वयम् आत्मगोवत्सान् प्रतिवार्य आत्मवत्सपैः आत्मविहारैः च क्रीडन् व्रजं प्राविशत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सर्वात्मा The soul of all, आत्मा the Self, स्वयम् himself, आत्मगोवत्सान् the calves who were his own expansions, प्रतिवार्य herding back, आत्मवत्सपैः with the cowherd boys who were also his own expansions, आत्मविहारैः with pastimes that were also himself, च and, क्रीडन् playing, व्रजं प्राविशत् entered Vraja.

Simple meaning: Krishna, the Self of all and the soul of everything, himself herded back the calves — who were none other than his own expansions — accompanied by the cowherd boys who were likewise his own expansions, and playing his own pastimes, entered Vraja.

तत्तद्वत्सान् पृथङ् नीत्वा तत्तद् गोष्ठे निवेश्य सः ।

तत्तद्त्माभवद् राजंस्तत्तत्सद्म प्रविष्टवान् ॥10.13.21॥

Word-by-word meaning: तत् तत् वत्सान् each and every group of calves, पृथक् separately, नीत्वा having led, तत् तत् गोष्ठे into each and every cowshed, निवेश्य having placed, सः He, तत् तत् आत्मा the self of each and every one of those boys, अभवत् became, राजन् O King, तत् तत् सद्म into each and every home, प्रविष्टवान् entered

Anvaya: राजन्! सः तत् तत् वत्सान् पृथक् नीत्वा तत् तत् गोष्ठे निवेश्य तत् तत् आत्मा अभवत् तत् तत् सद्म प्रविष्टवान्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: राजन् O King!, सः He, तत् तत् वत्सान् each and every group of calves, पृथक् separately, नीत्वा having led, तत् तत् गोष्ठे into each and every cowshed, निवेश्य having placed, तत् तत् आत्मा the self of each and every one of those boys, अभवत् became, तत् तत् सद्म into each and every home, प्रविष्टवान् entered.

Simple meaning: O King! He led each and every group of calves separately into their respective cowsheds, and having done so, became the very self of each and every one of those boys and entered each of their respective homes.

तन्मातरो वेणुरवत्वरोत्थिता उत्थाप्य दोर्भिः परिरभ्य निर्भरम् ।

स्नेहस्नुतस्तन्यपयः सुधासवं मत्वा परं ब्रह्म सुतानपाययन् ॥10.13.22॥

Word-by-word meaning: तत्-मातरः the mothers of those boys, वेणु-रव-त्वरा-उत्थिताः having risen quickly upon hearing the sound of the flute, उत्थाप्य lifting up, दोर्भिः with their arms, परिरभ्य embracing, निर्भरम् fully, स्नेह-स्नुत-स्तन्य-पयः whose breast milk flowed out of love, सुधा-असवम् like the nectar of immortality, मत्वा considering, परम् supreme, ब्रह्म Brahman, सुतान् their sons, अपाययन् gave to drink

Anvaya: तन्मातरः वेणुरवत्वरोत्थिताः उत्थाप्य दोर्भिः निर्भरं परिरभ्य स्नेहस्नुतस्तन्यपयः परं ब्रह्म सुतान् मत्वा सुधासवम् अपाययन्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तन्मातरः The mothers of those boys, वेणुरवत्वरोत्थिताः having risen quickly upon hearing the sound of the flute, उत्थाप्य lifting up, दोर्भिः with their arms, निर्भरं fully, परिरभ्य embracing, स्नेहस्नुतस्तन्यपयः whose breast milk flowed out of love, परं ब्रह्म the supreme Brahman, सुतान् their sons, मत्वा considering, सुधासवम् like the nectar of immortality, अपाययन् gave to drink.

Simple meaning: The mothers of those boys, having risen quickly upon hearing the sound of the flute, lifted their sons up and embraced them fully with their arms, their breast milk flowing out of pure love. Considering the supreme Brahman himself as their sons, they nursed Him.

ततो नृपोन्मर्दनमज्जलेपनालङ्काररक्षातिलकाशनादिभिः ।

संलालितः स्वाचरितैः प्रहर्षयन् सायं गतो यामयमेन माधवः ॥10.13.23॥

Word-by-word meaning: ततः then, नृप O King, उन्मर्दन-मज्जन-लेपन-अलङ्कार-रक्षा-तिलक-अशन-आदिभिः with massaging, bathing, anointing, decorating, protecting, applying tilaka, feeding and other such activities, संलालितः lovingly attended to, स्व-आचरितैः with his own pastimes, प्रहर्षयन् delighting, सायम् evening, गतः having come, याम-यमेन by a regular or stated occupation for every hour, माधवः Madhava

Anvaya: नृप! ततः सायं गतः माधवः यामयमेन उन्मर्दनमज्जनलेपनालङ्काररक्षातिलकाशनादिभिः संलालितः स्वाचरितैः प्रहर्षयन् ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नृप O King!, ततः Then, सायं गतः evening having come, माधवः Madhava, यामयमेन by a regular or stated occupation for every hour, उन्मर्दनमज्जनलेपनालङ्काररक्षातिलकाशनादिभिः with massaging, bathing, anointing, decorating, protecting, applying tilaka, feeding and other such activities, संलालितः lovingly attended to, स्वाचरितैः with his own pastimes, प्रहर्षयन् delighted.

Simple meaning: O King! Then every evening, Madhava was lovingly attended to with massaging, bathing, anointing, decorating, applying tilaka, feeding and all such other activities, and delighted everyone with his own pastimes.

गावस्ततो गोष्ठमुपेत्य सत्वरं हुङ्कारघोषैः परिहूतसङ्गतान् ।

स्वकान् स्वकान् वत्सतरानपाययन् मुहुर्लिहन्त्यः स्रवदौधसं पयः ॥10.13.24॥

Word-by-word meaning: गावः the cows, ततः then, गोष्ठम् to the cowshed, उपेत्य having come, सत्वरम् quickly, हुङ्कार-घोषैः with the sound of their mooing, परिहूत-सङ्गतान् having called and gathered, स्वकान् स्वकान् their own respective, वत्सतरान् calves, अपाययन् gave to drink, मुहुः repeatedly, लिहन्त्यः licking, स्रवत्-औधसम् flowing from their udders, पयः milk

Anvaya: ततः गावः सत्वरं गोष्ठम् उपेत्य हुङ्कारघोषैः सङ्गतान् परिहूय स्वकान् स्वकान् वत्सतरान् मुहुः लिहन्त्यः स्रवदौधसं पयः अपाययन्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: ततः गावः Then, the cows, सत्वरं quickly, गोष्ठम् उपेत्य having come to the cowshed, हुङ्कारघोषैः with the sound of their mooing, सङ्गतान् those gathered, परिहूय having called, स्वकान् स्वकान् वत्सतरान् their own respective calves, मुहुः लिहन्त्यः repeatedly licking, स्रवदौधसं पयः with milk flowing from their udders, अपाययन् gave to drink.

Simple meaning: Then the cows, having quickly come to the cowshed, called out to their respective calves with the sound of their mooing, and repeatedly licking them, nursed each of their own calves with milk flowing freely from their udders.

गोगोपीनां मातृतास्मिन् सर्वा स्नेहर्द्धिकां विना ।

पुरोवदास्वपि हरेस्तोकता मायया विना ॥10.12.25॥

Word-by-word meaning: गो-गोपीनाम् of the cows and the gopis, मातृता motherly affection, अस्मिन् in Him, सर्वा all, स्नेह-ऋद्धिकाम् increase of love, विना without, पुरः-वत् as before, आसु अपि even among them, हरेः of Hari, तोकता the state of being a child, मायया by maya, विना without

आसु इदम्,  मकारान्तः, स्त्रीलिङ्गः, सम्बोधनाभावः, सर्वनामशब्दः, सप्तमीबहुवचनम् src. Shabdapath, Ashtadhyayi

Anvaya: गोगोपीनाम् अस्मिन् सर्वा मातृता स्नेहर्द्धिकां विना पुरोवत्, आसु अपि हरेः तोकता मायया विना।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: गोगोपीनाम् Of the cows and the gopis, अस्मिन् in Him, सर्वा all, मातृता motherly affection, स्नेहर्द्धिकां increase of love, विना without, पुरोवत् as before, आसु अपि even among them, हरेः of Hari, तोकता the state of being a child, मायया by maya, विना without.

Simple meaning: The motherly affection of all the cows and gopis towards him grew beyond what it had been before — and this increase of love was not due to any maya, nor was Krishna's appearing as their child due to maya. It was all natural and spontaneous.

व्रजौकसां स्वतोकेषु स्नेहवल्ल्याब्दमन्वहम् ।

शनैर्निःसीम ववृधे यथा कृष्णे त्वपूर्ववत् ॥10.13.26॥

Word-by-word meaning: व्रज-ओकसाम् of the residents of Vraja, स्व-तोकेषु towards their own children, स्नेह-वल्ली the creeper of affection, आब्दम् for a year, अन्वहम् day by day, शनैः gradually, निःसीम without limit, ववृधे grew, यथा just as, कृष्णे towards Krishna, तु indeed, अपूर्व-वत् like never before

Anvaya: व्रजौकसां स्वतोकेषु स्नेहवल्ली आब्दम् अन्वहं शनैः निःसीम अपूर्ववत् ववृधे, यथा कृष्णे तु।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: व्रजौकसां स्वतोकेषु स्नेहवल्ली The creeper of affection of the residents of Vraja towards their own children, आब्दम् for a year, अन्वहं day by day, शनैः निःसीम अपूर्ववत् ववृधे gradually, without limit grew like never before, यथा कृष्णे तु just as in Krishna, indeed.

Simple meaning: The creeper of affection of the residents of Vraja towards their own children grew day by day, without limit, over the course of that year like never before — just as their love towards Krishna himself.

इत्थमात्माऽऽत्मनाऽऽत्मानं वत्सपालमिषेण सः ।

पालयन् वत्सपो वर्षं चिक्रीडे वनगोष्ठयोः ॥10.13.27॥

Word-by-word meaning: इत्थम् in this way, आत्मा the Self of all, आत्मना by himself, आत्मानम् himself, वत्सपाल-मिषेण under the pretext of being a cowherd boy, सः he, पालयन् maintaining, वत्सपः the cowherd boy, वर्षम् for a year, चिक्रीडे played, वन-गोष्ठयोः in the forest and in the cowshed

Anvaya: इत्थम् आत्मा आत्मना आत्मानं पालयन् सः वत्सपः वत्सपालमिषेण वर्षं वनगोष्ठयोः चिक्रीडे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: इत्थम् In this way, आत्मा the Self of all, आत्मना आत्मानं by Himself, Himself, पालयन् maintaining, सः वत्सपः He, the cowherd boy, वत्सपालमिषेण under the pretext of being a cowherd boy, वर्षं for a year, वनगोष्ठयोः चिक्रीडे in the forest and in the cowshed, played.

Simple meaning: The Self of all, maintaining Himself by Himself — appearing as both the calves and their keepers — sported for a full year as a cowherd boy in the forest and in the cowshed.

एकदा चारयन् वत्सान् सरामो वनमाविशत् ।

पञ्चषासु त्रियामासु हायनापूरणीष्वजः ॥10.13.28॥

Word-by-word meaning: एकदा once, चारयन् grazing, वत्सान् the calves, स-रामः along with Balarama, वनम् the forest, आविशत् entered, पञ्चषासु in five or six, त्रि-यामासु nights, हायन-अपूरणीषु that which completes a year, अजः the unborn one (Krishna)

हायन   A year. Src. Kosha. Ashtadhyayi

त्रियाम   त्रि—याम mf(आ)n. (the night) containing 3 watches (or 9 hours), Src. Kosha. Ashtadhyayi

Anvaya: पञ्चषासु त्रियामासु हायनापूरणीषु एकदा अजः सरामः वत्सान् चारयन् वनम् आविशत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: पञ्चषासु त्रियामासु हायनापूरणीषु When five or six nights still left over to complete the year, एकदा सरामः अजः once, the Unborn one, along with Balarama, वत्सान् चारयन् while grazing the calves, वनम् आविशत् entered the forest.

Simple meaning: Once, when there were but five or six nights remaining to complete the full year, Krishna, along with Balarama, entered the forest to graze the calves.

Literal meaning of the anvaya: ततः Then, गोवर्धनाद्रिशिरसि on the summit of Govardhana hill, चरन्त्यः गावः while the cows were grazing, विदूरात् from a distance, उपव्रजं near Vraja, वत्सान् तृणं चरतः ददृशुः saw the calves grazing on the grass.

दृष्ट्वाथ तत्स्नेहवशोऽस्मृतात्मा स गोव्रजोऽत्यात्मपदुर्गमार्गः ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अथ दृष्ट्वा Then, having seen the calves, तत्स्नेहवशः overcome by affection for them, अस्मृतात्मा forgetting themselves, सः गोव्रजः that herd of cows, अत्यात्मपदुर्गमार्गः escaping their own care takers and going on a difficult path, द्विपात् with pairs of legs together (galloping), ककुद्ग्रीवः with hump and neck raised, उदास्यपुच्छः with mouth and tail raised upward, घुङ्कृतैः with mooing sounds, आस्रुपया with milk flowing, जवेन with great speed, अगात् went.

Simple meaning: Upon seeing the calves, the entire herd of cows was overcome by affection for them and forgot themselves entirely. Escaping their care takers, they rushed down at great speed along a path difficult to traverse, galloping  with their humps, necks, mouths and tails all raised upward, mooing loudly, with milk flowing freely.

Simple meaning: Coming together below, the cows nursed the calves with milk from their own udders — even those cows who already had calves of their own — licking their limbs as if trying to swallow them up in their affection.

तदीक्षणोत्प्रेमरसाप्लुताशया जातानुरागा गतमन्यवोऽर्भकान् ।

Simple meaning: Their hearts flooded with a surging wave of love upon seeing the boys, all anger about the cows escaping dissolved, and with deep affection arising within them, the cowherd men lifted the boys up with their arms, embraced them, and smelling their heads, attained the highest joy.

Anvaya: यत्र नैसर्गदुर्वैराः नृमृगादयः अजितावासद्रुतरुट्तर्षकादिकं मित्राणि इव सह आसन्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यत्र Where, नैसर्गदुर्वैराः those who are natural enemies by their very nature, नृमृगादयः humans, animals and so on, अजितावासद्रुतरुट्तर्षकादिकं by the residence of Ajita/Krishna anger, hunger and so on gone away, मित्राणि इव as if friends, सह आसन् lived together.

Simple meaning: In Vrindavana, by the very presence of Krishna — those who are natural enemies by their very nature, humans and animals and so on, with all their fierceness and hunger gone away — lived together as if they were the closest of friends.

तत्रोद्वहत् पशुपवंशशिशुत्वनाट्यं ब्रह्माद्वयं परमनन्तमगाधबोधम् ।

वत्सान् सखीनिव पुरा परितो विचिन्वदेकं सपाणिकवलं परमेष्ठ्यचष्ट ॥10.13.61॥

Word-by-word meaning: तत्र there, उद्वहत् bearing/carrying, पशुप-वंश-शिशुत्व-नाट्यम् the play/drama of being a child of the cowherd community, ब्रह्म-अद्वयम् the non-dual Brahman, परम-अनन्तम् supreme and infinite, अगाध-बोधम् of unfathomable knowledge/wisdom, वत्सान् the calves, सखीन् इव as if friends, पुरा formerly/as before, परितः all around, विचिन्वत् searching/looking for, एकम् alone/the one, स-पाणि-कवलम् with a morsel of food in hand, परमेष्ठी Brahma, अचष्ट beheld

Anvaya: तत्र परमेष्ठी पशुपवंशशिशुत्वनाट्यम् उद्वहत् परमनन्तमगाधबोधं ब्रह्माद्वयं — पुरा इव सखीन् वत्सान् परितः विचिन्वत् सपाणिकवलम् एकम् अचष्ट।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तत्र There, परमेष्ठी Brahma, पशुपवंशशिशुत्वनाट्यम् उद्वहत् bearing the play of being a child of the cowherd community, परमनन्तमगाधबोधं supreme, infinite and of unfathomable knowledge, ब्रह्माद्वयं the non-dual Brahman — पुरा इव like before, सखीन् वत्सान् friends and the calves, परितः विचिन्वत् searching all around, सपाणिकवलम् with a morsel of food in hand, एकम् alone, अचष्ट beheld.

Simple meaning: There, Brahma beheld that one Supreme Being — the non-dual Brahman, infinite, supreme and of unfathomable wisdom — playing the role of a child of the cowherd community, standing alone with a morsel of food in hand, searching all around for the calves and cowherd boys looking for His dear friends, just as before.

दृष्ट्वा त्वरेण निजधोरणतोऽवतीर्य पृथ्व्यां वपुः कनकदण्डमिवाभिपात्य ।

स्पृष्ट्वा चतुर्मुकुटकोटिभिरङ्घ्रियुग्मं नत्वा मुदश्रुसुजलैरकृताभिषेकम् ॥10.13.62॥

Word-by-word meaning: दृष्ट्वा having seen, त्वरेण swiftly/hastily, निज-धोरणतः from his own vehicle/mount (the swan), अवतीर्य having descended, पृथ्व्याम् on the ground, वपुः his body, कनक-दण्डम् इव like a golden staff/rod, अभिपात्य having fallen/prostrated, स्पृष्ट्वा having touched, चतुर्-मुकुट-कोटिभिः with the tips of his four crowns, अङ्घ्रि-युग्मम् the pair of lotus feet, नत्वा having bowed, मुद-अश्रु-सुजलैः with the pure waters of tears of joy, अकृत-अभिषेकम् performed an abhisheka/anointing

Anvaya: दृष्ट्वा त्वरेण निजधोरणतः अवतीर्य पृथ्व्यां वपुः कनकदण्डम् इव अभिपात्य चतुर्मुकुटकोटिभिः अङ्घ्रियुग्मं स्पृष्ट्वा नत्वा मुदाश्रुसुजलैः अकृताभिषेकम्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: दृष्ट्वा Having seen, त्वरेण swiftly, निजधोरणतः अवतीर्य having descended from his own mount, पृथ्व्यां on the ground, कनकदण्डम् इव वपुः अभिपात्य prostrating his body like a golden staff, चतुर्मुकुटकोटिभिः अङ्घ्रियुग्मं स्पृष्ट्वा having touched the pair of lotus feet with the tips of his four crowns, नत्वा having bowed, मुदाश्रुसुजलैः अकृताभिषेकम् performed an abhisheka with the pure waters of his tears of joy.

Simple meaning: Upon seeing the Lord, Brahma swiftly descended from his swan, fell upon the ground prostrating his body like a golden staff, touched the Lord's lotus feet with the tips of his four crowns, bowed with deep reverence, the pure waters of his tears of joy falling on Krishna’s feet.

उत्थायोत्थाय कृष्णस्य चिरस्य पादयोः पतन् ।

आस्ते महित्वं प्राग्दृष्टं स्मृत्वा स्मृत्वा पुनः पुनः ॥10.13.63॥

Word-by-word meaning: उत्थाय उत्थाय rising up again and again, कृष्णस्य of Krishna, चिरस्य after a long time, पादयोः at the feet, पतन् falling, आस्ते remained/stayed, महित्वम् the greatness/glory, प्राक् दृष्टम् previously seen/witnessed, स्मृत्वा स्मृत्वा remembering again and again, पुनः पुनः repeatedly/over and over

Anvaya: उत्थाय उत्थाय चिरस्य कृष्णस्य पादयोः पतन् प्राग्दृष्टं महित्वं पुनः पुनः स्मृत्वा स्मृत्वा आस्ते।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: उत्थाय उत्थाय Rising up again and again, चिरस्य for a long time, कृष्णस्य पादयोः पतन् falling at the feet of Krishna, प्राग्दृष्टं महित्वं  the greatness and glory previously witnessed, पुनः पुनः स्मृत्वा स्मृत्वा remembering again and again repeatedly, आस्ते he remained.

Simple meaning: Rising up again and again only to fall once more at the feet of Krishna, Brahma remained there — remembering over and over again, repeatedly, the great glory and majesty he had just witnessed — overwhelmed and unable to do anything else.

शनैरथोत्थाय विमृज्य लोचने मुकुन्दमुद्वीक्ष्य विनम्रकन्धरः ।

कृताञ्जलिः प्रश्रयवान् समाहितः सवेपथुर्गद्गदयैलतेलया ॥10.13.64॥

Word-by-word meaning: शनैः slowly, अथ then, उत्थाय rising, विमृज्य wiping, लोचने the eyes, मुकुन्दम् Mukunda, उद्वीक्ष्य seeing, विनम्र-कन्धरः with bent neck, कृत-अञ्जलिः with folded hands, प्रश्रयवान् humble, समाहितः composed, स-वेपथुः trembling, गद्गदया choked, ऐलत extolled, इलया by speech

ऐलत् इल् , कर्तरि लङ्लकारः (परस्मैपदम्) , प्रथमपुरुषः , एकवचनम् src. Dhatupath. Ashtadhyayi

Anvaya: अथ शनैः उत्थाय लोचने विमृज्य मुकुन्दम् उद्वीक्ष्य विनम्रकन्धरः कृताञ्जलिः प्रश्रयवान् समाहितः सवेपथुः गद्गदया इलया ऐलत।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अथ Then, शनैः उत्थाय rising slowly, लोचने विमृज्य wiping the eyes, मुकुन्दम् उद्वीक्ष्य seeing Mukunda, विनम्रकन्धरः with bent neck, कृताञ्जलिः with folded hands, प्रश्रयवान् humble, समाहितः composed, सवेपथुः trembling, गद्गदया इलया with choked voice, ऐलत extolled.

Simple meaning: Then, slowly rising and wiping his eyes, he looked at Mukunda. With head bowed, hands folded, humble and composed, yet trembling, he began to speak in a choked voice.

श्रीमद्भागवते महापुराणे पारमहंस्यां संहितायां दशमस्कन्धे पूर्वार्धे

ब्रह्म विमोहन लीला नाम त्रयोदशः अध्यायः ॥13॥

Thus ends the thirteenth discourse named “Brahma is seized with and ultimately rid of infatuation”  in the first half of Book Ten of the great and glorious Bhagavata-Purana.
